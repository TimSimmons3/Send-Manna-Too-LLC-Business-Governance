## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the controlled operating method for managing a Send Manna Too LLC external deliverable from release-request initiation through external issuance, client review or acceptance coordination, post-issuance control, and external-deliverable record closure.

The procedure operationalizes the External Deliverable Governance Policy. It does not create authority, change policy obligations, provide legal advice, establish commercial terms, authorize a person to disclose information, prescribe technical delivery methods, or replace a more specific approved requirement.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies proportionately to work products prepared, approved, issued, presented, transmitted, or distributed by or on behalf of Send Manna Too LLC to an external recipient.

It applies to external deliverables that may include:

- Reports.
- Assessments.
- Findings.
- Recommendations.
- Risk analyses.
- Governance documents.
- Executive summaries.
- Presentations.
- Questionnaires.
- Evidence packages.
- Plans.
- Roadmaps.
- Formal correspondence.
- Proposals.
- Statements of work.
- Other work products intended for external reliance, action, decision-making, or record.

It applies regardless of file format, delivery channel, document title, or whether the deliverable is provided electronically, physically, through an approved platform, or through a formal presentation.

This procedure covers:

- External-release request initiation.
- Deliverable identification and status confirmation.
- Purpose, audience, recipient, intended-use, reliance, and redistribution review.
- Deliverable ownership and release-authority identification.
- Pre-release governance coordination.
- Release holds and unresolved-condition routing.
- Final-status determination.
- External-release decisions.
- Version, source, review, approval, publication, and issuance traceability.
- Publication and artifact-validation handoff.
- Controlled issuance and delivery evidence.
- Client receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance coordination.
- Material changes after approval.
- Post-issuance correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, reliance limitation, and recipient notification.
- External-deliverable record closure.
- External-deliverable records and evidence.

This procedure does not create:

- Pricing, contracting, signature, or commercial authority.
- Legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional conclusions.
- Information-classification technical controls.
- Disclosure authority that does not otherwise exist.
- Third-party qualification or contracting procedures.
- Fixed records-retention periods.
- Client-specific forms, checklists, trackers, registers, workflows, or ticketing requirements.
- Production Word, PDF, or PowerPoint artifacts.

## 3. Governing Authority and Precedence

The Constitution is the highest internal governance authority.

The Governance and Operating Authority Policy controls decision rights, approval expectations, operating authority, client-facing authority, and material exception authority.

The External Deliverable Governance Policy controls external-release authority, deliverable status, final-status determination, recipients, distribution, reliance, issuance, correction, withdrawal, replacement, notification, post-issuance control, and external-deliverable evidence.

The following approved documents control within their defined scopes:

- Client Engagement Governance Policy and Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure for engagement authorization, scope, delivery governance, deliverable coordination, client acceptance, and engagement closeout.
- Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy for proposals, pricing, contracts, obligations, signature authority, warranties, guarantees, commercial commitments, and commercial evidence.
- Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy for information use, access, sharing, disclosure, drafts, client-provided evidence, confidentiality, need-to-know, minimization, and redaction.
- Third-Party and Partner Governance Policy for partner qualification, authorization, role clarity, client-facing involvement, conflicts, conduct, information sharing, and partner evidence.
- Records Evidence and Retention Policy for record status, evidence sufficiency, integrity, provenance, retention, preservation, protection, recoverability, review, and authorized disposition.
- Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure for requested departures, completed departures, escalated issues, corrective actions, review, expiration, closure, reopening, recurrence, and root-cause review.
- Publication and Export Procedure and Publication Artifact Validation Checklist for controlled publication outputs and format-specific artifact validation.
- Document Quality Standard for evidence quality, source reliability, facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, residual risk, readability, review, legal and commercial caution, publication readiness, and acceptance criteria.

Signed agreements, applicable law, client obligations, and authorized professional requirements may impose additional requirements.

When authority, obligation, or precedence is unclear, the affected release or continued reliance shall pause when continued action could materially increase exposure, and the issue shall be documented and escalated.

## 4. Operating Principles

The procedure shall be applied using the following principles:

- Treat external release as a controlled decision, not a file-transfer event.
- Distinguish authority to create, publish, and release.
- Distinguish deliverable status from operating-process state.
- Confirm purpose, audience, recipient, intended use, and permitted reliance before release.
- Apply controls proportionately to sensitivity, reliance, complexity, obligation, and risk.
- Coordinate required reviews without duplicating the controlling subject-matter procedures.
- Do not release while a material requirement remains unresolved unless the issue is appropriately disclosed, limited, escalated, or covered by an approved exception.
- Do not infer approval from formatting, branding, export, upload, possession, silence, or informal review.
- Limit distribution to authorized recipients and approved purposes.
- Preserve source, version, review, approval, recipient, issuance, and delivery traceability when material.
- Distinguish receipt, review, acknowledgment, and acceptance.
- Reassess material changes after approval.
- Act promptly when an issued deliverable may be inaccurate, unauthorized, obsolete, misdirected, or improperly relied upon.
- Preserve issuance history during correction, withdrawal, supersession, or replacement.
- Verify closure rather than assuming completion.
- Link related engagement, commercial, confidentiality, partner, publication, records, and cross-policy evidence without creating duplicate authoritative records.
- Use the more specific approved policy or procedure for its defined subject matter.

The procedure may be applied without a dedicated form, checklist, register, tracker, software platform, or ticketing system when the required facts, decisions, authority, and evidence remain identifiable.

## 5. Deliverable Statuses and Operating-Process States

### 5.1 Deliverable Statuses

The following deliverable statuses are controlled by the External Deliverable Governance Policy:

| Deliverable Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Internal work product | Material intended only for internal preparation, analysis, or coordination. |
| Working draft | Incomplete or unapproved material subject to change. |
| Review draft | Material provided for defined review, comment, or validation but not approved as final. |
| Client review copy | Material provided to a client for review, clarification, or feedback without final-status representation. |
| Final deliverable | Material approved for the stated external purpose and intended recipient. |
| Superseded deliverable | A previously issued deliverable replaced by a later approved version. |
| Withdrawn deliverable | A deliverable whose use or reliance has been revoked. |
| Corrected deliverable | A deliverable reissued to correct an error while preserving its general purpose. |
| Replacement deliverable | A deliverable issued in place of a prior version when material content, scope, status, or intended reliance has changed. |

The current controlling status shall be clear when confusion could create risk.

A deliverable may change status only when the applicable conditions, decision, authority, and evidence support the change.

### 5.2 Operating-Process States

An external-deliverable release process may use the following states:

| Process State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Release not requested | The work product exists, but no controlled external-release request has been initiated. |
| Release requested | A request to provide the work product to an external recipient has been identified. |
| Under pre-release review | Purpose, status, scope, evidence, authority, recipient, restrictions, and required reviews are being assessed. |
| On release hold | Release is paused because a material condition is unresolved or continued action could increase exposure. |
| Pending release decision | Required review is complete enough for the authorized decision-maker to approve, reject, defer, or limit release. |
| Approved for release | Appropriate authority has approved the identified version for the stated purpose, recipient, and release conditions. |
| Not approved for release | Release was rejected, withdrawn from consideration, or deferred without current approval. |
| Issued | The approved version was delivered, presented, transmitted, or otherwise made available through the approved method. |
| Pending receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance | A defined external response or evidence remains outstanding. |
| Post-issuance action open | A material issue, status change, correction, withdrawal, replacement, limitation, or notification action remains open. |
| Ready for record closure | Required release, response, post-issuance, evidence, and open-item conditions are complete or appropriately dispositioned. |
| Closed | External-deliverable closure criteria are satisfied and closure is approved and documented. |

A process state may be adapted proportionately, but its meaning shall remain clear.

Status or state alone does not prove approval, authority, issuance, acceptance, or closure.

Changes in deliverable status or process state shall preserve the prior status or state, date, reason, authority, and supporting evidence when material.

### 5.3 Control Gates

The procedure uses the following control gates:

1. Release-request gate.
2. Deliverable identification and context gate.
3. Pre-release governance coordination gate.
4. Unresolved-condition disposition gate.
5. Final-status gate.
6. External-release decision gate.
7. Controlled-issuance and delivery-evidence gate.
8. External-deliverable record-closure gate.

A gate may be combined with another gate for a low-complexity deliverable when all applicable requirements remain satisfied and the decision record remains clear.

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Deliverable Owner

The deliverable owner coordinates the external-deliverable lifecycle, including purpose, status, scope alignment, required reviews, release readiness, evidence, issuance support, post-issuance action, and closure.

Ownership does not grant external-release authority.

### 6.2 Release-Request Initiator

The release-request initiator identifies the proposed external release and provides the known purpose, recipient, timing, intended use, and relevant context.

The initiator shall not represent the deliverable as approved merely by requesting release.

### 6.3 Engagement Owner

When the deliverable relates to a client engagement, the engagement owner provides the approved engagement context, including scope, expected deliverable, client use, timing, review or acceptance dependencies, and relationship to engagement closeout.

The engagement owner does not receive external-release authority solely from engagement ownership.

### 6.4 Source or Evidence Owner

The source or evidence owner confirms the identity, reliability, currency, limitations, and authorized use of material sources or supporting evidence within existing responsibility.

### 6.5 Quality Reviewer

The quality reviewer evaluates accuracy, completeness, clarity, evidence support, facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, residual risk, scope alignment, and applicable acceptance criteria.

### 6.6 Confidentiality or Information Reviewer

The confidentiality or information reviewer evaluates need-to-know, information sensitivity, minimization, redaction, disclosure authorization, markings, client-provided evidence, and recipient appropriateness within existing authority.

### 6.7 Commercial or Professional-Review Coordinator

When required, the commercial or professional-review coordinator obtains or confirms appropriate commercial, contractual, legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or other qualified review.

This role does not create professional authority or substitute for qualified advice.

### 6.8 Third-Party or Partner Coordinator

When a third party or partner affects the deliverable, the coordinator confirms applicable qualification, authorization, role clarity, information-sharing limits, client-facing involvement, deliverable ownership, and evidence.

### 6.9 Publication or Artifact-Validation Reviewer

The publication or artifact-validation reviewer confirms that an applicable Word, PDF, PowerPoint, or other publication output has completed the approved publication and format-validation process.

Publication validation does not grant external-release authority.

### 6.10 Decision or Approving Authority

The decision or approving authority approves, rejects, defers, limits, conditions, withdraws, or reauthorizes external release within existing business, governance, engagement, commercial, or other applicable authority.

This procedure does not create that authority.

### 6.11 Distribution or Delivery Owner

The distribution or delivery owner coordinates the approved delivery method, authorized recipients, restrictions, timing, transmission, and delivery evidence.

### 6.12 Client Communication Owner

The client communication owner coordinates material communications concerning release status, review purpose, reliance limitations, receipt, acceptance, correction, withdrawal, replacement, or required recipient action.

### 6.13 Acceptance or Closeout Reviewer

The acceptance or closeout reviewer confirms that applicable receipt, review, acknowledgment, acceptance, post-issuance, evidence, open-item, and closure conditions are satisfied.

Separation from the deliverable owner or delivery owner should be used when proportionate to risk.

### 6.14 Records Owner

The records owner, when distinct, confirms authoritative-record status, evidence context, confidentiality, preservation, recoverability, retention treatment, duplication control, and disposition restrictions.

### 6.15 Escalation Recipient

The escalation recipient evaluates material uncertainty or risk and determines the required authority, expertise, release hold, containment, limitation, or next controlled action.

One person may perform multiple roles for a low-complexity deliverable when authority remains appropriate and the record remains clear.

## 7. External-Release Request Initiation

A controlled external-release request shall be initiated before a work product is treated as approved for external release.

A request may arise from:

- An expected engagement deliverable.
- A client request.
- A prospective-client request.
- A proposal or contracting activity.
- A partner, vendor, auditor, insurer, attorney, regulator, board, executive, or other external-party request.
- A planned presentation or briefing.
- A required evidence package.
- A correction, replacement, or reissuance need.
- Another approved business purpose.

The release request shall identify, as applicable:

- Deliverable title or description.
- Current location or source.
- Current known deliverable status.
- Requesting person or source.
- Intended external recipient or recipient group.
- Business purpose.
- Intended use or decision supported.
- Expected timing.
- Related client, prospect, engagement, contract, partner, or obligation.
- Deliverable owner.
- Known release authority.
- Known confidentiality, commercial, partner, publication, records, or professional-review considerations.
- Known acceptance or acknowledgment expectation.
- Open questions.

An informal request, urgency, prior relationship, prior delivery, completed file, client possession, upload, or request from a senior person does not establish external-release approval.

When a work product is transmitted externally before a controlled request is completed, the matter shall be assessed for possible noncompliance, containment, notification, or corrective action.

## 8. Deliverable Identification and Status Confirmation

Before pre-release review proceeds, the deliverable owner shall confirm the specific work product and version under consideration.

The identification shall address, as applicable:

- Deliverable title.
- Source document or business record.
- Source path, system, Git commit, release tag, document version, or revision identifier.
- Publication-output identifier when applicable.
- Current deliverable status.
- Current process state.
- Related engagement, proposal, agreement, statement of work, decision, or obligation.
- Known prior versions.
- Known prior issuance.
- Known correction, withdrawal, supersession, or replacement relationship.
- Known source or evidence limitations.

The deliverable owner shall determine whether the material is:

- Internal work product.
- A working draft.
- A review draft.
- A client review copy.
- A candidate final deliverable.
- A previously issued deliverable requiring post-issuance action.

A client review copy shall be distinguishable from a final deliverable when misunderstanding could create risk.

Draft, review, superseded, withdrawn, or uncontrolled material shall not be represented as a current final deliverable.

When the controlling version or status cannot be determined, the deliverable shall remain on release hold until clarified or appropriately limited.

## 9. Purpose, Audience, Recipient, Intended Use, Reliance, and Redistribution Review

Before external release, the deliverable owner shall confirm that the proposed release context is sufficiently understood.

The review shall address:

- Legitimate business purpose.
- Intended audience.
- Named recipient, recipient role, or authorized recipient group.
- Recipient business need.
- Intended use, action, decision, communication, obligation, or outcome.
- Expected level of recipient knowledge.
- Whether the material is for review, comment, acknowledgment, acceptance, decision, action, or final reliance.
- Whether reliance should be limited by purpose, time, scope, system, client, engagement, or decision.
- Whether redistribution, publication, quotation, modification, or reuse should be restricted.
- Whether third-party reliance should be prohibited or limited.
- Whether independent professional review is required.
- Whether the deliverable could be misunderstood as legal advice, certification, guarantee, warranty, audit opinion, or binding commitment.
- Whether the delivery channel and destination are appropriate.

The recipient shall not be treated as authorized solely because the recipient requested the material, participated in the engagement, previously received similar material, is associated with the client, or appears on an informal communication thread.

When the intended use, recipient authority, or permitted reliance is unclear, the deliverable shall remain on release hold until clarified or appropriately limited.

Restrictions shall be clear, proportionate, consistent with applicable obligations, and not used to conceal a known error or contradict an approved commitment.

## 10. Ownership and Release-Authority Identification

Each material external deliverable shall have an identifiable deliverable owner or accountable business function.

The deliverable owner shall identify the person or role expected to make the external-release decision.

The authority review shall distinguish:

- Authority to prepare or revise content.
- Authority to perform analysis.
- Authority to review content.
- Authority to recommend approval.
- Authority to create a publication output.
- Authority to approve a client-facing commitment.
- Authority to disclose information.
- Authority to approve external release.
- Authority to approve distribution or reliance restrictions.
- Authority to correct, withdraw, supersede, replace, or limit reliance after issuance.

External release requires a person with appropriate business, governance, engagement, or commercial authority. Contractual obligations may require or constrain release, but they do not by themselves assign internal decision authority.

Ownership, authorship, subject-matter expertise, file control, publication responsibility, client communication responsibility, prior approval of similar material, or seniority does not by itself establish external-release authority.

When authority is unclear, conflicting, unavailable, or outside the current role's decision rights, the deliverable shall remain on release hold and the issue shall be escalated.

## 11. Pre-Release Governance Coordination Gate

Before a deliverable is approved for external release, the deliverable owner shall coordinate a proportionate review of applicable governance requirements.

The review shall address, as applicable:

- Engagement authorization and client-obligation alignment.
- Approved scope and expected-deliverable alignment.
- Source reliability, evidence sufficiency, and evidence currency.
- Material facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, limitations, dependencies, and residual risks.
- Accuracy, completeness, readability, quality, and acceptance criteria.
- Confidentiality, need-to-know, minimization, redaction, markings, disclosure authorization, and client-provided evidence.
- Commercial language, pricing, obligations, commitments, warranties, guarantees, signature implications, and acceptance terms.
- Legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, compliance, or other qualified professional-review needs.
- Third-party or partner qualification, authorization, role, information sharing, client-facing involvement, and deliverable ownership.
- Publication readiness and applicable artifact validation.
- Source, version, review, approval, and release traceability.
- Recipient, distribution, delivery, redistribution, quotation, modification, reuse, and reliance restrictions.
- Required receipt, review, acknowledgment, acceptance, or closeout evidence.
- Record status, authoritative source, preservation, protection, recoverability, and retention considerations.
- Known exceptions, noncompliance, escalations, or corrective actions.

The review may rely on approved evidence produced through another controlled process. It shall not recreate or duplicate an authoritative review merely to satisfy this procedure.

The review shall identify:

- Applicable review domains.
- Responsible reviewer or authority for each material domain.
- Review outcome.
- Open conditions.
- Required limitations or controls.
- Evidence location or reference.
- Whether the release may proceed, remain on hold, or move to decision.

A review domain may be marked not applicable when the rationale is clear and supportable.

## 12. Release Holds and Unresolved-Condition Routing

A deliverable shall remain on release hold when a material condition is unresolved and release or continued action could increase exposure.

Release-hold conditions may include:

- Unclear scope or engagement authorization.
- Unclear or conflicting release authority.
- Unclear recipient authority or need-to-know.
- Unclear intended use or permitted reliance.
- Unsupported, incomplete, unreliable, obsolete, or conflicting material information.
- Material factual error or unsupported conclusion.
- Omitted material assumption, limitation, dependency, or residual risk.
- Confidentiality, redaction, disclosure, or information-sharing concern.
- Commercial, contractual, legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional-review concern.
- Unclear third-party or partner role, authority, contribution, or ownership.
- Incomplete quality review.
- Incomplete publication or artifact validation.
- Unclear version, source, approval, or issuance traceability.
- Unresolved distribution, redistribution, quotation, modification, reuse, or third-party reliance restriction.
- Open exception, noncompliance, escalation, or corrective action that affects release.
- Request to bypass a controlled requirement.
- Request to issue an incomplete or unapproved deliverable.
- Request to suppress a material limitation or residual risk.

The release-hold record shall identify:

- Deliverable and affected version.
- Current status and process state.
- Hold reason.
- Requirement or obligation affected.
- Known facts, assumptions, and unresolved questions.
- Immediate containment or interim control.
- Responsible owner.
- Decision, review, or evidence needed.
- Timing or urgency.
- Conditions for release from hold.

A hold may be resolved through completion of review, clarification, correction, limitation, disclosure, re-scoping, reauthorization, escalation, approved exception, rejection, or withdrawal from consideration.

An unresolved condition shall route to the Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure when it involves a requested departure, completed departure, material escalation, corrective action, recurring issue, or authority conflict.

The deliverable shall not advance to final-status or release approval merely because the deadline, client expectation, delivery schedule, or business urgency remains unchanged.

## 13. Final-Status Determination

A deliverable shall be treated as final only when it is approved for its stated purpose, recipient, and release context.

Before assigning final status, the deliverable owner shall confirm, as applicable:

- The specific version is identifiable.
- Approved scope and obligation alignment exists.
- Required review is complete.
- Material issues are resolved, disclosed, limited, escalated, or covered by an approved exception.
- Appropriate release authority is identified.
- Content is sufficiently complete and supportable for the stated purpose.
- Facts, assumptions, limitations, dependencies, recommendations, and residual risks are appropriately presented.
- Confidentiality and distribution controls are appropriate.
- Applicable publication validation is complete.
- Source, version, review, approval, recipient, and release traceability are sufficient.
- Required reliance and redistribution restrictions are defined.
- Required acceptance or acknowledgment expectations are understood.

Final status shall not be inferred solely from:

- A filename containing "final."
- Conversion to PDF.
- Branding.
- A signature image.
- Publication formatting.
- Upload to a delivery platform.
- Placement in a client folder.
- Transmission to a client.
- Informal verbal approval.
- Client silence.
- Prior use of a similar deliverable.

A client review copy may be issued without final status when the review purpose, non-final status, reliance limits, recipient, and communication are clear and appropriately authorized.

Final-status determination does not replace the separate external-release decision.

## 14. External-Release Decision

After required review and final-status determination, the authorized decision-maker shall approve, reject, defer, limit, or condition external release.

The release decision shall identify, as applicable:

- Deliverable title and approved version.
- Deliverable status.
- Approved purpose.
- Authorized recipient or distribution group.
- Approved delivery method.
- Intended use.
- Reliance, redistribution, quotation, modification, reuse, or third-party restrictions.
- Applicable confidentiality marking or communication.
- Required receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance.
- Conditions or limitations.
- Decision authority.
- Decision date.
- Decision rationale.
- Related review and evidence.
- Required re-review condition when applicable.

Approval shall apply only to the identified version, purpose, recipient, and release context.

Approval for one recipient, engagement, purpose, date, or delivery method shall not automatically authorize another.

The release decision may be combined with another approved business decision when the external-release elements remain explicit and traceable.

A rejected, deferred, or limited decision shall identify the reason and any conditions for reconsideration when practical.

The release decision shall not be assumed from draft creation, review participation, formatting, export, storage, upload, client possession, informal discussion, meeting attendance, or lack of objection.

## 15. Version, Source, Review, Approval, Publication, and Issuance Traceability

Material external deliverables shall remain traceable to their approved source, version, review, approval, publication output, recipient, and issuance evidence where practical and proportionate.

Traceability may include:

- Source document or business record.
- Source path or system.
- Git commit or release tag.
- Document version or revision identifier.
- Evidence sources.
- Review records.
- Approval record.
- Publication-output identifier.
- Artifact-validation evidence.
- Issue date.
- Authorized recipient or distribution list.
- Delivery method.
- Delivery destination.
- Delivery or presentation evidence.
- Receipt, review, acknowledgment, acceptance, or closeout evidence.
- Correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, or notification relationship.

The authoritative source shall be identifiable.

Traceability may be maintained through one authoritative record or a linked set of approved records.

The procedure does not require duplicate storage of evidence already maintained in an approved authoritative source.

Uncontrolled duplicate copies, obsolete versions, and convenience records shall be avoided when they could create status, access, reliance, or disposition confusion.

## 16. Publication and Artifact-Validation Handoff

When a controlled publication format is required, the deliverable shall complete the applicable Publication and Export Procedure and Publication Artifact Validation Checklist requirements.

The publication handoff shall identify, as applicable:

- Approved source.
- Required publication format.
- Publication-output owner.
- Output path or identifier.
- Export method.
- Source comparison.
- Formatting validation.
- Branding.
- Confidentiality marking.
- Page or slide numbering.
- Client-data review.
- Placeholder review.
- Output location.
- Artifact-validation evidence.

Corrections should be made in the approved source or controlled artifact source and regenerated or re-exported when technically practical.

Publication preparation and validation shall not:

- Replace the authoritative source.
- Create new governance decisions.
- Introduce uncontrolled content changes.
- Establish final status by itself.
- Establish external-release authority by itself.
- Authorize a recipient.
- Authorize distribution or reliance.

A deliverable may remain on release hold when publication or artifact validation is incomplete or when the publication output does not preserve the approved meaning.

## 17. Controlled Issuance and Delivery Evidence

Only the version approved for the stated external purpose and recipient shall be issued.

Before issuance, the distribution or delivery owner shall confirm:

- The approved version and status.
- The approved recipient or distribution group.
- The approved purpose and intended use.
- The approved delivery method and destination.
- Applicable confidentiality, reliance, redistribution, quotation, modification, reuse, or third-party restrictions.
- Required accompanying communication.
- Required receipt, acknowledgment, acceptance, or follow-up.
- Any timing, expiration, or sequencing condition.
- That no known material change has invalidated the approval.

Issuance may occur through:

- Approved email.
- Approved client or business platform.
- Approved file-transfer method.
- Approved physical delivery.
- Formal presentation.
- Another reasonable and approved method.

Issuance evidence shall identify, as applicable:

- Deliverable and version.
- Issue date and time when material.
- Sender, presenter, or delivery owner.
- Recipient or destination.
- Delivery method.
- Restrictions or communication provided.
- Transmission, presentation, upload, or delivery confirmation.
- Known delivery failure or exception.
- Required next action.

For a formal presentation, issuance evidence may include the approved presentation version, date, audience, presenter, restrictions communicated, and meeting or delivery record.

A sent message, completed upload, successful presentation, or client possession does not cure or authorize an otherwise unauthorized release.

A delivery failure, misdirection, incorrect recipient, wrong version, or unauthorized disclosure shall be contained and routed for assessment.

## 18. Client Receipt, Review, Acknowledgment, or Acceptance Coordination

Receipt, review, acknowledgment, and acceptance are distinct and shall not be treated as interchangeable without support.

The external-deliverable record shall identify, as applicable:

- The expected client or recipient response.
- The responsible recipient or authority when known.
- The response method.
- Required evidence.
- Timing or response dependency.
- Open issues or conditions.
- Effect on continued work, obligation completion, payment, reliance, or engagement closeout.

Receipt evidence may include:

- Delivery confirmation.
- Platform access or download evidence.
- Written receipt confirmation.
- Meeting or presentation attendance record.
- Other appropriate business evidence.

Acknowledgment or acceptance evidence may include:

- Written approval.
- Contractual acceptance record.
- Approved signoff.
- Acknowledgment of completion.
- Meeting record with clear authority and outcome.
- Other appropriate business evidence.

Client or recipient possession, silence, meeting attendance, informal discussion, or lack of objection shall not be represented as acknowledgment or acceptance unless the applicable agreement, context, and authority support that conclusion.

When formal acceptance is not required, the record shall preserve sufficient delivery and completion evidence and the rationale for the applicable closeout method.

Disputed, incomplete, late, or unclear receipt, acknowledgment, or acceptance shall be documented and escalated when it may materially affect obligations, payment, reliance, delivery, continued work, or closeout.

The Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure controls engagement-level acceptance and closeout. This procedure provides external-deliverable evidence and status to that process.

## 19. Material Changes After Approval

A proposed change to an approved deliverable shall be assessed before issuance or continued reliance.

The assessment shall consider changes to:

- Scope.
- Findings.
- Conclusions.
- Recommendations.
- Evidence.
- Facts.
- Assumptions.
- Limitations.
- Dependencies.
- Residual risk.
- Commercial statements.
- Commitments.
- Confidential information.
- Recipient.
- Intended purpose.
- Intended use.
- Distribution or reliance restrictions.
- Version status.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Publication output.
- Applicable obligations.

A change is material when it could affect accuracy, completeness, authority, scope alignment, recipient understanding, intended reliance, confidentiality, commercial or contractual meaning, quality, acceptance, or continued use.

A materially changed deliverable shall not retain prior approval merely because its filename, format, branding, or general structure remains unchanged.

Material changes shall:

- Be made in the appropriate controlled source.
- Preserve relationship to the prior approved version.
- Receive proportionate re-review.
- Receive proportionate reauthorization.
- Receive updated publication or artifact validation when applicable.
- Receive updated recipient, distribution, reliance, and acceptance review when applicable.
- Preserve change and decision evidence.

Minor corrections that do not change meaning, obligation, recipient, status, reliance, or risk may be handled within existing authority when the rationale and traceability remain clear.

When materiality is unclear, the deliverable shall remain on release hold pending review.

## 20. Post-Issuance Issue and Status-Change Handling

A post-issuance issue shall be assessed when an issued deliverable may be:

- Materially inaccurate.
- Incomplete for its stated purpose.
- Unsupported.
- Obsolete.
- Misdirected.
- Sent to an unauthorized recipient.
- Based on the wrong version.
- In conflict with approved scope or obligation.
- Missing a material limitation.
- Misleading in status.
- Improperly relied upon.
- Affected by a material change in evidence or circumstances.
- Affected by an unauthorized disclosure.
- Affected by an unapproved commitment.
- Challenged by a client, reviewer, authority, or other responsible party.

The immediate response shall consider:

- Stopping or limiting further distribution.
- Limiting or stopping reliance.
- Preserving the issued version and delivery evidence.
- Identifying affected recipients.
- Containing unauthorized access or disclosure.
- Clarifying current status.
- Placing related work or decisions on hold.
- Escalating the matter.
- Obtaining qualified review.
- Initiating correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, or notification.
- Preserving evidence and suspending disposition when required.

The post-issuance record shall identify:

- Deliverable and issued version.
- Issue identified.
- Date and source of identification.
- Known recipients.
- Known or potential reliance.
- Facts, assumptions, and unresolved questions.
- Immediate containment or limitation.
- Matter owner.
- Required authority and review.
- Required communication.
- Required corrective action.
- Residual risk.
- Closure or reopening criteria.

A post-issuance issue involving a departure, material uncertainty, noncompliance, corrective action, or recurrence shall route to the Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure.

The external-deliverable process shall remain in Post-issuance action open until required domain actions are complete or appropriately transferred and dispositioned.

## 21. Correction, Withdrawal, Supersession, Replacement, and Reliance Limitation

The authorized decision-maker shall select a response proportionate to the issue, risk, obligation, recipient impact, and continued-reliance potential.

Available responses may include:

- Correction.
- Withdrawal.
- Supersession.
- Replacement.
- Reliance limitation.
- Clarification.
- Controlled reissuance.
- No change, with documented rationale.

### 21.1 Correction

A correction may be used when an error can be addressed without changing the deliverable's general approved purpose or intended reliance.

The correction shall identify the corrected version, affected content, reason, authority, date, recipients, and relationship to the prior version.

### 21.2 Withdrawal

Withdrawal may be used when continued use or reliance is no longer authorized or supportable.

The withdrawal decision shall identify the affected deliverable, effective date, authority, recipients, required cessation or limitation of reliance, and any replacement or follow-up action.

### 21.3 Supersession

Supersession may be used when a later approved version becomes the controlling deliverable for the relevant purpose.

The relationship between the superseded and controlling versions shall be traceable.

### 21.4 Replacement

Replacement may be used when material content, scope, status, recipient, intended reliance, or purpose has changed enough that a new approved deliverable is required.

The replacement shall receive the applicable pre-release review, final-status, release-decision, publication, issuance, and evidence controls.

### 21.5 Reliance Limitation

Reliance may be limited when continued use is appropriate only within defined conditions, scope, recipients, time, purpose, or decision context.

A limitation shall be clear, authorized, communicated when material, and not used to conceal a known error or avoid an applicable obligation.

The selected response shall preserve:

- The issued version.
- What changed or why status changed.
- Decision authority.
- Decision date.
- Affected recipients.
- Required notification.
- Corrected, replacement, or controlling version.
- Required recipient action.
- Evidence of execution.
- Residual risk.

A superseded, withdrawn, corrected, or replaced deliverable shall not be silently altered in a manner that destroys material issuance history.

## 22. Recipient Notification

Affected recipients shall be notified when a correction, withdrawal, replacement, supersession, reliance limitation, wrong version, misdirection, unauthorized disclosure, or other material status change could affect their use or reliance.

Notification shall be timely and proportionate to:

- Severity of the issue.
- Likelihood of continued reliance.
- Potential client, legal, commercial, confidentiality, operational, security, or reputational impact.
- Number and type of recipients.
- Applicable contractual or professional obligations.
- Availability of a corrected or replacement deliverable.
- Urgency of required recipient action.

The notification shall identify, as appropriate:

- Affected deliverable.
- Affected version.
- Issue or status change.
- Whether reliance should stop or be limited.
- Required recipient action.
- Corrected, replacement, or controlling version when available.
- Applicable timing.
- Contact or escalation path.

The notification shall:

- Use an authorized and reasonable communication method.
- Be approved by appropriate authority when material.
- Avoid unnecessary confidential information.
- Avoid unsupported admissions, promises, legal conclusions, or commercial commitments.
- Coordinate with engagement, commercial, confidentiality, partner, legal, or professional authority when applicable.
- Preserve evidence of recipients, timing, content, delivery, response, and unresolved failures when material.

Failure to reach an affected recipient shall be documented and escalated when continued reliance or exposure may remain material.

Recipient notification does not by itself close the related cross-policy matter, engagement, or external-deliverable record.

## 23. Cross-Policy Matter Routing

This procedure shall provide external-deliverable context but shall not duplicate the complete cross-policy lifecycle.

The following matters shall route to the approved Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure when applicable:

- Requested departure from a controlled requirement.
- External release completed without required authorization.
- Distribution to an incorrect or unauthorized recipient.
- Unauthorized disclosure.
- Use of an unapproved or wrong version.
- Material uncertainty requiring escalation.
- Material factual or evidence defect.
- Unsupported commitment, warranty, guarantee, certification, or promise.
- Request to suppress a material limitation or residual risk.
- Request to bypass quality, confidentiality, commercial, partner, records, or publication controls.
- Corrective action.
- Repeated issue or recurring condition.
- Review, extension, expiration, closure, or reopening of an exception or corrective action.
- Root-cause review.
- Material noncompliance.
- Conflicting authority or precedence.

The handoff shall identify:

- Deliverable and affected version.
- Applicable requirement.
- Deliverable status and process state.
- Known facts, assumptions, and unresolved questions.
- Immediate hold, containment, reliance limitation, or notification action.
- Deliverable owner.
- Affected client, engagement, contract, partner, information, record, recipient, or commitment.
- Decision or review needed.
- Relevant evidence.
- Timing or urgency.

Affected release, disclosure, distribution, continued reliance, record alteration, or disposition shall pause when required by policy or when continued action could materially increase exposure.

The cross-policy matter record and external-deliverable record may be linked rather than duplicated.

Completion or closure of one record does not automatically close the other.

## 24. External-Deliverable Record Closure

An external-deliverable record may move to Ready for record closure when the approved release activity is complete or otherwise dispositioned.

Closure criteria shall confirm, as applicable:

- The release request was approved, rejected, deferred, withdrawn, or otherwise dispositioned.
- The controlling deliverable and version are identifiable.
- Required pre-release reviews are complete.
- Final status was determined appropriately.
- The release decision and authority are documented.
- Approved issuance is complete or non-issuance is documented.
- Authorized recipients and delivery method are identifiable.
- Delivery evidence is sufficient.
- Required receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance is obtained or appropriately dispositioned.
- Material restrictions and reliance conditions were communicated.
- Material post-approval changes were reviewed and reauthorized.
- Required correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, limitation, or recipient notification is complete.
- Related cross-policy matters are closed or appropriately linked, transferred, and dispositioned.
- Related engagement, commercial, confidentiality, partner, publication, and records evidence is identifiable.
- Material open issues are resolved, deferred, transferred, or escalated.
- Required preservation needs and disposition restrictions are understood.
- Residual risk is documented and accepted by appropriate authority when necessary.
- No required follow-up remains unassigned.
- The closeout reviewer approves closure.
- Closure date and rationale are recorded.

Closure shall not be used to conceal:

- Unauthorized release.
- Incorrect recipient.
- Unresolved reliance risk.
- Missing evidence.
- Disputed acceptance.
- Incomplete notification.
- Open correction or withdrawal.
- Unmanaged exception.
- Incomplete corrective action.
- Unassigned follow-up.
- Unresolved confidentiality, commercial, legal, partner, records, or professional-review matter.

External-deliverable record closure does not:

- Close the related client engagement.
- Establish client acceptance.
- Close a related cross-policy matter.
- Authorize records disposition.
- Eliminate an obligation, claim, dispute, confidentiality requirement, preservation need, or corrective action.
- Reopen or amend scope.

A closed external-deliverable record shall be reopened when closure evidence is invalidated, a required action was not sustained, a material issue recurs, a recipient identifies a material defect, or new facts materially change the prior conclusion.

## 25. Records and Evidence

External-deliverable records shall be sufficient for a reasonable reviewer to understand:

- What was proposed for external release.
- Why release was requested.
- What deliverable and version were involved.
- What status and process state applied.
- Who owned the deliverable.
- What purpose, audience, recipient, intended use, and reliance conditions applied.
- What scope and obligations controlled.
- What evidence and sources supported the deliverable.
- What reviews were required and completed.
- What authority existed.
- What decision was made and why.
- What restrictions or limitations applied.
- What publication and artifact-validation evidence existed.
- What was issued, when, how, and to whom.
- What receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance occurred.
- What material changes occurred after approval.
- What post-issuance issues and actions occurred.
- What correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, reliance limitation, or notification occurred.
- What related cross-policy matters occurred.
- Why and when the external-deliverable record was closed or reopened.

Records may include, as applicable:

- Release request.
- Deliverable identification and status evidence.
- Approved scope or deliverable requirement.
- Source and evidence references.
- Review and approval records.
- Confidentiality, commercial, partner, publication, records, or professional-review evidence.
- Release-hold and unresolved-condition evidence.
- Final-status determination.
- External-release decision.
- Version or revision records.
- Publication validation records.
- Distribution or delivery evidence.
- Recipient list or delivery destination.
- Client receipt, acknowledgment, review, or acceptance evidence.
- Material-change and reauthorization evidence.
- Exception, escalation, noncompliance, and corrective-action records.
- Correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, limitation, or notification evidence.
- Closeout or reopening evidence.

No fixed identifier syntax, folder structure, software platform, register, checklist, or retention period is required by this procedure.

The authoritative source shall be identifiable.

A linked set of approved records may be used when one record would duplicate existing authoritative evidence.

Retention, preservation, confidentiality, access, recoverability, review, and disposition remain governed by the Records Evidence and Retention Policy and applicable obligations.

A closed status does not authorize disposition.

## 27. Review and Maintenance

This procedure shall be reviewed at least annually or when external-deliverable practices, services, client obligations, delivery methods, publication practices, partner use, information handling, records requirements, governing policies, or operating methods materially change.

Actual operating use should be reviewed for:

- Release requests that bypass controlled initiation.
- Confusion between deliverable status and process state.
- Confusion among authority to create, publish, and release.
- Omitted purpose, recipient, intended-use, reliance, or redistribution review.
- Repeated release holds.
- Incomplete or inconsistent pre-release review.
- Unclear release authority.
- Distribution to incorrect or unauthorized recipients.
- Weak issuance or delivery evidence.
- Client silence treated as acceptance.
- Material changes retaining stale approval.
- Delayed correction, withdrawal, replacement, or notification.
- Incomplete post-issuance action.
- Premature closure.
- Duplicate or conflicting engagement and deliverable records.
- Repeated need for an execution aid.

Changes shall follow the controlled governance release process.

A reusable form, checklist, tracker, register, workflow, ticketing requirement, or training artifact shall not be created merely for convenience. New operating evidence and separately approved scope are required.
