## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines one consistent operating method for identifying, assessing, escalating, deciding, documenting, tracking, reviewing, extending, closing, and analyzing exceptions, noncompliance, and corrective actions under the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

The procedure operationalizes existing policy requirements. It does not create authority, change policy obligations, provide legal advice, establish technical incident-response requirements, or replace more specific approved requirements.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies when activity governed by an approved Send Manna Too LLC policy involves:

- A request to depart from a controlled requirement.
- A departure that has already occurred.
- A material uncertainty or issue requiring escalation.
- A corrective action needed to remediate or prevent recurrence.
- A review, extension, expiration, closure, or reopening decision.
- A recurring condition that may indicate a governance weakness.

This procedure applies proportionately to governance, client engagement, commercial and contracting, information handling and confidentiality, third-party and partner, records and evidence, and external-deliverable matters.

This procedure does not create technical incident response, cybersecurity, privacy, employment discipline, contract remedies, client-specific workflows, fixed legal or financial thresholds, retention schedules, automated tooling, or production publication artifacts.

## 3. Governing Authority and Precedence

The Constitution is the highest internal governance authority.

The Governance and Operating Authority Policy controls decision rights, exception authority, approval expectations, and controlled operating discipline.

The more specific approved policy controls for its defined subject matter. Signed agreements, applicable law, client obligations, and authorized professional requirements may impose additional requirements.

This procedure cannot:

- Authorize an exception to applicable law or an obligation that cannot lawfully or contractually be excepted.
- Create approval authority that a person or role does not otherwise possess.
- Weaken confidentiality, preservation, evidence, client, or contractual obligations.
- Override a more specific approved policy.
- Convert an unresolved issue into an approved exception merely by recording it.

Unresolved authority or precedence questions shall be documented and escalated.

## 4. Operating Principles

The procedure shall be applied using the following principles:

- Act before exposure increases when practical.
- Distinguish facts, assumptions, unresolved questions, and recommendations.
- Apply controls proportionately to impact, urgency, complexity, and recurrence.
- Use conservative escalation when authority, obligation, or materiality is unclear.
- Keep exceptions limited in scope and duration.
- Do not use exceptions to normalize avoidable noncompliance.
- Assign clear ownership.
- Preserve sufficient evidence.
- Verify closure rather than assuming completion.
- Review recurring conditions for root cause and governance improvement.
- Avoid duplicate records and uncontrolled copies.

## 5. Matter Types and Statuses

### 5.1 Matter Types

A matter shall be classified as one or more of the following:

- Exception request: A proposed departure submitted before reliance or implementation.
- Approved exception: A departure authorized by appropriate authority within defined limits.
- Noncompliance: A controlled requirement was not followed, regardless of whether the departure was intentional.
- Escalated issue: A matter requiring review beyond the current operating authority because of impact, uncertainty, conflict, or required expertise.
- Corrective action: A defined action to remediate a condition, reduce exposure, restore compliance, or prevent recurrence.
- Recurring issue: A repeated or related pattern that may indicate a systemic weakness.
- Reopened matter: A previously closed matter that no longer satisfies closure conditions or has materially recurred.

Classification may change as facts develop. Reclassification shall preserve the prior record and rationale.

### 5.2 Statuses

The record shall use an appropriate status, such as:

- Identified.
- Under assessment.
- Escalated.
- Pending decision.
- Approved.
- Rejected.
- Action in progress.
- Pending verification.
- Closed.
- Expired.
- Reopened.
- Superseded.

Status alone does not prove approval or closure.

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Matter Owner

The matter owner coordinates assessment, documentation, communication, review dates, and evidence.

### 6.2 Corrective-Action Owner

The corrective-action owner is accountable for completing assigned actions and providing completion evidence.

### 6.3 Decision or Approving Authority

The decision or approving authority approves, rejects, limits, extends, or closes a matter within existing authority.

Material policy exceptions require governance authority unless a more specific approved requirement assigns another authorized decision right.

### 6.4 Escalation Recipient

The escalation recipient evaluates the matter, confirms required expertise and authority, and determines the next controlled action.

### 6.5 Closure Reviewer

The closure reviewer confirms that closure criteria and evidence are sufficient. Separation from the action owner should be used when proportionate to risk.

One person may perform multiple roles for low-complexity matters when authority remains appropriate and the record remains clear.

## 7. Intake and Initial Record

A matter shall be recorded when the issue is material, requires approval, requires tracking, affects an external party, may recur, or needs evidence for accountability.

The initial record shall include, as applicable:

- Unique identifier.
- Date identified or requested.
- Matter type.
- Current status.
- Requirement and controlled document affected.
- Client, engagement, contract, partner, record, deliverable, process, or activity affected.
- Matter owner.
- Source of identification.
- Known facts.
- Assumptions and unresolved questions.
- Immediate containment or interim action.
- Related evidence and records.
- Confidentiality or access limitations.

No fixed identifier syntax is required unless an approved system or template establishes one.

## 8. Assessment and Materiality

The matter owner shall perform a proportionate assessment before a decision, continued reliance, external release, record disposition, or other affected action when practical.

The assessment shall consider:

- Actual and potential impact.
- Scope and number of affected matters or parties.
- Client trust or external-party impact.
- Legal, contractual, regulatory, tax, insurance, or professional-review implications.
- Confidentiality, information handling, or unauthorized-disclosure impact.
- Commercial, revenue, collection, or financial exposure.
- Scope, delivery, acceptance, or operational impact.
- Third-party, partner, subcontractor, or vendor involvement.
- Evidence integrity, provenance, preservation, retention, recoverability, or disposition impact.
- External-deliverable accuracy, authority, distribution, or reliance.
- Duration and urgency.
- Ability to contain, reverse, correct, or recover.
- Recurrence or pattern.
- Authority uncertainty.
- Need for qualified professional review.

The assessment may classify a matter as routine, elevated, or material for operational handling. These classifications do not replace legal, contractual, client, or domain-specific determinations.

A matter shall be escalated when materiality remains uncertain and continued activity could increase exposure.

## 9. Exception Request and Decision

An exception request should be submitted before the departure is implemented or relied upon when practical.

The exception record shall include:

- Unique identifier.
- Date requested.
- Requirement being excepted.
- Reason and business need.
- Affected scope.
- Known facts and assumptions.
- Risk created or accepted.
- Materiality assessment.
- Compensating controls, limitations, or follow-up actions.
- Matter owner.
- Decision or approving authority.
- Decision and rationale.
- Effective date.
- Review date, expiration date, expected duration, or closure condition.
- Distribution, reliance, or access limitations when applicable.
- Related corrective actions.
- Related evidence.
- Status.

The approving authority may approve, approve with conditions, reject, defer, or require additional review.

An exception shall be limited to the approved scope and period. Approval does not authorize broader or future departures.

An exception shall not be approved when it would bypass the Constitution, applicable law, an unalterable contractual or client obligation, required preservation, confidentiality requirements, required professional review, or authority limits.

A departure that occurred without prior approval shall be treated as noncompliance unless authorized governance determines that another classification is appropriate. Later approval shall not erase the history of the prior departure.

## 10. Escalation

Escalation is required when an issue exceeds current operating authority, may materially affect obligations or trust, requires qualified review, or cannot be resolved safely at the current level.

Escalation triggers include:

- Unclear or conflicting authority.
- Conflicting policy, contract, client, partner, or legal requirements.
- Potential legal, contractual, regulatory, insurance, tax, or professional exposure.
- Potential confidentiality breach, misdirected information, or unauthorized disclosure.
- Unsupported commitment, guarantee, certification, warranty, or promise.
- Material scope, delivery, pricing, capacity, collection, or acceptance concern.
- Potential conflict of interest or independence concern.
- Partner, subcontractor, vendor, or external-collaborator uncertainty.
- Missing, altered, lost, unrecoverable, or prematurely disposable evidence.
- Potentially inaccurate, obsolete, misdirected, unauthorized, or improperly relied-upon external deliverable.
- Request to conceal risk or suppress a material limitation.
- Request to bypass approved governance.
- Repeated noncompliance or repeated exception extensions.
- Unresolved high-impact corrective action.
- Uncertain materiality where continued activity could increase exposure.

The escalation record shall identify the issue, reason for escalation, affected obligations, immediate controls, responsible owner, evidence, decision needed, and required timing.

Affected commitments, disclosures, releases, partner involvement, record alteration or disposition, or other high-risk activity shall pause when required by the governing policy or when continued action could materially increase exposure.

## 11. Corrective Action

Corrective action shall be proportionate to the cause, impact, urgency, recurrence risk, and affected obligation.

A corrective-action record shall include:

- Action description.
- Condition or cause addressed.
- Action owner.
- Target completion date.
- Priority or urgency.
- Dependencies.
- Interim or compensating control.
- Required approvals.
- Progress status.
- Completion evidence.
- Validation method.
- Residual risk.
- Closure reviewer.
- Closure decision and date.

Corrective actions may include correction, containment, withdrawal, replacement, recipient notification, restored evidence, process change, document update, governance decision, approved exception, client communication, commercial review, confidentiality review, professional review, training recommendation, template improvement, or tooling recommendation when authorized and appropriate.

Completion of an activity does not by itself establish closure. The result shall be verified against the defined closure criteria.

## 12. Review, Extension, Expiration, and Closure

### 12.1 Periodic Review

Open exceptions and corrective actions shall be reviewed according to their risk, duration, obligation, and review date.

The review shall confirm:

- Current need.
- Current scope.
- Current risk and materiality.
- Effectiveness of compensating controls.
- Progress of corrective actions.
- Changes in obligations or circumstances.
- Evidence sufficiency.
- Continued appropriateness of approval and authority.
- Need for extension, escalation, closure, or reopening.

### 12.2 Extension

An extension shall be approved before expiration when practical.

The extension record shall include:

- Reason for extension.
- Updated risk assessment.
- Corrective-action status.
- Continued or revised compensating controls.
- New review or expiration date.
- Approving authority.
- Decision rationale.

Repeated extensions shall trigger escalation and recurrence review.

### 12.3 Expiration

An expired exception shall not remain an authorization for continued departure.

At expiration, the matter shall be closed, extended through approved authority, superseded, or treated as noncompliance if the departure continues without authorization.

### 12.4 Closure

Closure criteria shall be defined when the matter is opened or when corrective action is approved.

Closure requires, as applicable:

- The departure ended or the approved condition was satisfied.
- Required corrective actions were completed.
- Completion evidence was reviewed.
- External correction, withdrawal, replacement, or notification was completed when required.
- Records and evidence are preserved.
- Residual risk is accepted by appropriate authority when necessary.
- No required follow-up remains unassigned.
- The closure reviewer approved closure.
- Closure date and rationale are recorded.

### 12.5 Reopening

A matter shall be reopened when closure evidence is invalidated, the condition recurs materially, a required action was not sustained, or new facts change the prior decision.

## 13. Domain-Specific Response Preservation

This procedure preserves, rather than replaces, domain-specific responses.

Examples include:

- Client engagement: resolve, defer, document, communicate, or separate follow-up work.
- Commercial and contracting: clarify, revise, exclude, escalate, obtain professional review, or withhold commitment.
- Confidentiality: stop or limit disclosure, reliance, reuse, or distribution; obtain authorization or review.
- Third party and partner: limit, suspend, or discontinue involvement.
- Records and evidence: preserve, suspend disposition, restore recoverability, correct status, or authorize disposition.
- External deliverables: correct, withdraw, supersede, replace, limit reliance, and notify affected recipients when material.

The applicable policy controls the required domain response.

## 14. Records and Evidence

Matter records shall be sufficient for a reasonable reviewer to understand:

- What occurred or was requested.
- When it occurred.
- Which requirement and scope were affected.
- Who owned, reviewed, approved, acted, verified, and closed the matter.
- What facts, assumptions, risks, and evidence supported the decision.
- What compensating controls and corrective actions applied.
- What review, extension, expiration, closure, or reopening occurred.
- What external communication or deliverable action occurred.
- What residual risk remains.

Records shall remain traceable to related policies, agreements, engagements, clients, partners, deliverables, decisions, releases, and evidence when material.

The Records Evidence and Retention Policy controls retention, preservation, protection, recoverability, review, and authorized disposition.

The Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy controls access, use, sharing, disclosure, and protection.

This procedure does not establish retention periods or storage architecture.

## 15. Recurrence and Root-Cause Review

A recurrence or root-cause review shall be considered when:

- The same requirement is repeatedly excepted.
- Similar noncompliance occurs more than once.
- Corrective actions repeatedly miss target dates.
- Exceptions are repeatedly extended.
- Similar issues occur across clients, engagements, contracts, partners, records, or deliverables.
- Corrections, withdrawals, replacements, distribution errors, or reliance disputes recur.
- Compensating controls repeatedly fail.
- Closure decisions are repeatedly reopened.
- The pattern suggests unclear guidance, unrealistic requirements, inadequate review, insufficient capability, weak templates, missing training, process failure, or ineffective tooling.

The review shall distinguish immediate cause, contributing factors, and systemic cause when practical.

Recommendations may include a controlled document change, decision record, procedure improvement, template or checklist, training, operating-model change, or tooling proposal.

Recommendations do not take effect until approved through the applicable governance process.

## 16. Minimum Cross-Policy Traceability

Each material matter shall identify the applicable policy or policies.

Where multiple policies apply, the record shall identify:

- The primary governing policy.
- Supporting policies.
- The controlling authority.
- Any conflict or precedence decision.
- Domain-specific actions required.
- The evidence required for closure.

The implementation record for Release 1.7 shall map this procedure to all seven core policies before approval.

## 18. Prohibited Uses

This procedure shall not be used to:

- Create authority where none exists.
- Conceal noncompliance.
- Retroactively erase an unauthorized departure.
- Avoid legal, contractual, client, confidentiality, preservation, or professional obligations.
- Normalize recurring avoidable exceptions.
- Misrepresent a draft, expired exception, incomplete action, or unsupported closure as approved.
- Destroy or alter required evidence.
- Bypass required client communication, professional review, or governance review.
- Create uncontrolled commitments.
- Replace a required domain-specific action with a generic record.

## 19. Review and Maintenance

This procedure shall be reviewed at least annually or when:

- A governing policy materially changes.
- Recurring matters indicate an operational weakness.
- Authority, status, field, or workflow ambiguity is identified.
- A legal, contractual, client, confidentiality, records, partner, commercial, delivery, or publication obligation materially changes.
- A template, checklist, training, or tool is proposed to support execution.

Approved changes shall follow the controlled governance lifecycle and remain traceable in Git.
