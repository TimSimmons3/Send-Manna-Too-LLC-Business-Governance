## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the controlled operating method for managing a Send Manna Too LLC client engagement from prospective intake through qualification, acceptance, authorization, delivery, client review, acceptance, and closeout.

The procedure operationalizes the Client Engagement Governance Policy. It does not create authority, change policy obligations, provide legal advice, establish commercial terms, authorize external release, prescribe technical delivery methods, or replace a more specific approved requirement.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to prospective and active client engagements conducted by Send Manna Too LLC under the Business Governance Framework.

It applies proportionately to:

- Prospective engagement intake.
- Qualification and fit review.
- Acceptance, rejection, or deferral.
- Engagement authorization.
- Scope baselining.
- Assumption and dependency management.
- Engagement initiation.
- Material client communications.
- Delivery governance.
- Deliverable coordination.
- Material engagement changes.
- Client review, acceptance, and acknowledgment.
- Engagement closeout.
- Follow-up-work separation.
- Suspension, termination, or withdrawal when applicable.
- Engagement records and evidence.

This procedure does not create:

- Pricing, contracting, signature, or commercial authority.
- Legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional conclusions.
- External-deliverable release authority.
- Information-classification technical controls.
- Third-party qualification procedures.
- Fixed records-retention periods.
- Client-specific forms, trackers, checklists, registers, workflows, or ticketing requirements.
- Production Word, PDF, or PowerPoint artifacts.

## 3. Governing Authority and Precedence

The Constitution is the highest internal governance authority.

The Governance and Operating Authority Policy controls decision rights, approval expectations, operating authority, client-facing authority, and material exception authority.

The Client Engagement Governance Policy controls engagement qualification, authorization, scope, communications, delivery governance, changes, escalation, acceptance, closeout, and engagement records.

The following approved documents control within their defined scopes:

- Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy for proposals, pricing, contracts, obligations, signature authority, and commercial commitments.
- Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy for information use, access, sharing, disclosure, drafts, client evidence, and confidentiality.
- Third-Party and Partner Governance Policy for partner qualification, authorization, role clarity, involvement, conflicts, conduct, and partner risk.
- Records Evidence and Retention Policy for record status, evidence sufficiency, retention, preservation, protection, recoverability, review, and disposition.
- External Deliverable Governance Policy for external-release authority, final status, recipients, distribution, reliance, publication validation, issuance, correction, withdrawal, replacement, and post-issuance control.
- Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure for exceptions, completed departures, escalated issues, corrective actions, review, expiration, closure, reopening, recurrence, and root-cause review.
- Publication and Export Procedure for controlled publication outputs.

Signed agreements, applicable law, client obligations, and authorized professional requirements may impose additional requirements.

When authority, obligation, or precedence is unclear, the affected activity shall pause when continued action could materially increase exposure, and the issue shall be documented and escalated.

## 4. Operating Principles

The procedure shall be applied using the following principles:

- Qualify before accepting.
- Distinguish acceptance from authorization.
- Do not begin affected work before appropriate authorization.
- Define scope before treating work as approved.
- Identify facts, assumptions, dependencies, constraints, and unresolved questions.
- Make client-facing commitments only within approved authority.
- Apply controls proportionately to engagement risk, complexity, reliance, and obligations.
- Escalate uncertainty before exposure increases.
- Preserve confidentiality and need-to-know boundaries.
- Control third-party involvement before reliance.
- Coordinate deliverables without inferring external-release authority.
- Document material changes before implementation.
- Verify acceptance and closure rather than assuming them.
- Separate new follow-up work from closed work unless it is already authorized within the existing scope.
- Preserve sufficient evidence while avoiding duplicate authoritative records.
- Use the more specific approved policy or procedure for its defined subject matter.

## 5. Lifecycle States

An engagement may use the following states:

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Prospective | A possible engagement or request has been identified but qualification has not begun. |
| Under qualification | Fit, capability, capacity, constraints, risk, and expected work are being assessed. |
| Deferred | A decision is postponed pending information, timing, capacity, authority, or another defined condition. |
| Rejected | The engagement will not be pursued under the reviewed conditions. |
| Accepted pending authorization | The firm has decided in principle to pursue the engagement, but work is not authorized to begin. |
| Authorized | Required business and engagement authorization exists and initiation conditions are satisfied or specifically limited. |
| Active | Authorized work is underway within the approved scope. |
| On hold | Affected work or commitment is paused pending resolution, approval, dependency, or risk treatment. |
| Pending client review or acceptance | Work or a deliverable is awaiting client review, acknowledgment, acceptance, or another defined response. |
| Closing | Substantive work is complete and closeout evidence, open-item disposition, acceptance, or records actions are being completed. |
| Closed | Approved closure criteria are satisfied and closure is documented. |
| Terminated | The engagement ended before planned completion or was discontinued under appropriate authority. |
| Superseded | A later authorized engagement or record replaces the prior engagement for the relevant purpose. |

A status may be adapted proportionately, but its meaning shall remain clear.

Status alone does not prove acceptance, authorization, external release, client acceptance, completion, or closure.

State changes shall preserve the prior state, date, reason, authority, and supporting evidence when material.

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Engagement Owner

The engagement owner coordinates qualification, authorization, scope, delivery governance, client communication, change control, acceptance, closeout, and required evidence.

### 6.2 Qualification Reviewer

The qualification reviewer evaluates business fit, requested services, capability, capacity, independence, delivery risk, client expectations, and material constraints.

### 6.3 Decision or Approving Authority

The appropriate decision or approving authority accepts, rejects, defers, authorizes, limits, suspends, terminates, or closes an engagement within existing authority.

This procedure does not create that authority.

### 6.4 Delivery Owner

The delivery owner coordinates authorized work, evidence, dependencies, quality, schedule, and issue identification within the approved scope.

### 6.5 Client Communication Owner

The client communication owner coordinates material communications and confirms that commitments, status, assumptions, open items, risks, and next steps are accurate and authorized.

### 6.6 Deliverable Owner

The deliverable owner coordinates the engagement-level readiness, scope alignment, evidence, review, and handoff of an expected deliverable.

Ownership does not grant external-release authority.

### 6.7 Change Decision Authority

The change decision authority approves or rejects a material engagement change within existing business, commercial, contractual, and governance authority.

### 6.8 Escalation Recipient

The escalation recipient evaluates material uncertainty or risk and determines the required authority, expertise, pause condition, or next controlled action.

### 6.9 Acceptance or Closeout Reviewer

The acceptance or closeout reviewer confirms that applicable acceptance, completion, open-item, evidence, and closure conditions are satisfied.

Separation from the delivery owner should be used when proportionate to risk.

### 6.10 Records Owner

The records owner, when distinct, confirms authoritative-record status, evidence context, confidentiality, preservation, recoverability, and appropriate retention treatment.

One person may perform multiple roles for a low-complexity engagement when authority remains appropriate and the record remains clear.

## 7. Prospective Engagement Intake

A prospective engagement shall be recorded when the request may require qualification, commitment, scheduling, information access, third-party involvement, or material client communication.

The intake shall identify, as applicable:

- Prospective client or contracting party.
- Requesting person and apparent role.
- Source of the opportunity or request.
- Requested services or business need.
- Expected outcomes or deliverables.
- Desired timing.
- Known client responsibilities.
- Known firm responsibilities.
- Known assumptions, dependencies, and constraints.
- Known commercial or contracting status.
- Known confidentiality or information-handling implications.
- Known third-party or partner involvement.
- Initial engagement owner.
- Open questions requiring qualification.

Client identity, authority, scope, and commitment shall not be inferred solely from an informal conversation, referral, email, draft, prior relationship, or request for urgency.

## 8. Qualification Assessment

Before acceptance, the prospective engagement shall receive a proportionate qualification assessment.

The assessment shall consider:

- Business fit.
- Requested services.
- Capability and expertise.
- Resource and schedule availability.
- Independence and conflict concerns.
- Delivery feasibility and risk.
- Client expectations.
- Expected deliverables.
- Material assumptions and dependencies.
- Client and firm responsibilities.
- Legal, commercial, contractual, confidentiality, third-party, evidence, and operational constraints.
- Need for qualified legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or other professional review.
- Information needed before a reliable decision can be made.
- Conditions that would require deferral or rejection.

The qualification record shall distinguish:

- Known facts.
- Assumptions.
- Unresolved questions.
- Risks and constraints.
- Recommended disposition.
- Required follow-up information.
- Applicable authority.

An engagement shall not be accepted when the firm lacks sufficient understanding of the requested work, expected deliverables, material dependencies, material risks, or ability to perform.

## 9. Accept, Reject, or Defer Decision

The qualification outcome shall be one of the following:

### 9.1 Accept Pending Authorization

The engagement may be accepted in principle when it is an appropriate business fit and no unresolved condition prevents continued controlled planning.

Acceptance does not authorize work to begin, create a contract, establish pricing, permit information disclosure, authorize partner involvement, or authorize external release.

### 9.2 Reject

The engagement shall be rejected when the work is not an appropriate fit, cannot be performed responsibly, presents unacceptable unresolved risk, conflicts with independence, lacks appropriate authority, or cannot be aligned with applicable obligations.

The record shall identify the decision, authority, date, and concise rationale.

### 9.3 Defer

The engagement may be deferred when a decision depends on additional information, client clarification, capacity, timing, commercial review, professional review, conflict resolution, confidentiality clarification, partner review, or another defined condition.

The deferral record shall identify:

- Reason for deferral.
- Required information or condition.
- Responsible owner.
- Review timing or trigger.
- Any communication to the prospect.
- Status of any draft commitment or requested start.

A deferred engagement shall not be treated as accepted or authorized.

## 10. Engagement Authorization

An engagement shall not begin until appropriate authorization exists.

Authorization shall confirm, as applicable:

- The client or contracting party is sufficiently identified.
- The requesting and approving parties are sufficiently understood.
- The engagement has been accepted.
- The authorized scope is identifiable.
- Expected deliverables are identifiable.
- Client and firm responsibilities are identifiable.
- Material assumptions, dependencies, constraints, and exclusions are documented.
- Timing expectations are supportable.
- Required commercial or contracting review is complete.
- Required business, signature, or other approval exists.
- Confidentiality and information-sharing boundaries are understood.
- Third-party or partner involvement is approved or excluded.
- Required professional review has been completed or appropriately dispositioned.
- The engagement owner and delivery owner are identified.
- The authoritative authorization evidence is identifiable.
- Required records and evidence can be maintained appropriately.

Authorization evidence may include an approved agreement, statement of work, order, written client approval, approved engagement record, or other appropriate business evidence.

Internal planning that remains within operating authority may occur before engagement authorization, but it shall not:

- Begin client delivery.
- Create a binding commitment.
- Represent the engagement as active.
- Use or disclose client information beyond authorized purposes.
- Incur or represent unapproved commercial obligations.
- Involve an unauthorized third party.
- Release a deliverable externally.

When authorization is incomplete, the engagement shall remain accepted pending authorization, deferred, or on hold.

## 11. Scope Baseline

Before active work begins, the engagement shall maintain a scope baseline sufficient for controlled execution.

The scope baseline shall identify, as applicable:

- Engagement objectives.
- In-scope services and activities.
- Out-of-scope services and activities.
- Expected deliverables.
- Client responsibilities.
- Firm responsibilities.
- Material assumptions.
- Material dependencies.
- Target dates or timing expectations.
- Known constraints.
- Exclusions.
- Acceptance or acknowledgment expectations.
- Relevant commercial or contractual reference.
- Confidentiality and information-sharing boundaries.
- Approved third-party or partner involvement.
- Material evidence expectations.
- Known residual risk.

A scope baseline may be contained in one authoritative record or traceable set of approved records.

Material uncertainty shall be resolved or explicitly controlled before affected work is treated as authorized, final, or client-ready.

## 12. Assumption and Dependency Control

Material assumptions and dependencies shall be identifiable throughout the engagement.

For each material assumption or dependency, the engagement record should identify:

- Description.
- Source.
- Owner.
- Required date or review trigger.
- Current status.
- Impact if inaccurate, incomplete, delayed, or unavailable.
- Required communication.
- Related decision, scope item, deliverable, or risk.
- Evidence of resolution or disposition.

When an assumption changes or a dependency fails, the engagement owner shall assess the effect on:

- Scope.
- Deliverables.
- Schedule.
- Resources.
- Client and firm responsibilities.
- Commercial or contractual obligations.
- Confidentiality or information access.
- Third-party involvement.
- Quality and evidence sufficiency.
- Acceptance and closeout.

Affected work shall be placed on hold when continued activity could materially increase exposure or produce unreliable work.

The issue shall route to material change control, cross-policy escalation, or both when applicable.

## 13. Engagement Initiation

An authorized engagement may move to Active only when the initiation gate is satisfied.

The initiation gate shall confirm, as applicable:

- Authorization evidence is available.
- The current scope baseline is identifiable.
- Owners and decision paths are clear.
- Client communication channels and responsible contacts are clear.
- Material assumptions, dependencies, and open items are understood.
- Required access and information are authorized and available or appropriately staged.
- Approved partner involvement and boundaries are clear.
- Deliverable expectations and ownership are clear.
- Material risks and pause conditions are understood.
- Records and evidence locations or methods are appropriate.

A kickoff meeting is not required unless appropriate to the engagement, but the initiation evidence shall be sufficient to show why active work was authorized.

## 14. Material Client Communications

Material client communications shall be timely, accurate, professional, and within approved authority.

Communications shall distinguish, as applicable:

- Facts.
- Assumptions.
- Recommendations.
- Open items.
- Decisions needed.
- Risks or constraints.
- Scope status.
- Dependency status.
- Change status.
- Deliverable status.
- Acceptance status.
- Next steps and responsible parties.

Material communications shall not:

- Expand scope informally.
- Create unsupported promises.
- Commit pricing, timing, staffing, outcomes, or obligations beyond authority.
- Treat a draft as final.
- Represent client silence as approval.
- Disclose information without authorization.
- Misstate partner authority or involvement.
- Imply external-release approval.

Material communications shall be retained as engagement evidence when needed to support accountability, client clarity, change control, acceptance, or closeout.

## 15. Delivery Governance

Active work shall remain aligned with the approved scope, authority, assumptions, dependencies, obligations, and evidence expectations.

The engagement owner and delivery owner shall monitor, as applicable:

- Scope alignment.
- Deliverable progress.
- Material assumptions and dependencies.
- Client and firm responsibilities.
- Schedule and resource constraints.
- Quality and evidence sufficiency.
- Confidentiality and information handling.
- Partner involvement.
- Commercial or contractual implications.
- Material risks and decisions.
- Required client communications.
- Change requests.
- Acceptance and closeout dependencies.

A delivery method, project-management method, technical method, or software platform is not mandated by this procedure.

## 16. Deliverable Coordination and External-Release Handoff

The engagement record may coordinate:

- Expected deliverable.
- Deliverable owner.
- Purpose and intended client use.
- Scope alignment.
- Required source and evidence.
- Material assumptions, limitations, and dependencies.
- Required engagement-level review.
- Client review or acceptance dependency.
- Expected timing.
- Relationship to engagement completion and closeout.

The External Deliverable Governance Policy controls:

- External-release authority.
- Final-status determination.
- Authorized recipients.
- Distribution and reliance restrictions.
- Publication validation.
- Version and issuance traceability.
- Delivery evidence.
- Correction, withdrawal, supersession, or replacement.
- Recipient notification.
- Post-issuance reliance control.

A deliverable shall remain on release hold when scope, authority, recipient, confidentiality, reliance, evidence, publication, or approval is materially unresolved.

Completion, formatting, branding, conversion to PDF, upload, client possession, or informal review does not establish release approval.

## 17. Material Engagement Change Control

A proposed change shall be compared with the current approved scope baseline before implementation.

The review shall consider impact to:

- Objectives.
- In-scope and out-of-scope work.
- Deliverables.
- Schedule.
- Resources.
- Assumptions.
- Dependencies.
- Client responsibilities.
- Firm responsibilities.
- Pricing, payment, or commercial basis.
- Contractual obligations.
- Confidentiality or information sharing.
- Third-party or partner involvement.
- Quality and evidence.
- Acceptance and closeout.
- Business, client, legal, commercial, or reputational risk.

A change is material when it could alter an approved commitment, obligation, deliverable, schedule, resource need, risk, recipient, reliance, confidentiality boundary, commercial basis, or acceptance expectation.

Material changes shall be documented and approved through appropriate authority before affected work is implemented.

The current approved scope remains controlling until the change is approved.

Minor clarifications that do not change an approved commitment or obligation may be handled within existing authority, but the rationale and communication shall remain clear when later ambiguity could create risk.

A requested departure, completed departure, exception, noncompliance, or corrective action shall route to the Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure.

## 18. Cross-Policy Matter Routing

The client engagement procedure shall provide engagement context but shall not duplicate the complete cross-policy lifecycle.

The following matters shall route to the approved cross-policy procedure when applicable:

- Requested departure from a controlled requirement.
- Work begun without required authorization.
- Informal scope expansion already performed.
- Unauthorized commitment or disclosure.
- Material uncertainty requiring escalation.
- Corrective action.
- Repeated issue or recurring condition.
- Review, extension, expiration, closure, or reopening of an exception or corrective action.
- Root-cause review.
- Material noncompliance.

The handoff shall identify:

- Engagement and affected activity.
- Applicable requirement.
- Current lifecycle state.
- Known facts, assumptions, and unresolved questions.
- Immediate pause, hold, or containment action.
- Engagement owner.
- Affected client, deliverable, partner, information, record, or commitment.
- Decision or review needed.
- Relevant evidence.
- Timing or urgency.

Affected commitments, work, disclosures, partner involvement, external release, or record disposition shall pause when required by policy or when continued action could materially increase exposure.

## 19. Client Review, Acceptance, or Acknowledgment

Client review, receipt, acknowledgment, and acceptance are distinct and shall not be treated as interchangeable without support.

The engagement record shall identify, as applicable:

- What the client is reviewing or accepting.
- The applicable acceptance method or expectation.
- The responsible client contact or authority when known.
- Required evidence.
- Open issues or conditions.
- Timing or response dependency.
- Effect on continued work, completion, payment, or closeout.

Acceptance evidence may include:

- Written approval.
- Contractual acceptance record.
- Approved signoff.
- Acknowledgment of completion.
- Meeting record with clear authority and outcome.
- Other appropriate business evidence.

Client possession, silence, meeting attendance, informal discussion, or lack of objection shall not be represented as acceptance unless the applicable agreement, context, and authority support that conclusion.

When formal client acceptance is not required, the engagement record shall preserve sufficient completion and communication evidence and the rationale for the applicable closeout method.

Disputed, incomplete, or unclear acceptance shall be documented and escalated when it may materially affect obligations, payment, reliance, delivery, or closeout.

## 20. Engagement Closeout

An engagement may move to Closing when substantive authorized work is complete or otherwise dispositioned.

Closure criteria shall confirm, as applicable:

- Authorized work is complete, terminated, superseded, or otherwise dispositioned.
- Expected deliverables are completed or dispositioned.
- Required external-release activity is complete.
- Client review, acknowledgment, or acceptance is obtained or appropriately dispositioned.
- Material open issues are resolved, deferred, transferred, or escalated.
- Material assumptions and dependencies are closed or dispositioned.
- Approved changes are reflected in the final engagement record.
- Follow-up work is separated unless already authorized within the existing scope.
- Commercial and contractual completion conditions are addressed through appropriate governance.
- Partner involvement is concluded or dispositioned.
- Required communications are complete.
- Required records and evidence are identifiable, protected, and recoverable.
- Preservation needs and disposition restrictions are understood.
- Residual risk is documented and accepted by appropriate authority when necessary.
- No required follow-up remains unassigned.
- The closeout reviewer approves closure.
- Closure date and rationale are recorded.

Closeout shall not be used to conceal unresolved commitments, unmanaged exceptions, incomplete deliverables, disputed acceptance, missing evidence, or unauthorized work.

Closed status does not authorize disposal of engagement records. Retention, preservation, and disposition remain governed by the Records Evidence and Retention Policy.

## 21. Follow-Up-Work Separation

Requested follow-up work shall be evaluated against the approved scope baseline.

Follow-up work shall be separated into a new or amended authorized scope when it:

- Falls outside the approved scope.
- Creates a new deliverable.
- Changes timing, resource, pricing, obligation, confidentiality, partner, or acceptance expectations.
- Requires new authority.
- Would otherwise keep a completed engagement open without a defined reason.

The follow-up record shall identify:

- Relationship to the prior engagement.
- Requested work.
- Scope disposition.
- Required qualification or authorization.
- Commercial or contracting dependency.
- Responsible owner.
- Applicable records and evidence.

A closed engagement shall not be reopened solely for convenience when the requested work is a separate engagement or material change.

## 22. Suspension, Termination, or Withdrawal

An engagement or affected work may be placed On hold, terminated, or withdrawn when appropriate authority determines that continuation is not authorized, feasible, supportable, or responsible.

Potential conditions include:

- Missing or withdrawn authorization.
- Unresolved scope or authority conflict.
- Material dependency failure.
- Unacceptable delivery or quality risk.
- Confidentiality or information-handling concern.
- Commercial or contractual issue.
- Conflict of interest or independence concern.
- Partner conduct, capability, or authorization concern.
- Unresolved client responsibility.
- Request to bypass approved governance.
- Material noncompliance or repeated issue.

The record shall identify:

- Decision and authority.
- Effective date.
- Affected work, deliverables, commitments, information, partners, and records.
- Required client or other communication.
- Immediate protection or preservation actions.
- Open obligations.
- Residual risk.
- Reauthorization or closure conditions when applicable.

This procedure does not create contract remedies, termination rights, refund obligations, legal conclusions, or dispute procedures.

Termination or withdrawal does not erase existing obligations, evidence, confidentiality requirements, preservation needs, or required corrective action.

## 23. Records and Evidence

Engagement records shall be sufficient for a reasonable reviewer to understand:

- Who the client or prospect was.
- What was requested.
- How the engagement was qualified.
- What decision was made and by whom.
- What authorization existed.
- What scope controlled.
- What assumptions, dependencies, constraints, and risks applied.
- What work and deliverables were authorized.
- What material communications occurred.
- What changes were requested, approved, rejected, or deferred.
- What cross-policy matters occurred.
- What the client reviewed, acknowledged, or accepted.
- How open items and follow-up work were dispositioned.
- Why and when the engagement was closed, terminated, or superseded.

Records may include, as applicable:

- Intake and qualification evidence.
- Acceptance, rejection, or deferral decision.
- Authorization evidence.
- Scope baseline.
- Assumption and dependency records.
- Material client communications.
- Change decisions.
- Deliverable review and handoff evidence.
- Escalation, exception, noncompliance, and corrective-action records.
- Client review, acknowledgment, or acceptance evidence.
- Closeout evidence.
- Related commercial, confidentiality, partner, records, publication, or professional-review evidence.

No fixed identifier syntax, folder structure, software platform, register, or retention period is required by this procedure.

The authoritative source shall be identifiable. Uncontrolled duplicate records shall be avoided.

Retention, preservation, confidentiality, access, recoverability, review, and disposition remain governed by the Records Evidence and Retention Policy and applicable obligations.

## 25. Review and Maintenance

This procedure shall be reviewed at least annually or when client engagement practices, service offerings, governing policies, client obligations, delivery methods, partner use, information handling, records requirements, publication practices, or operating methods materially change.

Actual operating use should be reviewed for:

- Repeated qualification omissions.
- Confusion between acceptance and authorization.
- Informal scope expansion.
- Repeated dependency failures.
- Unauthorized commitments.
- External-release boundary errors.
- Incomplete acceptance or closeout evidence.
- Unclear follow-up-work separation.
- Duplicate or conflicting records.
- Repeated need for an execution aid.

Changes shall follow the controlled governance release process.

A reusable form, checklist, tracker, register, workflow, ticketing requirement, or training artifact shall not be created merely for convenience. New operating evidence and separately approved scope are required.
