## 1. Purpose

This standard defines the minimum governance and operating requirements for private commercial operations and confidential commercial records maintained by or for Send Manna Too LLC.

It establishes the public control method for:

1. Private authoritative commercial records.
2. Private tax and financial-administration records used in commercial decisions.
3. Mixed workflows that use public governance controls and private evidence.
4. Commercial artifact identification, ownership, classification, access, version, approval, integrity, backup, recovery, preservation, supersession, and closure.
5. Confidential rate-card, pricing-model, commercial-exception, invoicing, collection, scenario, capacity, KPI, and approval records.
6. Stable non-confidential evidence references.
7. Qualified-review status.
8. Stop, escalation, fallback, exception, noncompliance, and corrective-action treatment.

This standard does not create actual commercial values, authorize a live client or vendor, approve external pricing, establish legal or tax sufficiency, authorize contract execution, or prove market acceptance, profitability, operating effectiveness, scalability, or compliance.

## 2. Scope

This standard applies to private commercial operations and records that support:

1. Internal rate development.
2. Cost and effort modeling.
3. Margin and price-boundary analysis.
4. Complexity, risk, timing, travel, expense, remobilization, and contingency analysis.
5. Pricing approvals and exceptions.
6. Strategic-investment, nonprofit, and pro bono decisions.
7. Invoicing and payment administration.
8. Expense and travel administration.
9. Collection, dispute, settlement, write-off, and bad-debt administration.
10. Commercial scenarios.
11. Capacity, realization, margin, collection, concentration, staleness, and recovery KPIs.
12. Commercial approvals, reviews, changes, supersession, and closeout.
13. Qualified legal, tax, accounting, insurance, technical, or other professional input used for a commercial decision.
14. Private repository access, backup, recovery, and incident evidence.

This standard applies to public control artifacts and private evidence used together through a B-09 mixed workflow.

This standard applies before private records are created, while they are active, when they are reviewed or changed, when they are superseded, and while they remain preserved.

## 7. Core Principles

Private commercial operations shall follow these principles:

1. Public controls and private evidence shall remain separate.
2. One authoritative private source or controlled set of sources shall be identified.
3. No private record shall be populated or relied upon until G2 passes.
4. No qualified financial conclusion or applicable live use shall occur until G3 requirements are sufficiently satisfied.
5. Actual values shall not appear in public Git.
6. Information shall be minimized to the approved purpose.
7. Access shall be limited to documented need-to-know.
8. Record status shall be evidence-based.
9. Assumptions shall not be represented as facts.
10. Owner decisions shall not be represented as independent review.
11. Qualified-review status shall be explicit.
12. Material inputs, formulas, outputs, decisions, and changes shall be traceable.
13. Records shall be versioned and recoverable.
14. Superseded records shall not remain available for uncontrolled active use.
15. Preservation shall continue until authorized disposition exists.
16. A failed control shall block the affected activity.
17. No control failure authorizes use of public Git or an uncontrolled location.
18. External terms and signed agreements control over preferred internal positions when validly accepted.
19. Mock or fictional validation does not prove operating effectiveness.
20. A controlled model does not prove market acceptance or profitability.

## 13. Access and Need-to-Know

Access shall be limited to:

1. The owner.
2. Specifically authorized qualified reviewers.
3. Specifically authorized bookkeeping, banking, tax, insurance, legal, technical, or financial participants.
4. Another authorized recipient with a documented purpose and need.

Access shall not be assumed from:

1. Convenience.
2. Prior access.
3. General relationship to the firm.
4. Vendor status.
5. Partner status.
6. Informal participation.
7. Possession of a link.
8. Possession of a copy.

1. Person or role.
2. Purpose.
3. Record family.
4. Access level.
5. Approval.
6. Start date.
7. Review date.
8. End date.

## 14. Authentication and Encryption

Private authoritative sources shall use:

1. Supported authentication.

3. Device encryption appropriate to the information.

## 15. Sharing, Export, and Transfer

Before sharing or exporting a private commercial record:

1. Verify the recipient.
2. Verify authority.
3. Verify purpose.
4. Verify classification.
5. Verify need-to-know.
6. Minimize the content.
7. Confirm the applicable agreement or obligation.
8. Use an approved secure transfer method.
9. Confirm the correct version.
10. Remove comments, tracked changes, hidden content, and unnecessary metadata when applicable.
11. Record material disclosure evidence.
12. Preserve the authoritative source.
13. Confirm return, closure, or continued access requirements when applicable.

A counterparty possessing a private record does not make the record public.

A private record shall not be moved into public Git to simplify collaboration or traceability.

## 24. Recovery Requirements

1. The backup can be located.
2. The authorized person can access it.
3. The correct version can be identified.
4. The record can be restored.
5. The restored record is readable.
6. The restored record is complete.
7. The restored record preserves material formulas or relationships.
8. The restored record can be reconciled to the approved source or integrity evidence.
9. Access remains restricted.
10. Recovery evidence is preserved.
11. Recovery limitations are documented.
12. Residual risk is accepted or remediated.

A failed recovery test is a stop condition.

## 33. Preservation and No-Disposition Boundary

Private commercial records shall remain preserved under the current no-disposition boundary unless and until:

1. An approved retention period or disposition authority applies.
2. No preservation requirement exists.
3. No open obligation, engagement, contract, claim, dispute, audit, investigation, incident, exception, or review requires continued preservation.
4. Disposition is authorized and evidenced.
5. Applicable confidentiality and security controls are maintained through disposition.

This standard does not authorize disposition.

Working materials and convenience copies shall not be removed merely because they appear duplicative when preservation status is unclear.

## 48. Point-Counterpoint

| Objection | Counterpoint | Control or Fallback | Residual Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A single-member firm does not need formal private record controls | Commercial values, tax records, negotiation positions, and recovery dependencies create material risk regardless of firm size | Apply bounded controls and single-member compensating measures | Administrative burden remains |
| The pricing standard already covers these topics | It defines pricing and approval requirements but not the full private record lifecycle, access, integrity, backup, recovery, preservation, and incident method | Maintain a clear interface and no-loss mapping | Readers may still perceive overlap |
| Public Git is easier to manage | Public storage would expose commercial strategy, private evidence, and sensitive records | Use B-09 mixed workflows and stable identifiers | Private system administration may be less convenient |
| A backup copy is enough | Backup existence does not prove access, integrity, completeness, or restoration | Require representative recovery | Unseen recovery failures remain |
| Owner approval is independent review | Owner approval establishes authority, not independence or qualified sufficiency | Label owner review and obtain qualified review when triggered | Cost and timing may delay decisions |
| A controlled model proves profitability | The model may rely on assumptions and lacks actual demand, effort, collection, and cost evidence | Preserve limitations and require later evidence-based recalibration | Business decisions may still rely on uncertain inputs |
| Detailed retention periods should be added now | Retention periods require qualified and domain-specific authority and are assigned to later work | Preserve under the no-disposition boundary | Over-retention risk remains |
| Vendor compensation should be included | Vendor-channel compensation belongs to Releases 2.7 and 2.9 | Exclude and defer | Later integration may require controlled updates |
| Redaction is sufficient for public release | Metadata, context, reconstruction, and re-identification can defeat redaction | Prefer separate public controls and private evidence | Residual inference risk remains |
| Private source details should be public for auditability | Public disclosure of paths, account details, or security configuration can increase exposure | Use non-confidential evidence identifiers and sanitized validation summaries | Public reviewers have less direct reproducibility |
