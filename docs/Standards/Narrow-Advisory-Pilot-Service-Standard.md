## 1. Purpose

This standard defines the minimum approved requirements for one narrowly scoped advisory offering that may later qualify as the actual operating-use pilot authorized in principle by DEC-0017.

Approved service:

`Cybersecurity Governance Baseline and 90-Day Executive Action Roadmap`

This standard does not authorize an actual client, engagement, price, contract, disclosure, external release, or pilot start.

## 2. Governing Authority

This standard is subordinate to the Constitution, the seven approved core policies, the Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure, the External Deliverable Lifecycle Procedure, the Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure, publication controls, the Document Quality Standard, DEC-0011, DEC-0017, and DEC-0018 when approved.

No requirement in this standard creates authority beyond those sources.

## 3. Service Purpose

The service provides an executive-level baseline of selected cybersecurity governance practices and a prioritized 90-day management action roadmap.

It is intended to help a client:

1. Understand selected governance strengths, gaps, and decision needs.
2. Identify practical near-term priorities.
3. Clarify ownership, sequencing, dependencies, and management decisions.
4. Improve executive visibility without technical control operation.
5. Establish a bounded basis for separately authorized future work.

The service is advisory and decision-support oriented. It is not an audit, certification, attestation, penetration test, legal opinion, regulatory determination, or guarantee of security or compliance.

## 4. Intended Client Profile

A prospective client is suitable only when:

1. The client or contracting party is identifiable.
2. Requesting and approving authority can be understood.
3. The requested outcome matches the approved service.
4. The engagement can be owner-operated.
5. Only authorized low-sensitivity information is needed.
6. No production system access or credentials are needed.
7. The commercial arrangement is standard and low complexity or receives appropriate qualified review.
8. One primary external deliverable is sufficient.
9. The client accepts the advisory, evidence-limited, non-assurance nature of the work.
10. Work can pause safely when a stop condition occurs.

## 5. Qualification Criteria

Before acceptance, review:

1. Business fit.
2. Requested service alignment.
3. Capability and capacity.
4. Independence and conflicts.
5. Commercial and contracting complexity.
6. Information sensitivity.
7. Expected reliance.
8. Timing.
9. Client and firm responsibilities.
10. Assumptions and dependencies.
11. Legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional-review needs.
12. Third-party involvement.
13. Records and evidence feasibility.
14. Deliverable and acceptance expectations.
15. DEC-0017 eligibility and prohibited characteristics.

The outcome shall be Accept pending authorization, Defer, Reject, or Escalate.

Acceptance pending authorization does not permit work to begin.

## 6. Approved Scope

The service may include:

1. One controlled initiation discussion.
2. Up to three management interviews unless a client-specific SOW approves another bounded number.
3. Review of a limited set of authorized governance documents or summaries.
4. Review of governance roles, decision rights, risk oversight, policies, reporting, third-party oversight, incident governance, resilience governance, and improvement priorities.
5. Identification of information limitations and unanswered questions.
6. Executive-level current-state observations.
7. Prioritized governance gaps or decision needs.
8. Practical recommendations.
9. A 90-day management action roadmap.
10. One client factual-review cycle.
11. One final external deliverable.
12. Controlled engagement and deliverable closeout.

The SOW shall select only applicable scope elements.

## 7. Primary External Deliverable

The service shall produce one:

`Cybersecurity Governance Baseline and 90-Day Executive Action Roadmap`

The deliverable should include:

1. Executive summary.
2. Purpose and approved scope.
3. Assessment basis.
4. Information reviewed at a category level.
5. Material limitations.
6. Current-state observations.
7. Prioritized gaps, risks, or decision needs.
8. Recommendations.
9. 90-day action roadmap.
10. Proposed owners or responsible roles when supportable.
11. Dependencies and sequencing.
12. Intended use.
13. Reliance and redistribution limitations.
14. Residual risk.
15. Next-step considerations.

Facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, and residual risks shall be distinguished when confusion could create risk.

## 8. Delivery Method

### 8.1 Authorization and Initiation

Confirm:

1. Engagement authorization.
2. Scope baseline.
3. Commercial and contracting status.
4. Client and firm owners.
5. Information boundary.
6. Interview and evidence plan.
7. Schedule assumptions.
8. Stop and escalation conditions.
9. Records method.

### 8.2 Information Collection

Use only the minimum authorized information required for scope, such as management interviews, governance summaries, risk summaries, responsibility descriptions, and management reports.

### 8.3 Analysis and Roadmap Development

Develop evidence-supported observations, material limitations, prioritized gaps, recommendations, a sequenced 90-day roadmap, and one client review copy.

### 8.4 Review, Finalization, and Closeout

Complete factual review, supported correction, quality review, confidentiality review, final-status and release decision, controlled issuance, client response handling, and closeout.

## 9. Duration and Schedule Boundary

Planning assumption:

`Ten to fifteen business days`

The period begins only after:

1. The initiation gate is satisfied.
2. Required initial payment or commercial start condition is satisfied.
3. Required information and interview access are available.
4. No stop condition is active.

This is not a guaranteed completion date. The SOW controls the schedule and dependency treatment.

## 10. Client Responsibilities

The client shall:

1. Identify an authorized contact.
2. Provide appropriate participants.
3. Provide accurate, authorized information.
4. Avoid unnecessary regulated, privileged, highly sensitive, security-sensitive, credential, secret, or personal information.
5. Confirm permission to share materials.
6. Respond to material questions and dependencies.
7. Review the client review copy for factual accuracy.
8. Identify material errors promptly.
9. Use the deliverable only for the approved purpose.
10. Observe reliance and confidentiality restrictions.
11. Retain responsibility for decisions and implementation.

## 11. Firm Responsibilities

Send Manna Too LLC shall:

1. Perform only authorized work.
2. Maintain scope discipline.
3. Use information only for the authorized purpose.
4. Minimize information collection.
5. Distinguish evidence from assumptions and recommendations.
6. Avoid unsupported guarantees.
7. Identify limitations.
8. Escalate material uncertainty.
9. Preserve source, version, review, approval, issuance, and closeout evidence.
10. Follow publication and external-release controls.
11. Separate follow-up work requiring new scope.

## 12. Assumptions and Dependencies

Unless the SOW states otherwise:

1. The client will provide sufficient management-level information.
2. The service relies primarily on client-supplied information and authorized public information.
3. Send Manna Too LLC will not independently verify every statement or operate technical controls.
4. No production system access is required.
5. No credential, secret, raw log, exploit data, or detailed incident evidence is required.
6. The client remains responsible for legal, risk, compliance, business, and implementation decisions.
7. One review cycle and one primary deliverable are sufficient.
8. Material changes require change control.

Material dependencies include timely authorization, client information, participant availability, client decisions, intended-use clarity, qualified review, third-party exclusion, and an approved records method.

4. Penetration testing or vulnerability scanning.
5. Technical implementation.
6. Production system access.
7. Credential or secret handling.
8. Managed detection, monitoring, or response.
9. Incident response retainers.
10. Forensic analysis.
11. Security-tool administration.
12. Detailed regulated or privileged information.
13. Third-party delivery.
14. Guaranteed risk reduction.
15. Guaranteed compliance or breach prevention.
16. Unlimited meetings, revisions, or support.
17. Work outside the approved SOW.
18. Follow-up services not separately authorized.

## 14. Information Boundary

Use only authorized, need-to-know, low-sensitivity information necessary for the approved purpose.

Exclude unless separately reviewed and approved:

1. Regulated personal information.
2. Privileged legal information.
3. Highly sensitive client evidence.
4. Production credentials.
5. Secrets or keys.
6. Detailed exploit information.
7. Raw security logs.
8. Detailed incident evidence.
9. Unnecessary personal information.
10. Information the client is not authorized to disclose.

Minimize, summarize, anonymize, or redact when practical. Unclear sensitivity, authority, or recipient need-to-know requires a pause.

## 15. Third-Party Boundary

No subcontractor, referral partner, vendor, external collaborator, or other third party may participate in delivery.

A third party may not conduct interviews, review client information, draft or review the deliverable, communicate recommendations, receive engagement records, or be represented as part of the delivery team.

A proposed exception requires separately approved scope and is not authorized by DEC-0017.

## 16. Intended Use and Reliance

The deliverable is advisory and based on approved scope and available information.

It shall not be represented as:

1. Legal advice.
2. Certification.
3. Attestation.
4. Audit opinion.
5. Warranty.
6. Guarantee.
7. Proof of compliance.
8. Proof that an incident will not occur.
9. A substitute for independent qualified review.

Redistribution, quotation, modification, and third-party reliance shall be limited as appropriate.

## 17. Acceptance Method

Recommended default:

1. Issue one client review copy.
2. Allow five business days for material factual-error or scope-conformity comments.
3. Evaluate timely comments.
4. Correct supported material errors.
5. Issue the final deliverable through the approved external-release process.
6. Preserve receipt, acknowledgment, acceptance, or completion evidence.

Client silence shall not be represented as acceptance unless the signed agreement expressly supports that result.

## 18. Change Control

A material change affects scope, deliverable, schedule, price, payment, responsibilities, assumptions, dependencies, information handling, third-party involvement, recipients, reliance, acceptance, qualified-review needs, or risk.

Material changes require an approved Commercial Change Order or other written amendment before affected work begins.

The current approved scope remains controlling until approval.

## 19. Stop and Escalation Conditions

Pause affected work when:

1. Authorization is absent, withdrawn, inconsistent, or unclear.
2. Scope, deliverables, timing, responsibilities, pricing, or obligations change without approval.
3. Work outside scope is requested.
4. Required information is missing, unreliable, or unsupported.
5. Information sensitivity or disclosure authority is unclear.
6. Prohibited information is proposed.
7. Third-party participation is proposed or discovered.
8. Qualified review is required but incomplete.
9. Commercial or contracting authority is unclear.
10. Release authority, recipient, reliance, redistribution, or acceptance is unresolved.
11. A material error, misdirection, obsolete version, or reliance concern exists.
12. Required records are missing, unstable, unrecoverable, or duplicated without control.
13. Continued work could materially increase exposure.
14. The matter exceeds approved authority or competence.

The applicable procedure controls escalation, exception, corrective action, reopening, and closure.

## 22. Residual Risk

Even when followed:

1. Client information may be incomplete.
2. Interviews may conflict.
3. Not every weakness may be identified.
4. Recommendations depend on client authority and implementation.
5. Conditions may change.
6. Workload, timing, client behavior, evidence adequacy, and friction remain unproven until actual use.
7. The client remains responsible for decisions and implementation.
