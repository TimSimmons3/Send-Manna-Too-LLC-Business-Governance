# Send Manna Too LLC Document Quality Standard

## 1. Document Control

Version: 0.1
Status: Draft
Owner: Send Manna Too LLC
Canonical Source: Markdown in Git repository
Applies To: All Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework documents and final deliverables
Effective Date: TBD
Review Cycle: At least annually or upon material governance, business, legal, commercial, or publication-format change

## 2. Purpose

This standard defines the quality, review, validation, evidence, writing, and publication requirements for Send Manna Too LLC framework documents and final deliverables.

The purpose is to ensure documents are accurate, clear, concise, evidence-based, executive-ready, legally careful, commercially disciplined, and suitable for review by clients, partners, counsel, procurement, regulators, and leadership.

## 3. Scope

This standard applies to governance documents, advisory documents, standards, frameworks, procedures, playbooks, templates, checklists, scripts, records, and final publication outputs maintained or produced by Send Manna Too LLC.

This standard does not define document hierarchy, document placement, document type classification, or conflict-resolution order. Those requirements belong in the Document Hierarchy Standard.

## 4. Relationship to the Constitution and Document Hierarchy Standard

The Send Manna Too LLC Constitution is the highest internal governance authority for the framework.

The Document Hierarchy Standard defines how documents are classified, placed, named, and controlled.

This Document Quality Standard defines how documents must be written, reviewed, validated, and prepared for final publication.

If this standard conflicts with the Constitution, the Constitution controls. If this standard conflicts with the Document Hierarchy Standard on classification or placement, the Document Hierarchy Standard controls.

## 5. Quality Principles

Documents must reflect the following quality principles:

- Integrity before growth.
- Evidence before opinion.
- Governance before execution.
- Quality before speed.
- Simplicity before complexity.
- Consistency before customization.
- Plain English.
- Executive-quality writing.
- One source of truth.
- No duplicate guidance.
- One primary purpose per document.
- Professional independence.
- Commercial integrity.

## 6. Required Document Attributes

Each document must be clear about its purpose, scope, owner, status, canonical source, applicability, and review cycle.

Each document must have one primary purpose and must align with the applicable document type defined in the Document Hierarchy Standard.

Each document must use clear headings, concise language, and a structure appropriate to its purpose.

Each document must avoid unnecessary bloat, vague assertions, unsupported claims, and duplicative guidance.

Each document must be understandable to an informed executive, reviewer, client, partner, or control owner without requiring unnecessary interpretation.

## 7. Content Quality Rules

Content must be accurate, relevant, concise, and purpose-specific.

Content must use plain English and avoid unnecessary jargon.

Content must distinguish mandatory requirements from recommendations, examples, observations, and optional guidance.

Content must not duplicate guidance that belongs in another document. When related guidance is needed, the document should reference the canonical source.

Content must be written so that reasonable objections, limitations, and implementation risks are anticipated where relevant.

## 8. Evidence and Source Rules

Documents must distinguish facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, and residual risks where relevant.

Facts must be supported by reliable evidence when they are material to a decision, recommendation, risk position, or client deliverable.

Primary sources should be preferred when available. Secondary sources may be used when appropriate and reliable. Unsupported claims must be avoided.

Source currency must be considered when laws, regulations, standards, market facts, technical facts, pricing, or external requirements may have changed.

Internal framework documents must be traceable to the canonical Markdown source in Git.

## 9. Facts, Assumptions, Inferences, Recommendations, and Residual Risk

Facts are statements supported by evidence.

Assumptions are accepted inputs that have not been fully verified.

Inferences are reasoned conclusions drawn from facts, assumptions, or evidence.

Recommendations are proposed actions or decisions.

Residual risks are remaining risks after known controls, recommendations, limitations, or constraints are considered.

Documents must label these categories when failure to distinguish them could create confusion, legal exposure, commercial exposure, or decision risk.

## 10. Legal, Commercial, and Promise-Control Rules

Documents must avoid unsupported guarantees, compliance warranties, breach-prevention promises, or commitments that exceed signed agreements or approved scope.

Documents must not imply that Send Manna Too LLC provides legal advice unless the firm has obtained appropriate legal review and authority to do so.

Documents must preserve professional independence and commercial integrity.

Documents must disclose or manage material assumptions, limitations, conflicts, and scope boundaries where relevant.

Client-facing and partner-facing documents must use careful language that supports trust without creating unsupported obligations.

## 11. Review Gate Requirements

Documents must follow the approved development lifecycle unless an exception is documented, justified, and approved.

The standard lifecycle is planning, outline review, draft, technical review, executive review, red team review, editorial review, approval, Git commit, GitHub push, and baseline.

Technical review must validate structure, scope alignment, document placement, file path, headings, links where applicable, and repository status.

Executive review must validate clarity, usefulness, tone, business relevance, and decision-readiness.

Red team review must challenge assumptions, legal exposure, commercial exposure, ambiguity, duplicate guidance, overreach, and misuse risk.

Editorial review must validate grammar, spelling, punctuation, consistency, formatting, concision, and plain-English readability.

## 12. Technical Validation Requirements

Before commit, Markdown documents must be checked for expected location, expected headings, non-ASCII characters, trailing whitespace, and unintended file changes.

New files must be reviewed with Git diff before commit.

Repository status must be checked before and after commit.

After push, local and remote branches must be confirmed as synchronized.

Technical validation results should be sufficient for a later reviewer to understand what was changed and why.

## 13. Publication and Final Deliverable Requirements

Markdown in Git remains the canonical source for framework documents.

Word, PDF, PowerPoint, and other formatted outputs are publication formats and must remain traceable to the canonical Markdown source unless a documented exception states otherwise.

Final client-facing, partner-facing, executive, or publication-ready deliverables must use a controlled publication format where technically practical.

Final publication-ready deliverables must include the Send Manna Too LLC logo in the header where the format supports headers.

Final publication-ready deliverables must include a footer confidentiality marking: Property and Confidential.

Final publication-ready deliverables must include page numbering in the format: Page 1 of X Pages, where the format supports page numbering.

Publication-format requirements apply to final outputs. They do not make Word, PDF, or PowerPoint the authoritative source.

## 14. Acceptance Criteria

A document is acceptable when it is accurate, clear, concise, purpose-specific, non-duplicative, aligned to the Constitution, aligned to the Document Hierarchy Standard, and technically validated.

A final deliverable is acceptable when it is publication-ready, grammatically clean, properly marked where applicable, traceable to its source, and free from unsupported claims or unintended commitments.

A document must not be approved if it creates duplicate guidance, unclear authority, unsupported promises, unexplained assumptions, unmanaged conflicts, or avoidable legal or commercial exposure.

## 15. Exceptions

Exceptions to this standard must be documented, justified, and approved.

Exceptions must not compromise the Constitution, source-of-truth control, confidentiality, professional integrity, commercial integrity, or document traceability.

Recurring exceptions may indicate that the quality standard, publication templates, or review process should be updated.
