## 1. Purpose

This standard defines the minimum requirements for developing, reviewing, approving, presenting, changing, administering, validating, and preserving pricing and related commercial decisions.

It operationalizes the Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy without creating actual prices, signature authority, legal terms, client commitments, accounting conclusions, tax conclusions, insurance conclusions, market validation, operating-effectiveness claims, or pilot authority.

This standard governs:

1. Pricing bases.
2. Scope-to-price traceability.
3. Effort estimation.
4. Cost and pricing calculations.
5. Complexity, risk, timing, capacity, and collection adjustments.
6. Default commercial positions.
7. Deposits and advance payments.
8. Invoicing, payment, expense, travel, remobilization, collection, settlement, write-off, and bad-debt decisions.
9. Discounts, concessions, strategic investment, nonprofit treatment, and pro bono treatment.
10. Pricing approval authority.
11. Private rate-card and commercial-model control requirements.
12. Client-specific pricing records.
13. Commercial scenarios and KPI governance.
14. Qualified-review triggers.
15. Records, exceptions, noncompliance, review, and approval.

This standard does not establish that G2 or G3 has passed.

## 3. Pricing Principles

Pricing shall be:

1. Tied to understood scope.
2. Supported by a reasonable effort estimate.
3. Adjusted for complexity, timing, risk, dependencies, and required expertise.
4. Commercially supportable.
5. Clear about assumptions and exclusions.
6. Approved before presentation as final.
7. Revised when material conditions change.
8. Protected when disclosure could weaken commercial position.
9. Traceable to the applicable service, proposal, SOW, and approval.
10. Free from unsupported promises or hidden obligations.
11. Traceable to approved private evidence identifiers when private values are used.
12. Supported by identified sources, assumptions, formulas, and versions.
13. Evaluated for capacity, collection, and opportunity-cost effects when material.
14. Explicit about qualified-review status.
15. Separated into public controls and private populated evidence.
16. Free from unsupported precision.
17. Versioned, approved, recoverable, and superseded through controlled records when relied upon.
18. Clear that owner approval is not independent or qualified review.
19. Conservative when material information is incomplete or unreliable.
20. Limited to authority actually granted.

## 7. Pricing Calculation Method

Use the confidential pricing record:

`Base effort value = approved estimated hours x approved confidential rate`

`Proposed fee = base effort value + approved complexity, risk, timing, expense, and contingency adjustments`

A different final price requires documented rationale, commercial effect, risk, assumptions, capacity effect, collection effect, and approval.

## 23. Public-Private Evidence Architecture

Pricing governance uses a B-09 mixed workflow:

1. Public Git contains the governance method.
2. Approved private sources contain populated rates, costs, prices, margins, thresholds, models, approvals, invoices, collections, and qualified-review evidence.
3. Stable non-confidential evidence identifiers connect the public and private sources.
4. The public artifact defines required fields but does not contain populated private values.
5. The private artifact identifies the governing public control.
6. Public summaries are sanitized and approved.
7. Private comments, paths, values, identities, and privileged advice remain private.
8. Failure of private evidence controls blocks the affected pricing or commercial activity.

A public evidence reference shall not reveal:

1. Private file name.
2. Private path.
3. Client or vendor identity.
4. Commercial value.
5. Signature.
6. Contract term.
7. Privileged description.
8. Credential.
9. Recovery detail.
10. Unnecessary personal information.

## 33. Stop Conditions and Fallbacks

Stop the affected activity when:

1. G2 has not passed before private population or reliance.
2. Applicable G3 requirements are incomplete before a qualified conclusion or live use.
3. A private authoritative source is not identified.
4. A material scope or obligation remains unclear.
5. An assumption is represented as evidence.
6. A management metric is represented as a qualified accounting measure without review.
7. A formula cannot be independently recomputed.
8. A model cannot reconcile.
9. A hidden or unauthorized override exists.
10. A required qualified review is concealed or bypassed.
11. An exception lacks approval, impact evidence, expiration, or compensating control.
12. A private value is proposed for public Git.
13. A stale version may be used.
14. A recovery test fails.
15. External pricing, invoicing, collection, contracting, delivery, or pilot activity is proposed without authority.
16. Release 2.7 or later work begins.
17. A stricter requirement conflicts with this standard.

Required fallbacks include:

| Condition | Fallback |
| --- | --- |
| Private source unavailable | Complete public controls and unpopulated structures only |
| G2 fails | Repair or replace the source and repeat validation |
| G3 input unavailable | Restrict the conclusion or live use and obtain qualified input |
| Market evidence weak | Use a labeled assumption or range and conservative scenario |
| Model does not reconcile | Freeze approval, correct, and rerun affected tests |
| Qualified review pending | Record interim restriction and defer affected use |
| Recovery fails | Repair backup or recovery and repeat the test |
| Client terms conflict | Escalate, revise, price, accept with authority, or reject later |
| Confidential exposure occurs | Contain, preserve evidence, remediate, and revalidate |
