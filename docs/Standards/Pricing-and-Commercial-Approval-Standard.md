## 1. Purpose

This standard defines minimum requirements for developing, reviewing, approving, presenting, changing, and preserving pricing and related commercial decisions.

It operationalizes the Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy without creating actual prices, signature authority, legal terms, or client commitments.

## 2. Governing Authority

This standard is subordinate to the Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy, the Governance and Operating Authority Policy, the Client Engagement Governance Policy and procedure, confidentiality and records policies, the External Deliverable Governance Policy, the Document Quality Standard, DEC-0017, and DEC-0018 when approved.

Pricing approval does not authorize an engagement to begin.

## 3. Pricing Principles

Pricing shall be:

1. Tied to understood scope.
2. Supported by a reasonable effort estimate.
3. Adjusted for complexity, timing, risk, dependencies, and required expertise.
4. Commercially supportable.
5. Clear about assumptions and exclusions.
6. Approved before presentation as final.
7. Revised when material conditions change.
8. Protected when disclosure could weaken commercial position.
9. Traceable to the applicable service, proposal, SOW, and approval.
10. Free from unsupported promises or hidden obligations.

## 4. Permitted Pricing Bases

Approved bases:

1. Fixed fee.
2. Time and materials.
3. Milestone fee.
4. Capped time and materials.
5. Another basis approved as a commercial exception.

For the Narrow Advisory Pilot Service:

- Fixed fee is preferred when scope and assumptions are clear.
- Capped time and materials may be used when uncertainty remains but can be bounded.
- Open-ended time and materials requires explicit approval.
- Retainers, recurring managed-service pricing, success fees, contingent fees, commissions, and revenue sharing are outside Release 2.4 unless separately approved.

## 5. Scope-to-Price Traceability

A pricing record shall identify:

1. Service.
2. Client or opportunity identifier.
3. Scope version.
4. Deliverable.
5. Estimated phases and effort.
6. Assumptions.
7. Dependencies.
8. Exclusions.
9. Schedule.
10. Required expertise.
11. Complexity factors.
12. Risk factors.
13. Expenses.
14. Payment structure.
15. Acceptance assumptions.
16. Change-control method.
17. Approver and approval date.
18. Validity period.
19. Related proposal or SOW.

Pricing shall not be final while material scope or obligation uncertainty remains.

## 6. Effort Estimation

Consider:

1. Qualification and planning.
2. Commercial and contract review.
3. Initiation.
4. Interviews.
5. Information review.
6. Analysis.
7. Drafting.
8. Quality review.
9. Client review support.
10. Finalization.
11. Publication and release validation.
12. Delivery.
13. Acceptance and closeout.
14. Administration.
15. Reasonable rework.
16. Contingency for identified uncertainty.

Governance, evidence, review, publication, and closeout work shall not be omitted merely because it is less visible to the client.

## 7. Pricing Calculation Method

Use the confidential pricing record:

`Base effort value = approved estimated hours x approved confidential rate`

`Proposed fee = base effort value + approved complexity, risk, timing, expense, and contingency adjustments`

A different final price requires documented rationale, commercial effect, risk, and approval.

Actual rates, margins, floors, ceilings, discount thresholds, and negotiation positions shall not be stored in the public repository.

## 8. Complexity and Risk Adjustments

Consider:

1. Unclear scope.
2. Multiple stakeholders.
3. Compressed timing.
4. Unusual review.
5. Additional revisions.
6. Difficult dependencies.
7. High expected reliance.
8. Complex acceptance.
9. Extensive negotiation.
10. Expanded information obligations.
11. Specialized expertise.
12. Travel.
13. Scheduling disruption.
14. Client-imposed tools or administration.
15. Elevated legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional-review needs.

A material factor shall be priced, excluded, limited, escalated, or separately authorized.

## 9. Narrow Advisory Pilot Default Commercial Position

Subject to client-specific review:

1. Fixed fee.
2. Fifty percent due before initiation.
3. Fifty percent invoiced upon final issuance or another defined milestone.
4. Net 15 payment terms.
5. Preapproved reasonable expenses billed at cost when applicable.
6. Proposal validity of thirty calendar days.
7. One factual-review cycle.
8. Five business days for client review unless the SOW states otherwise.
9. No out-of-scope work without approved change control.
10. No obligation to begin until authorization and applicable initial-payment conditions are satisfied.
11. No guarantee of outcome, compliance, risk reduction, or breach prevention.
12. Right to pause affected work for material nonpayment, subject to signed terms and appropriate notice.

These are business positions, not automatically binding terms. The signed agreement controls.

## 10. Deposit and Advance-Payment Decisions

An initial payment is generally appropriate when:

1. Capacity is reserved.
2. Work is fixed fee.
3. The client is new.
4. Material preparation occurs before visible delivery.
5. Collection risk is uncertain.
6. The engagement is short.
7. The firm would otherwise carry disproportionate risk.

Record amount or percentage, invoice trigger, due date, start dependency, termination treatment, and any refund or credit treatment requiring review.

## 11. Invoicing, Payment, Expenses, and Taxes

The approved record shall define:

1. Invoice triggers.
2. Invoice timing.
3. Due dates.
4. Payment method.
5. Expenses.
6. Taxes.
7. Disputed invoice handling.
8. Late-payment consequences.
9. Suspension rights.
10. Final invoice and closeout dependency.

Expenses shall be necessary, reasonable, in scope, preapproved when practical, and evidenced.

No unsupported tax conclusion may be made. Qualified review applies when interpretation is required.

No bank, tax identifier, or sensitive financial information shall be stored in the public repository.

## 12. Discounts and Concessions

A discount, concession, deferred payment, extended term, free work commitment, extra revision, or other departure shall identify:

1. Business rationale.
2. Financial effect.
3. Scope or delivery effect.
4. Risk.
5. Client expectation.
6. Approval authority.
7. Expiration.
8. Compensating limitation or control.

A discount shall not conceal scope, reduce required quality, bypass review, or create an unsupported obligation.

## 13. Price Validity

The recommended default is thirty calendar days.

After expiration, reconfirm price when scope, timing, availability, costs, market conditions, dependencies, contract terms, expertise, or risk may have changed.

## 14. Change Pricing

A material change shall be priced and approved before affected work begins.

Record:

1. Current scope.
2. Requested change.
3. Effort effect.
4. Schedule effect.
5. Deliverable effect.
6. Assumption and dependency effect.
7. Commercial and contractual effect.
8. Information-handling effect.
9. Review needs.
10. Revised price or basis.
11. Approval.
12. Effective date.

Informal discussion, urgency, or partial performance does not approve a price change.

## 15. Pricing Approval Authority

Only authorized business authority may approve:

1. Final price.
2. Pricing basis.
3. Payment structure.
4. Discount or concession.
5. Extended terms.
6. No-charge work.
7. Material write-off.
8. Commercial exception.
9. Change price.
10. Client-facing price communication.

The approval record shall identify the decision, date, approver, scope, price, assumptions, and related document.

## 16. Confidential Internal Rate Card Control

An actual rate card may be created only in an approved non-public location.

It shall identify service or role, pricing basis, standard rate, minimum approved rate, fixed-fee assumptions, complexity and risk treatment, rush treatment, discount authority, effective date, version, approver, review date, confidentiality marking, authorized users, authoritative source, backup, recovery, and supersession.

Before actual values are created:

1. Approve a non-public storage location.
2. Restrict access.
3. Define version and approval controls.
4. Validate backup and recovery.
5. Document market, cost, experience, or other pricing evidence.
6. Approve the values.
7. Prevent public exposure.

## 17. Client-Specific Pricing Record

The confidential record shall preserve client or opportunity, scope, proposed and approved price, basis, estimate, assumptions, adjustments, discounts, payment terms, validity, approval, proposal and SOW traceability, changes, outcome, and collection status when material.

The public repository shall not contain actual client-specific values.

## 18. Qualified-Review Triggers

Escalate for:

1. Unusual liability.
2. Indemnity.
3. Warranty or guarantee.
4. Insurance.
5. Audit rights.
6. Security obligations.
7. Compliance commitments.
8. Regulatory or professional conclusions.
9. Tax interpretation.
10. Intellectual-property transfer.
11. Unusual termination or refund obligations.
12. Penalties, service credits, liquidated damages, or contingent fees.
13. Automatic renewal.
14. Most-favored pricing.
15. Long payment periods.
16. Client unilateral change rights.
17. Unlimited revisions or support.
18. Material third-party dependencies.
19. Material confidentiality or data-protection obligations.
20. Another term creating material exposure.

## 19. Standalone SLA Determination

A standalone SLA is not required for the Narrow Advisory Pilot Service because it is fixed scope, time bounded, advisory, and not an uptime, continuous monitoring, incident response, ongoing support, or service-credit service.

The SOW shall define schedule, milestones, communication cadence, review period, response expectations, dependency treatment, hold conditions, acceptance, escalation, suspension, and termination.

A future recurring or operational service requires separate approval and may require an SLA.

## 20. Records, Exceptions, and Noncompliance

Pricing evidence may include scope, estimate, confidential rate source, adjustment rationale, approval, discount or exception, proposal, SOW, change order, invoice, payment, and closeout.

The authoritative source shall be identifiable, protected, and recoverable.

Material pricing exceptions follow the cross-policy procedure.

Unauthorized commitments, discounts, out-of-scope work, unsupported promises, or uncontrolled client-facing prices shall be assessed for noncompliance and corrective action.

## 21. Review and Maintenance

Review when services, costs, market conditions, capacity, operating evidence, payment experience, contracting requirements, storage methods, or qualified review identify a material change.

Actual confidential rates shall be reviewed in their approved non-public source.
