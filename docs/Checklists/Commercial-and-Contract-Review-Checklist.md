## 1. Purpose

This checklist supports proportionate review before Send Manna Too LLC approves, presents, signs, changes, or relies on a proposal, SOW, order, MSA, confidentiality agreement, amendment, or other commercial instrument.

It does not provide legal advice, approve a contract, grant signature authority, or replace qualified review.

## 2. Use and Status Values

For a specific matter, create a review record in an approved non-public location.

Use:

- `Yes`
- `No`
- `Not applicable`
- `Open`
- `Escalate`

A completed checklist is supporting evidence and does not authorize signature or work commencement.

## 3. Identification

- [ ] Client or contracting party is identified.
- [ ] Document type, title, version, and source are identified.
- [ ] Proposed effective date is identified.
- [ ] Reviewer and review date are identified.
- [ ] Opportunity or engagement is identified.
- [ ] Authoritative contract source is identified.
- [ ] Confidentiality and access restrictions are identified.

## 4. Authority

- [ ] Client requesting authority is understood.
- [ ] Client signature authority is understood.
- [ ] Send Manna Too LLC business approval authority is identified.
- [ ] Send Manna Too LLC signature authority is identified.
- [ ] No draft, email, proposal, or informal statement is treated as authorization.
- [ ] Required legal, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional reviewer is identified.
- [ ] No work begins before authorization.
- [ ] Any exception is documented and approved.

## 5. Scope and Deliverables

- [ ] Purpose is clear.
- [ ] In-scope and out-of-scope services are clear.
- [ ] Deliverables and formats are clear.
- [ ] Deliverable status and release requirements are understood.
- [ ] Client and firm responsibilities are clear.
- [ ] Assumptions, dependencies, constraints, and schedule are supportable.
- [ ] Revision limits are clear.
- [ ] Follow-up work requires separate authorization.
- [ ] No unapproved third-party delivery is required.
- [ ] No prohibited information or system access is required.
- [ ] Scope aligns with the approved service standard when applicable.

## 6. Pricing and Payment

- [ ] Pricing basis is identified.
- [ ] Price is traceable to scope and estimate.
- [ ] Price has appropriate approval.
- [ ] Proposal validity is defined.
- [ ] Initial payment is defined when applicable.
- [ ] Invoice triggers and due dates are clear.
- [ ] Expenses and taxes are addressed.
- [ ] Late-payment and suspension treatment are addressed.
- [ ] Refund or credit treatment is understood when present.
- [ ] Discounts or concessions are approved.
- [ ] No actual pricing is stored in the public repository.
- [ ] Change pricing is required before out-of-scope work.

## 7. Acceptance and Completion

- [ ] Acceptance criteria are clear.
- [ ] Client review period and authority are clear.
- [ ] Receipt, review, acknowledgment, and acceptance are not conflated.
- [ ] Client silence is not acceptance unless expressly supported.
- [ ] Disputed acceptance treatment is addressed.
- [ ] Effect on invoicing and closeout is clear.
- [ ] Open items and follow-up work are addressed.

## 8. Change Control

- [ ] Material change is defined.
- [ ] Approval authority is clear.
- [ ] Written evidence is required.
- [ ] Scope, price, schedule, responsibilities, assumptions, dependencies, information, and acceptance effects are addressed.
- [ ] Existing scope remains controlling until approval.
- [ ] Urgency does not bypass approval.
- [ ] Unauthorized completed change can route to noncompliance and corrective action.

## 9. Confidentiality and Information Handling

- [ ] Confidential information is sufficiently defined.
- [ ] Authorized purpose and need-to-know recipients are clear.
- [ ] Information sharing is limited.
- [ ] Client information use is limited to the authorized purpose.
- [ ] Minimization, anonymization, summarization, or redaction is used when appropriate.
- [ ] External disclosure authority is clear.
- [ ] Return, deletion, or preservation terms do not conflict with records requirements.
- [ ] Client-name or publicity use requires approval.
- [ ] Privileged or highly sensitive information is excluded or separately reviewed.
- [ ] Security and data-protection obligations are supportable.
- [ ] Confidential pricing and negotiation strategy remain outside the public repository.

## 10. Intellectual Property and Reuse

- [ ] Firm pre-existing materials are identified or protected.
- [ ] Client-provided materials are identified.
- [ ] Deliverable ownership or license is clear.
- [ ] Firm reuse rights are clear and consistent with confidentiality.
- [ ] Client reuse and modification rights are clear.
- [ ] Third-party materials and license restrictions are addressed.
- [ ] Client information is not reused without sanitization and approval.
- [ ] No uncontrolled transfer of the Business Governance Framework occurs.

## 11. Warranties, Guarantees, and Disclaimers

- [ ] No guarantee of outcome, compliance, certification, risk reduction, or breach prevention is created.
- [ ] Advisory and evidence limitations are clear.
- [ ] Warranty obligations are identified and supportable.
- [ ] Disclaimers do not contradict scope or signed obligations.
- [ ] No unlimited correction, revision, support, or performance obligation exists.
- [ ] No legal, audit, certification, or attestation opinion is implied.

## 12. Indemnity and Liability

- [ ] Indemnity scope, defense duties, and settlement control are understood.
- [ ] Liability cap and basis are identified.
- [ ] Excluded damages and carve-outs are identified.
- [ ] Uncapped or disproportionate exposure is escalated.
- [ ] Liability treatment is consistent across documents.
- [ ] Qualified legal and insurance review is complete when required.

## 13. Insurance

- [ ] Required insurance types and limits are identified.
- [ ] Coverage is available or the requirement is revised or excluded.
- [ ] Additional insured, waiver, primary, notice, and evidence requirements are reviewed.
- [ ] Obligations do not exceed actual coverage without approval.
- [ ] Qualified insurance review is complete when required.

## 14. Security, Privacy, Audit, and Compliance

- [ ] Security obligations are identified and supportable.
- [ ] Security addendum need is identified.
- [ ] Privacy and data-processing obligations are identified.
- [ ] DPA or BAA need is identified.
- [ ] Audit rights are bounded.
- [ ] Evidence-production duties are supportable.
- [ ] Compliance language does not create unsupported certification or warranty.
- [ ] Incident-notification duties are supportable.
- [ ] Regulatory or professional review is complete when required.

## 15. Third Parties and Assignment

- [ ] Subcontracting and assignment rights are clear.
- [ ] No unapproved subcontractor is required.
- [ ] Vendor or platform dependencies are understood.
- [ ] Referral or partner obligations are identified.
- [ ] Third-party delivery and information access remain excluded for the narrow pilot.
- [ ] Any proposed exception is separately approved.

## 16. Term, Renewal, Suspension, and Termination

- [ ] Term and start conditions are clear.
- [ ] Renewal and automatic renewal are identified.
- [ ] Termination for convenience and cause are understood.
- [ ] Cure periods and suspension rights are understood.
- [ ] Payment upon termination is addressed.
- [ ] Work product, confidentiality, records, and preservation after termination are addressed.
- [ ] Transition duties are supportable.
- [ ] Termination does not require unauthorized record disposition.

## 17. Disputes and General Terms

- [ ] Governing law, venue, and dispute process are understood.
- [ ] Arbitration and attorneys' fees terms are reviewed.
- [ ] Notice method is workable.
- [ ] Force majeure is addressed.
- [ ] Independent-contractor status is clear.
- [ ] Restrictive or publicity terms are identified.
- [ ] Amendment, order of precedence, entire agreement, survival, and electronic signature terms are clear.
- [ ] Qualified legal review is complete when required.

## 18. Records and Evidence

- [ ] Proposal and pricing approval are preserved.
- [ ] Contract-review evidence is preserved.
- [ ] Final agreement and signature evidence are identifiable.
- [ ] Changes, invoices, payments, deliverables, and acceptance are traceable as applicable.
- [ ] Exceptions and escalations are preserved.
- [ ] Authoritative source is clear.
- [ ] Records are protected and recoverable.
- [ ] No record disposition is authorized during a DEC-0017 pilot.
- [ ] Duplicate authoritative sources are avoided.

## 19. Mandatory Escalation

Select `Escalate` when there is:

- [ ] Unclear authority.
- [ ] Materially unusual terms.
- [ ] Unlimited or disproportionate liability.
- [ ] Broad indemnity or defense duty.
- [ ] Warranty, guarantee, certification, or attestation.
- [ ] Material insurance requirement.
- [ ] Audit or inspection right.
- [ ] Security or compliance warranty.
- [ ] DPA, BAA, or regulated-data requirement.
- [ ] Intellectual-property transfer.
- [ ] Unusual refund, penalty, service credit, or liquidated damages.
- [ ] Automatic renewal or long commitment.
- [ ] Client unilateral change right.
- [ ] Unusual governing law, venue, or dispute term.
- [ ] Third-party delivery or access.
- [ ] Privileged or highly sensitive information.
- [ ] Tax, insurance, regulatory, or professional conclusion.
- [ ] Another condition creating material exposure.

## 20. Final Determination

Select one:

- [ ] Approved for signature.
- [ ] Approved with conditions.
- [ ] Return for revision.
- [ ] Defer.
- [ ] Reject.
- [ ] Escalate.

Record decision, date, authority, open conditions, reviewer, signature authority, authorized start condition, evidence, and residual risk.
