## 1. Purpose and Legal Boundary

This matrix defines Send Manna Too LLC business requirements, preferred positions, escalation triggers, and evidence expectations for an MSA or similar governing agreement.

This matrix:

1. Is not an MSA.
2. Is not legal advice.
3. Does not establish legal sufficiency.
4. Does not replace qualified counsel.
5. Does not grant signature authority.
6. Does not authorize an engagement.
7. Shall not contain client-confidential negotiation details in the public canonical source.

A client-specific review record shall be maintained in an approved non-public location.

## 3. Business Requirements Matrix

| Topic | Minimum Business Requirement | Preferred Business Position | Escalate or Reject When | Required Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Parties | Correct legal entities and capacity | Full legal names and addresses | Entity, affiliate scope, or capacity is unclear | Entity and authority verification |
| Authority | Signers have authority | Named authorized representatives | Informal acceptance or unclear signer authority | Signature approval |
| Scope mechanism | Work starts only under approved SOW or order | No work without signed SOW | MSA creates open-ended work | Ordering-method review |
| Ordering documents | Scope, fees, schedule, acceptance, and exclusions are required | Standard SOW fields | Client can issue unilateral binding orders | SOW approval |
| Order of precedence | Conflicts can be resolved | Signed change, SOW, MSA, exhibits | Hidden terms or dynamic policies control | Final document-set review |
| Fees | Fees are defined in SOWs | No implied free work | Client may change fees unilaterally | Pricing approval |
| Invoicing | Triggers and timing are clear | Milestone or delivery based | Payment depends on undefined acceptance | Invoice-term review |
| Payment terms | Collection exposure is bounded | Net 15 unless approved otherwise | Excessive term, pay-when-paid, or contingent payment | Commercial approval |
| Initial payment | Advance payment may be required | Initial payment before initiation for short fixed-fee work | Reasonable start condition is prohibited without review | Pricing approval |
| Expenses | Treatment is clear | Preapproved reasonable expenses at cost | Unlimited or unapproved expenses are expected | Expense approval |
| Taxes | Responsibility is clear | Client pays applicable transaction taxes excluding firm income taxes | Unsupported tax conclusion is required | Tax review |
| Confidentiality | Mutual duties are workable | Need-to-know use for authorized purpose | Definition or duties are unbounded | NDA/MSA review |
| Information security | Duties match service and capability | Reasonable safeguards proportionate to information | Prescriptive controls, certification, or warranty exceeds capability | Security and legal review |
| Data protection | Obligations match information boundary | No regulated or high-risk data for narrow pilot | DPA, BAA, cross-border, or regulated-data duty arises | Privacy/legal review |
| Client materials | Client may provide materials lawfully | Client confirms authority to provide | Firm bears risk for unlawful client content | Contract review |
| Pre-existing firm materials | Firm retains ownership | Express reservation of methods, templates, know-how, and frameworks | Client claims unrelated or pre-existing IP | IP review |
| Deliverable rights | Client gets rights appropriate to paid deliverable | Internal-use license or approved ownership subject to retained firm materials | Transfer captures firm methods or framework | IP and SOW review |
| Reuse and know-how | Generalized learning may be retained without client identity or confidential content | Express sanitized know-how right after counsel review | Clause blocks ordinary professional learning or conflicts with confidentiality | IP/legal review |
| Third-party materials | External rights are respected | Only authorized material is used | Transfer of rights firm does not own | Source and license evidence |
| Warranties | Duties are limited and supportable | Professional performance; no outcome warranty | Broad result, compliance, security, or noninfringement warranty | Legal review |
| Disclaimers | Advisory limits are preserved | No guarantee, certification, attestation, or legal opinion | Broad reliance remains while disclaimers are removed | Legal and risk review |
| Indemnity | Duty is narrow, balanced, and insurable | Controlled third-party claims and fault | Broad, first-party, uncapped, defense-on-demand, or one-way indemnity | Legal and insurance review |
| Defense control | Defense duties are manageable | Notice, control, cooperation, settlement protections | Settlement can impose admission or obligation | Legal review |
| Liability cap | Exposure is proportionate | Cap tied to affected SOW fees or another approved amount | Unlimited or unrelated cap | Governance, legal, insurance approval |
| Excluded damages | Consequential exposure is limited | Mutual exclusion of indirect, special, punitive, and lost-profit damages | Carve-outs eliminate practical protection | Legal review |
| Liability carve-outs | Carve-outs are deliberate | Only specifically approved obligations | Uncapped confidentiality, security, IP, indemnity, or negligence exposure | Legal and insurance review |
| Insurance | Requirements match coverage and risk | Limits consistent with actual policies | Unavailable coverage or burdensome additional-insured duties | Broker or insurance review |
| Audit rights | Rights are proportionate | Limited records, notice, frequency, purpose, and cost | Intrusive on-demand audit or unrelated access | Legal and operational review |
| Compliance | Duties apply within scope | No blanket compliance warranty | Unknown laws, policies, or standards are incorporated | Legal/regulatory review |
| Client policies | Only provided and approved policies apply | No unilateral future policy changes | Dynamic web policies bind automatically | Contract review |
| Publicity | Names and marks require approval | Mutual written consent | Client may announce or use marks unilaterally | Brand approval |
| Non-solicitation | Restriction is narrow or removed | No restriction unless justified | Broad workforce, client, or market restriction | Legal review |
| Independent contractor | Relationship is clear | No partnership, agency, or employment | Terms create agency or authority to bind | Legal review |
| No authority to bind | Neither party can bind the other | Express limitation | Client or partner can create firm obligations | Authority review |
| Assignment | Transfer is controlled | Consent with approved transaction exception | Assignment to unknown higher-risk party | Legal/commercial review |
| Subcontracting | Firm retains control | No subcontractor for narrow pilot | Client requires or assumes subcontractor delivery | Governance and legal review |
| Force majeure | Excused delay is reasonable | Events beyond reasonable control allow adjustment | Payment or confidentiality is improperly excused | Legal review |
| Suspension | Firm can pause for nonpayment, risk, missing dependency, or unauthorized conditions | Written notice and controlled hold | Firm must continue despite nonpayment or unsafe conditions | Commercial/legal review |
| Termination for convenience | Financial effect is fair | Payment for completed work, reserved capacity, and noncancelable cost | Client can terminate without paying accrued amounts | Legal/commercial review |
| Termination for cause | Cause and cure are clear | Material breach and reasonable cure | Immediate termination for minor or subjective issue | Legal review |
| Effect of termination | Open obligations and work product are addressed | Accrued fees, confidentiality, IP, records, and preservation survive as appropriate | Required evidence must be destroyed or unpaid transition is required | Legal/records review |
| Refunds and credits | Duties are defined and bounded | No automatic refund absent agreed failure or termination term | Broad refund, penalty, chargeback, or service credit | Legal/commercial approval |
| Service levels | Only applicable commitments are included | No standalone SLA for fixed-scope advisory work | Uptime, continuous support, service credits, or operational response is imposed | Separate SLA decision and review |
| Dispute escalation | Business escalation precedes formal process when practical | Executive negotiation then agreed process | One-sided or impractical process | Legal review |
| Governing law | Jurisdiction is understood | Illinois or another approved jurisdiction | Unfamiliar or high-risk law | Qualified legal review |
| Venue | Forum is practical | Illinois or another approved forum | Distant or burdensome exclusive venue | Qualified legal review |
| Arbitration | Cost and rights are understood | Use only after qualified review | Mandatory process is costly, one-sided, or unclear | Legal review |
| Attorneys' fees | Exposure is balanced | Each party bears own fees or approved prevailing-party term | One-way fee shifting | Legal review |
| Notices | Method and contacts are workable | Email plus recognized formal method when appropriate | Notice may be ineffective or unstable | Contract administration review |
| Renewal | Renewal is deliberate | No automatic renewal for narrow project | Automatic renewal or long notice period | Commercial/legal review |
| Most-favored pricing | Avoided | No MFN obligation | Broad comparison or retroactive price right | Governance/legal review |
| Exclusivity | Avoided | No exclusivity | Client restricts market, industry, or services | Governance/legal review |
| Records | Commercial evidence can be preserved | Contract, approvals, changes, delivery, acceptance, and closeout remain recoverable | Destruction or access terms conflict with evidence needs | Records/legal review |
| Preservation | Holds can be honored | Disposition suspended when required | Automatic deletion conflicts with preservation | Legal/records review |
| Confidential legal review | Privilege and confidentiality are protected | Counsel advice remains outside public repository | Privileged analysis must be disclosed | Counsel review |
| Amendments | Changes require written authority | Signed amendment or change order | Informal or unilateral amendment binds | Authority and contract review |
| Counterparts and e-signature | Execution is valid and traceable | Electronic signatures accepted | Identity or authority is unreliable | Signature evidence |
| Entire agreement | Integrated documents are identifiable | MSA, SOW, and signed exhibits only | External policies or proposals create hidden duties | Final document-set review |
| Survival | Only appropriate obligations survive | Payment, confidentiality, IP, limitation, disputes, and records as approved | Operational duties survive indefinitely without justification | Legal review |

## 4. Client-Specific Review Record

The non-public record shall identify:

1. Contract title and version.
2. Counterparty.
3. Reviewer and date.
4. Status for each material topic.
5. Requested revision.
6. Business rationale.
7. Qualified-review need.
8. Final negotiated position.
9. Approval and signature authority.
10. Residual risk.
11. Authoritative final agreement.
12. Related SOW and commercial evidence.

## 5. Mandatory Escalation

Qualified review is required for:

1. Unlimited or disproportionate liability.
2. Broad indemnity or defense.
3. Security or compliance warranty.
4. Regulated-data duties.
5. IP transfer beyond the paid deliverable.
6. Audit or inspection rights.
7. Material insurance changes.
8. Penalties, credits, refunds, or liquidated damages.
9. Unusual governing law, venue, arbitration, or fee shifting.
10. Automatic renewal, exclusivity, MFN, or long-term commitment.
11. Third-party delivery or access.
12. Another term creating material legal, financial, insurance, operational, confidentiality, or reputational exposure.

## 6. Final Determination

Record one:

- Acceptable for signature.
- Acceptable with conditions.
- Return for revision.
- Defer.
- Reject.
- Escalate.

The determination does not grant signature authority.
