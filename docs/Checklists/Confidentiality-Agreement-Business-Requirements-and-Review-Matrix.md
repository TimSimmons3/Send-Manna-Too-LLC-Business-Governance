## 1. Purpose and Legal Boundary

This matrix defines business requirements and review questions for an NDA, confidentiality agreement, or confidentiality section.

This matrix:

1. Is not an NDA.
2. Is not legal advice.
3. Does not establish legal sufficiency.
4. Does not replace qualified counsel.
5. Does not grant signature or disclosure authority.
6. Does not authorize receipt of information outside an approved purpose.
7. Shall not contain client-confidential negotiation details in the public canonical source.

## 3. Business Requirements Matrix

| Topic | Minimum Business Requirement | Preferred Business Position | Escalate or Reject When | Required Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Parties | Legal entities and capacity are clear | Correct full legal names | Affiliate or recipient scope is unclear | Entity and authority verification |
| Structure | Mutual or unilateral structure matches the relationship | Mutual when both parties disclose | One-way duties apply despite mutual disclosure | Business and legal review |
| Authorized purpose | Use is limited to a defined legitimate purpose | Evaluation or performance of the identified relationship | Purpose is unlimited, vague, or unrelated | Purpose and engagement record |
| Definition | Protected information is reasonably identifiable | Information disclosed for the purpose and reasonably understood as confidential | Definition captures public, independently developed, or unrelated information without exclusions | Legal review |
| Marking | Protection does not depend solely on impractical marking | Written, oral, and visual information protected when reasonably understood | Unmarked non-public information would be unprotected despite context | Contract review |
| Oral disclosures | Treatment is workable | Protected when identified or reasonably understood, with confirmation when practical | Impractical confirmation requirement defeats protection | Business/legal review |
| Exclusions | Standard exclusions apply | Public, previously known, independently developed, rightfully received, and approved release | Exclusions are absent or proof burden is unreasonable | Legal review |
| Need-to-know | Access is limited to authorized representatives | Employees and qualified advisers with need to know and protective duties | Broad affiliate, partner, vendor, or third-party access is allowed | Access and role evidence |
| Representative responsibility | Responsibility is proportionate | Party responsible for authorized representatives under reasonable control | Strict liability applies to unrelated or unknown parties | Legal review |
| Standard of care | Protection standard is reasonable | At least reasonable care and no less than own similar information | Absolute security or outcome warranty | Legal/security review |
| Minimization | Only necessary information is disclosed | Staged, summarized, anonymized, or redacted disclosure | Agreement is used to justify unnecessary collection | Information review |
| Security duties | Duties match information and capability | Reasonable safeguards proportionate to sensitivity | Prescriptive certification, technical control, or audit duty arises | Security/legal review |
| Compelled disclosure | Lawful disclosure is permitted with limits | Prompt notice when legally allowed and minimum disclosure | Agreement prevents lawful compliance or requires unauthorized action | Legal review |
| Residual knowledge | Client trust and firm know-how are protected | No broad residuals right for client content; ordinary unaided professional learning only after counsel review | Residuals clause permits reuse of sensitive client content | Legal/IP review |
| No license | Disclosure does not transfer ownership | Express no-license provision | Agreement grants implied IP rights | IP/legal review |
| No obligation | Evaluation does not create a transaction | Express no obligation to proceed | Disclosure creates work, purchase, exclusivity, or reliance duty | Commercial/legal review |
| Accuracy and warranty | No implied information warranty unless approved | Information provided as available for evaluation | Firm warrants completeness or fitness of third-party information | Legal review |
| Return or deletion | Duties are feasible and respect preservation | Return or delete on request, subject to backups, law, professional records, and preservation | Immediate deletion conflicts with evidence, backup, legal, or contractual needs | Records/legal review |
| Backup copies | Ordinary backups are addressed | Inaccessible backups remain protected until overwritten | Absolute deletion duty cannot be performed | Technical/legal review |
| Professional records | Required records may be preserved | One archival copy or required records retained under protection | Agreement prohibits preservation of required evidence | Records/legal review |
| Term | Disclosure period is clear | Defined term appropriate to purpose | Indefinite disclosure duty creates uncontrolled relationship | Commercial/legal review |
| Survival | Duration matches information type | Defined period, with longer trade-secret treatment as legally appropriate | All information is protected forever without differentiation | Legal review |
| Trade secrets | Protection continues while legally qualifying | Separate trade-secret treatment | Agreement creates unsupported perpetual status | Legal review |
| Personal or regulated information | Separate duties are identified | Excluded from narrow pilot unless separately approved | NDA is treated as sufficient for regulated data, PHI, or privacy duties | Privacy/legal review |
| Privileged information | Privilege is not assumed protected by NDA alone | Exclude unless counsel directs controlled sharing | Client proposes privileged disclosure without counsel | Counsel review |
| Export or restricted information | Special restrictions are identified | Excluded unless separately approved | Controlled, classified, export-restricted, or prohibited information is proposed | Legal/regulatory review |
| Publicity | Relationship and names are protected | No publicity without written consent | Party may announce relationship or use marks unilaterally | Brand/legal review |
| Reverse engineering | Applies only when relevant | Narrow technical-evaluation restriction if approved | Broad restriction affects ordinary advisory work | Legal review |
| Non-solicitation | Separate from confidentiality | Omit unless independently justified | NDA contains broad employment, client, or market restriction | Legal review |
| Non-circumvention | Avoided unless specifically justified | Omit | Broad commercial restriction appears in NDA | Legal/commercial review |
| Remedies | Remedies are balanced and subject to law | Injunctive-relief language reviewed by counsel | Automatic damages, penalties, waiver, or one-sided relief | Legal review |
| Indemnity | Not embedded casually | Omit or separately negotiate | Broad indemnity applies to any disclosure or representative act | Legal/insurance review |
| Liability | Exposure is bounded | Governed by MSA or appropriate limitation | NDA creates uncapped or inconsistent liability | Legal/insurance review |
| Governing law | Jurisdiction is understood | Illinois or another approved jurisdiction | Unfamiliar or high-risk law | Qualified legal review |
| Venue | Forum is practical | Illinois or another approved forum | Distant or burdensome exclusive venue | Qualified legal review |
| Disputes | Process is understood | Consistent with governing agreement | NDA conflicts with MSA dispute terms | Legal review |
| Assignment | Transfer is controlled | Consent or approved transaction exception | Duties can be assigned to unknown higher-risk party | Legal review |
| Entire agreement | Relationship to MSA is clear | NDA controls confidentiality until expressly superseded | Conflicting confidentiality duties remain | Final document-set review |
| Order of precedence | Conflicts are resolved | Approved MSA terms coordinate or supersede expressly | Multiple inconsistent terms apply | Legal review |
| Amendment | Changes require written authority | Signed written amendment | Informal or unilateral amendment binds | Authority review |
| E-signature | Execution is valid and traceable | Electronic signatures accepted | Identity or authority is unreliable | Signature evidence |
| Records | Agreement and approvals are preserved | Final signed version, review, disclosure decision, and termination remain recoverable | Authoritative source is unclear or unstable | Records evidence |

## 4. Narrow Advisory Pilot Information Boundary

For the Narrow Advisory Pilot Service:

1. The agreement does not expand the approved information boundary.
2. Regulated, privileged, highly sensitive, security-sensitive, credential, secret, or unnecessary personal information remains excluded unless separately reviewed and approved.
3. No third party participates in delivery.
4. Need-to-know access remains limited to the owner and authorized client participants.
5. Client information shall not be placed in the public repository.
6. The NDA does not replace a DPA, BAA, security addendum, privilege protocol, or other required instrument.
7. No disclosure occurs until authority and purpose are clear.

## 5. Mandatory Escalation

Qualified review is required when:

1. Regulated or privileged information is involved.
2. Security duties exceed reasonable administrative safeguards.
3. Return or deletion conflicts with preservation or backup.
4. Residuals, IP, publicity, restrictive terms, indemnity, liability, or remedies are present.
5. Governing law, venue, or dispute terms require interpretation.
6. The NDA conflicts with an MSA, SOW, client policy, or other obligation.
7. A third party will receive information.
8. Disclosure authority is unclear.
9. Another condition creates material legal, confidentiality, commercial, privacy, security, evidence, or reputational exposure.

## 6. Client-Specific Review Record

The non-public record shall identify:

1. Agreement title and version.
2. Parties.
3. Authorized purpose.
4. Expected information.
5. Information exclusions.
6. Authorized recipients.
7. Review status by topic.
8. Requested changes.
9. Qualified-review evidence.
10. Final negotiated position.
11. Signature approval.
12. Disclosure authorization.
13. Authoritative signed agreement.
14. Term and survival.
15. Return, deletion, or preservation duties.
16. Residual risk.

## 7. Final Determination

Record one:

The determination does not authorize disclosure or engagement commencement.
