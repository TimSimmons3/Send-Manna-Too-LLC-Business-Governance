## 5. Service Determination

Approved service:

`Cybersecurity Governance Baseline and 90-Day Executive Action Roadmap`

The service is:

1. Owner operated.
2. Low complexity.
3. Time bounded.
4. Limited to one primary external deliverable.
5. Advisory and evidence limited.
6. Excluded from production system access and credential handling.
7. Bounded to low-sensitivity authorized information.
8. Third-party-free for delivery.
9. Subject to client-specific commercial and qualified review.
10. Subject to a separate DEC-0017 eligibility and start gate when used as the pilot.

## 6. Pricing Determination

Fixed fee is preferred when scope and assumptions are sufficiently clear.

The pricing standard establishes scope-to-price traceability, effort estimation, complexity and risk consideration, payment positions, discount control, change pricing, approval authority, confidential pricing handling, client-specific evidence, and qualified-review triggers.

No actual rate, margin, floor, negotiation position, or client amount is stored in the public repository.

## 7. Confidential Rate Card Determination

An actual rate card is not created in the public repository.

Before actual values are created, Send Manna Too LLC must approve and validate:

1. A non-public authoritative location.
2. Restricted access.
3. Version and approval control.
4. Backup and recovery.
5. Confidentiality marking.
6. Pricing evidence.
7. Supersession and review.
8. Prevention of public exposure.

This is a confidentiality and commercial control, not a missing governing method.

## 8. Standalone SLA Determination

A standalone SLA is not required for the approved service.

The service is fixed scope, time bounded, advisory, and does not provide uptime, continuous monitoring, incident response, recurring support, service credits, or measurable operational availability.

The SOW defines schedule, milestones, communications, review period, dependency treatment, acceptance, escalation, suspension, and termination.

A future recurring or operational service requires separate scope and may require a separately approved SLA.

## 9. Legal and Professional Review Boundary

Release 2.4 artifacts define business requirements and controlled review methods.

They do not provide legal advice, establish legal sufficiency, replace qualified counsel, approve professional conclusions, grant signature authority, create privilege, authorize disclosure, or authorize an engagement.

## 16. Executive and Red-Team Review

The review challenged whether the release was too broad, actual rates should be published, legal agreements should be drafted without counsel, an SLA was necessary, the service implied assurance, third parties could enter informally, client silence could be acceptance, templates could be mistaken for authority, sensitive information could enter Git, and DEC-0017 could be bypassed.

Controls limit the package to one service, prohibit actual pricing in Git, use business matrices instead of legal agreements, reject an SLA for this service, state advisory limits, prohibit third-party delivery, require signed-term support for acceptance, require client-specific approval, protect confidential records, and preserve the separate start gate.
