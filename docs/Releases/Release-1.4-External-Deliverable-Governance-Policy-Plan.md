# Release 1.4 - External Deliverable Governance Policy Plan

## Release Objective

Draft and approve the seventh controlled governance policy: External Deliverable Governance Policy.

## Source Path

`docs/Foundation/External-Deliverable-Governance-Policy.md`

## Release Type

Policy-only governance release.

## Planning Decision

The Release 0.7 planning record proposed `docs/Client-Engagement/External-Deliverable-Governance-Policy.md`.

Releases 0.8 through 1.3 established the operative convention that controlled governance policies are maintained under `docs/Foundation`.

Release 1.4 therefore selects `docs/Foundation/External-Deliverable-Governance-Policy.md` as the canonical source path. This decision preserves controlled-policy placement consistency, keeps the seven-policy governance set together, avoids a new domain-path exception, and does not change the frozen Release 0.1 governance architecture.

Publication artifacts, publication workflow changes, approval workflow automation, and external-deliverable tooling are deferred unless separately approved.

## Policy Boundary

Existing governance documents remain authoritative for engagement scope, commercial commitments, confidentiality, partner involvement, records and evidence, decision rights, document quality, and publication execution.

Release 1.4 will govern the decision boundary between internally prepared work product and an externally issued deliverable. It will establish external-release authorization, recipient and reliance controls, final-status governance, post-approval change control, correction, withdrawal, replacement, distribution evidence, and related escalation and exceptions.

Release 1.4 will reference rather than duplicate the Client Engagement Governance Policy deliverable baseline, Document Quality Standard review requirements, Publication and Export Procedure execution steps, confidentiality rules, commercial controls, records lifecycle requirements, and third-party governance requirements.

## In Scope

- External deliverable governance principles
- Deliverable purpose, intended audience, recipient, and permitted reliance
- Deliverable ownership and release accountability
- Scope, engagement, authority, and client-obligation alignment
- Evidence that applicable quality and source-validation reviews were completed
- Evidence that applicable confidentiality and sensitive-information reviews were completed
- Evidence that applicable legal, commercial, partner, records, and publication reviews were completed
- Assumption, limitation, dependency, and residual-risk disclosure governance
- Internal review and approval for external release
- Draft, review, final, superseded, withdrawn, corrected, and replaced status
- Version and source traceability
- Publication-readiness confirmation without duplicating publication procedures
- Authorized distribution and recipient restrictions
- Redistribution and reliance limitations where applicable
- Post-approval deliverable changes
- Correction, withdrawal, replacement, and recipient notification
- Client acceptance or acknowledgment where applicable
- External-deliverable issuance, approval, and distribution evidence
- External-deliverable risk escalation
- External-deliverable exceptions and noncompliance
- Relationships to existing governance policies, standards, procedures, checklists, templates, and records

## Out of Scope

Release 1.4 will not create:

- Word, PDF, or PowerPoint publication artifacts
- Publication templates or template changes
- Export-tool instructions
- Detailed formatting instructions already controlled by publication requirements
- Duplicate Document Quality Standard requirements
- Duplicate Publication and Export Procedure steps
- Duplicate Publication Artifact Validation Checklist controls
- Duplicate Client Engagement Governance Policy deliverable requirements
- Client engagement intake, qualification, scope, change, or closeout procedures
- Contract templates, legal clauses, or legal advice
- Pricing, proposal, contracting, or signature procedures
- New confidentiality classifications or technical information-handling controls
- Third-party onboarding, assessment, or procurement procedures
- Records retention schedules or fixed retention periods
- Technical cybersecurity controls
- Data-classification technical standards
- Detailed evidence collection checklists
- Detailed approval workflow automation
- Electronic signature tooling
- Document-management or delivery-platform software requirements
- Changes to the frozen Release 0.1 governance architecture
- Release automation tooling

## Drafting Guardrails

- Maintain the frozen Release 0.1 governance architecture.
- Keep Markdown in Git as the canonical source.
- Create one policy only.
- Use `docs/Foundation/External-Deliverable-Governance-Policy.md` as the canonical source path.
- Keep the policy governance-level.
- Preserve the Governance and Operating Authority Policy as the authority for decision rights, approvals, exceptions, and conflict resolution.
- Preserve the Client Engagement Governance Policy as the authority for engagement scope, general deliverable governance, client communications, acceptance, and closeout.
- Preserve the Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy as the authority for obligations, commitments, signature authority, and commercial risk.
- Preserve the Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy as the authority for information sharing, disclosure, need-to-know access, and confidentiality.
- Preserve the Third-Party and Partner Governance Policy as the authority for partner participation, partner roles, partner-related information sharing, and partner risk.
- Preserve the Records Evidence and Retention Policy as the authority for record status, evidence sufficiency, provenance, traceability, retention, preservation, and disposition.
- Preserve the Document Quality Standard as the authority for content quality, source quality, assumptions, limitations, residual risk, review gates, and acceptance criteria.
- Preserve the Publication and Export Procedure as the authority for publication preparation, formatting validation, export, traceability recording, storage, and operational distribution steps.
- Preserve the Publication Artifact Validation Checklist as the authority for format-specific publication artifact validation.
- Do not repeat the general deliverable checklist in Section 9 of the Client Engagement Governance Policy.
- Govern the external-release decision and post-issuance lifecycle rather than publication mechanics.
- Distinguish approval to create, approval to publish, and approval to release externally.
- Distinguish final status from file existence, formatting, client possession, or informal approval.
- Require proportionate controls based on purpose, audience, sensitivity, reliance, obligation, and risk.
- Avoid unsupported guarantees, legal conclusions, commercial promises, and implied acceptance.
- Do not create publication artifacts unless separately approved after the Markdown policy is approved.
