## 1. Purpose

This plan governs Release 2.5 of the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

Release 2.5 will create the authoritative rebaseline of known outstanding current, future, deferred, event-triggered, evidence-triggered, and prerequisite work after completion of Release 2.4.

The release will convert post-Release 2.4 owner direction, existing framework records, prior release evidence, and newly identified commercial, corporate, legal, insurance, private-record, vendor-channel, and pilot dependencies into one controlled and sequenced roadmap.

Release 2.5 is planning and disposition work only.

It will not implement confidential pricing, private financial models, vendor-channel standards, client or vendor legal agreements, external demonstrations, vendor onboarding, client delivery, or a real pilot.

## 2. Release Objective

Create and validate one authoritative outstanding-work baseline that:

1. Identifies every known unresolved or future item supported by current evidence.
2. Assigns each item one unique identifier.
3. Records the source and current status of each item.
4. Assigns each item exactly one controlled disposition.
5. Separates release work from non-release prerequisites and event-triggered execution.
6. Maps dependencies and sequencing.
7. Separates public governance artifacts from private commercial, legal, financial, corporate, insurance, and client or vendor records.
8. Establishes a controlled release roadmap through initial real operating pilots and evidence-based remediation.
9. Prevents a Big Bang release.
10. Preserves one active release at a time and one principal control domain per release.
11. Prevents premature claims of legal approval, compliance, scalability, or operating effectiveness.
12. Provides a defensible basis for opening Release 2.6 only after Release 2.5 is fully closed.

## 3. Change-in-Direction and Authority Basis

Release 2.4 closed with the next authorized activity characterized as engagement-triggered and stated that no repository change was required merely to await a qualifying engagement.

After Release 2.4 closeout, the owner identified additional material planning needs involving:

1. Private commercial operations.
2. Confidential pricing and profitability controls.
3. Corporate-record remediation.
4. Insurance readiness.
5. Vendor referral, demonstration, and sales-closure opportunities.
6. Commercial independence and conflict controls.
7. Counsel-ready client contract baselines.
8. Counsel-ready vendor-channel agreement baselines.
9. Integrated mock validation before external execution.
10. Separate real vendor and client pilots.
11. Evidence-based remediation before expansion.
12. Controlled service-ladder expansion.

The owner then directed that these items be divided into smaller, sequenced releases rather than handled through one large implementation effort.

Release 2.5 is the first canonical Git record of that post-Release 2.4 direction.

Release 2.5 shall not:

1. Retroactively change Release 2.4 evidence.
2. Reopen Release 2.4 deliverables.
3. Invalidate DEC-0018.
4. Weaken DEC-0017.

7. Treat the prior engagement-triggered conclusion as defective.

Release 2.5 may supersede the prior next-activity sequence only through a new, explicit, evidence-supported roadmap decision approved during this release.

## 8. Planning Principles

Release 2.5 shall apply the following principles:

1. One active release at a time.
2. One principal control domain per release.
3. Plan before implementation.
4. Evidence before expansion.
5. Public and private artifacts remain separately controlled.
6. Confidential commercial and legal records remain outside public Git.
7. Real client and vendor execution remain separately gated.
8. A design release shall not claim operating effectiveness.
9. A counsel-ready artifact shall not be called counsel-reviewed.
10. A pilot shall not be called scalable without validated evidence.
11. No release shall claim full compliance without opportunity-specific applicability review.
12. Future work shall be pulled forward only by a documented dependency, current opportunity, or operating-evidence need.
13. Existing completed work shall not be reopened without specific defect evidence.
14. Every outstanding item shall receive exactly one disposition.
15. Duplicate or overlapping work shall be consolidated without losing source traceability.
16. Rejected work shall retain the rationale and authority for rejection.
17. Deferred work shall retain a trigger or reconsideration condition when known.

## 14. Controlled Disposition Model

Each outstanding item shall receive exactly one of the following dispositions:

1. Completed.
2. Non-release prerequisite.
3. Assigned to a named release.
4. Engagement-triggered.
5. Vendor-triggered.
6. Evidence-triggered.
7. Deferred.
8. Rejected.

The following rules apply:

1. Completed items shall cite completion evidence.
2. Non-release prerequisites shall identify the external-execution gate they support.
3. Assigned items shall identify exactly one named release.
4. Engagement-triggered items shall identify the client event required.
5. Vendor-triggered items shall identify the vendor event required.
6. Evidence-triggered items shall identify the operating evidence required.
7. Deferred items shall state why they are postponed and when reconsideration may occur.
8. Rejected items shall state the decision rationale.
9. An item shall not have more than one disposition.
10. Cross-cutting dependencies shall be recorded as dependencies, not duplicate dispositions.
11. Items with mixed public and private outputs shall remain one work item only when one accountable outcome exists; otherwise they shall be separated.
12. Unresolved ambiguity shall prevent final approval of the affected item.
