## 2. Purpose

Release 1.9 will create one controlled Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure covering the engagement lifecycle from prospective-client qualification through authorization, execution governance, client acceptance, and closeout.

The procedure will provide a proportionate, auditable, and repeatable operating method while preserving existing policy authority and subject-matter boundaries.

Release 1.9 will not change substantive policy obligations, create new authority, create client-specific records, establish legal or commercial conclusions, or create reusable forms, checklists, registers, trackers, automated workflows, or training materials.

## 4. Planning Basis

The Release 1.5 completion review identified client engagement execution procedure and supporting evidence aids as a confirmed operationalization need after the cross-policy operating method.

Releases 1.7 and 1.8 completed and validated the cross-policy exception, escalation, noncompliance, and corrective-action procedure. The next supported priority is client engagement lifecycle operationalization.

The Client Engagement Governance Policy establishes requirements for:

- Qualification.
- Acceptance and authorization.
- Scope control.
- Assumptions and dependencies.
- Client communications.
- Deliverable governance.
- Engagement changes.
- Risk escalation.
- Client acceptance.
- Closeout.
- Required records.

The policy does not provide one complete execution sequence, lifecycle-state model, minimum decision gates, role handoffs, or consolidated closeout method. Release 1.9 will address that operating-method gap without duplicating policy text or creating unsupported aids.

## 10. Lifecycle and Role Requirements

The procedure may use proportionate states such as:

- Prospective.
- Under qualification.
- Deferred.
- Rejected.
- Accepted pending authorization.
- Authorized.
- Active.
- On hold.
- Pending client review or acceptance.
- Closing.
- Closed.
- Terminated.
- Superseded.

No specific software platform, numbering syntax, register, or workflow tool shall be required. Status alone shall not prove authorization, acceptance, or closure.

Role-based responsibilities may include:

- Engagement owner.
- Qualification reviewer.
- Decision or approving authority.
- Delivery owner.
- Client communication owner.
- Deliverable owner.
- Change decision authority.
- Escalation recipient.
- Acceptance or closeout reviewer.
- Records owner when distinct.

One person may perform multiple roles for a low-complexity engagement when authority remains appropriate and the record remains clear.

## 11. Policy and Procedure Boundaries

### 11.1 Cross-Policy Matters

The client engagement procedure shall not duplicate the complete exception, escalation, noncompliance, or corrective-action lifecycle.

Requested departures, completed departures, material uncertainty, corrective action, extension, expiration, reopening, recurrence, and root-cause review shall route to:

`docs/Procedures/Cross-Policy-Exception-Escalation-Noncompliance-and-Corrective-Action-Procedure.md`

The client engagement procedure may identify engagement-specific triggers, pause conditions, owners, context, and handoff evidence.

### 11.2 External Deliverables

The client engagement procedure may coordinate expected deliverables, ownership, scope alignment, timing, client review, acceptance dependencies, and completion evidence.

It shall not replace external-release authority, final-status determination, recipient authorization, publication validation, distribution restrictions, issuance evidence, correction, withdrawal, replacement, recipient notification, or post-issuance reliance control.

### 11.3 Commercial and Contracting

The procedure shall not create or approve pricing, contract terms, statements of work, signature authority, warranties, guarantees, concessions, payment terms, remedies, or binding commitments.

Commercial matters shall remain governed by the Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy and applicable agreements.

### 11.4 Information, Third Parties, and Records

The procedure shall preserve:

- Confidentiality, need-to-know, disclosure, minimization, and handling requirements.
- Third-party qualification, authorization, role, conflict, conduct, and review requirements.
- Record status, evidence sufficiency, integrity, provenance, preservation, protection, recoverability, retention, and disposition principles.

Release 1.9 shall not create a technical information-classification standard, detailed retention schedule, third-party procedure, or technical security or privacy control.

## 12. Validation Scenario Rules

All scenarios shall be:

- Fictional.
- Non-client-specific.
- Free of real client, employee, partner, vendor, secret, credential, or regulated personal information.
- Clearly identified as tests.
- Limited to lifecycle and operating-path validation.
- Maintained as controlled release evidence rather than operational records.

Scenario content shall distinguish facts, assumptions, unresolved questions, required decisions, recommendations, and residual risk.

## 13. Required Validation Scenario Set

### 13.1 Unclear Fit and Capacity

Test a prospective engagement with unclear services, resources, timing, and expectations.

Required coverage:

- Intake and qualification.
- Capability and resource review.
- Risk and assumption review.
- Deferral or rejection.
- Reconsideration conditions.
- Evidence.

### 13.2 Accepted but Not Authorized

Test an acceptable engagement lacking sufficient written authorization or containing unresolved commercial and confidentiality constraints.

Required coverage:

- Acceptance versus authorization.
- Required approvals.
- Pause conditions.
- Commercial and confidentiality handoffs.
- Start prohibition.
- Activation evidence.

### 13.3 Informal Scope Expansion

Test an active engagement where a client informally requests material additional work.

Required coverage:

- Scope-baseline comparison.
- Material-change decision.
- Communication.
- Resource, schedule, and commercial impact.
- Approval before implementation.
- Updated assumptions and evidence.

### 13.4 Dependency Failure

Test an active engagement where client information or access becomes unavailable and could affect quality or timing.

Required coverage:

- Dependency monitoring.
- Impact assessment.
- Communication.
- Hold or continuation decision.
- Escalation and change control.
- Residual risk.

### 13.5 Deliverable With Unclear Release Authority

Test a completed deliverable with unresolved release authority, recipient, confidentiality, or reliance questions.

Required coverage:

- Engagement-level coordination.
- External-deliverable handoff.
- Release hold.
- Review and approval.
- Recipient and reliance clarification.
- Publication-validation dependency.
- No inferred approval.

### 13.6 Closeout With Unresolved Follow-Up

Test an engagement where core work is complete but acceptance, an open issue, and requested follow-up remain unresolved.

Required coverage:

- Acceptance or acknowledgment.
- Open-item disposition.
- Closeout criteria and evidence.
- Follow-up-work separation.
- Escalation.
- Closure approval and residual risk.
