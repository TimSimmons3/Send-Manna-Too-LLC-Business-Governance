## 8. Lifecycle Design Conclusion

The approved procedure uses thirteen proportionate states:

1. Prospective.
2. Under qualification.
3. Deferred.
4. Rejected.
5. Accepted pending authorization.
6. Authorized.
7. Active.
8. On hold.
9. Pending client review or acceptance.
10. Closing.
11. Closed.
12. Terminated.
13. Superseded.

The state model is sufficient to distinguish:

- Opportunity from qualified work.
- Acceptance from authorization.
- Authorization from active delivery.
- Active work from hold conditions.
- Delivery completion from client acceptance.
- Closing activity from verified closure.
- Normal closure from termination or supersession.

No software, tracker, identifier syntax, or workflow platform is mandated.

## 9. Required Decision Gates

The approved procedure defines the following gates:

| Gate | Required conclusion |
| --- | --- |
| Intake gate | The prospective client, request, source, initial owner, and material open questions are identifiable. |
| Qualification gate | Fit, capability, capacity, independence, risk, expectations, constraints, and information sufficiency are assessed. |
| Acceptance gate | The engagement is accepted pending authorization, rejected, or deferred through appropriate authority. |
| Authorization gate | Scope, approvals, commercial status, confidentiality, partner involvement, owners, dependencies, and evidence are sufficient to authorize work. |
| Initiation gate | The current scope, owners, communications, access, dependencies, deliverables, risks, and records method are ready for active work. |
| Material-change gate | Proposed changes are compared to the baseline and approved before implementation. |
| External-release handoff | Engagement readiness is established, but external release remains governed by the External Deliverable Governance Policy. |
| Acceptance gate | The required client review, acknowledgment, acceptance, or documented alternative is evidenced. |
| Closeout gate | Deliverables, open items, follow-up, obligations, records, residual risk, and reviewer approval are complete or dispositioned. |

## 10. Authority and Boundary Conclusions

### 10.1 Governance Authority

The approved procedure does not create authority. It assigns responsibilities only within authority established by approved policy, agreement, or business approval.

### 10.2 Commercial and Contracting

The approved procedure does not create pricing, terms, signature authority, payment obligations, warranties, guarantees, remedies, or binding commitments.

### 10.3 Information Handling and Confidentiality

The approved procedure requires authorized use, need-to-know, limited sharing, controlled disclosure, and confidentiality escalation without creating a technical data-classification standard.

### 10.4 Third Parties and Partners

The approved procedure preserves partner qualification, authorization, role clarity, information boundaries, client-facing involvement, conflict review, conduct, and risk controls under the Third-Party and Partner Governance Policy.

### 10.5 Records and Evidence

The approved procedure identifies engagement-specific records. Retention, preservation, protection, recoverability, review, and disposition remain governed by the Records Evidence and Retention Policy.

### 10.6 External Deliverables

The approved procedure coordinates deliverable purpose, ownership, scope alignment, evidence, review, timing, and acceptance dependencies.

It does not grant release authority or replace final-status, recipient, distribution, publication, issuance, correction, withdrawal, replacement, notification, or post-issuance controls.

### 10.7 Cross-Policy Matters

Exceptions, completed departures, noncompliance, escalated issues, corrective action, review, expiration, reopening, recurrence, and root-cause review route to the approved cross-policy procedure.

## 11. Fictional Scenario Validation

All scenarios are fictional, non-client-specific, and contain no real client, employee, partner, vendor, credential, secret, or regulated personal information.

### 11.1 Scenario CE-01 - Unclear Fit and Capacity

Facts:

- A prospect requests an accelerated advisory engagement.
- The requested work is generally within the firm's service area.
- Required specialist capacity is not confirmed.
- The prospect expects a start before the capacity question can be resolved.

Procedure result:

- Current state: Under qualification.
- Decision: Defer.
- Authority: Appropriate business decision authority.
- Pause condition: No start date or delivery commitment.
- Evidence: Intake, capability review, capacity constraint, client communication, and review trigger.
- Residual risk: The prospect may seek another provider.

Conclusion:

The procedure prevents premature acceptance and unsupported delivery promises without requiring a form or tracker.

### 11.2 Scenario CE-02 - Accepted but Not Authorized

Facts:

- The firm decides the opportunity is an appropriate fit.
- A draft statement of work exists.
- Pricing and signature approval remain incomplete.
- The client asks the firm to begin substantive work.

Procedure result:

- Current state: Accepted pending authorization.
- Decision: Do not begin affected work.
- Authority: Commercial, signature, and business approval remain required.
- Handoff: Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy.
- Evidence: Acceptance decision, incomplete authorization conditions, client communication, and release of the hold only after approval.
- Residual risk: Schedule pressure.

Conclusion:

The procedure clearly distinguishes acceptance from authorization and blocks unauthorized work.

### 11.3 Scenario CE-03 - Informal Scope Expansion

Facts:

- An active client asks during a meeting for a material additional analysis.
- The request would add a deliverable, specialist effort, and two weeks.
- No change approval exists.

Procedure result:

- Current state: Active, with the requested addition not authorized.
- Decision: Continue only the current approved scope.
- Required review: Baseline comparison, resource and timing impact, commercial review, and change authority.
- Communication: Confirm that the request is under change review.
- Evidence: Request, impact assessment, decision, updated scope if approved.
- Residual risk: Client expectation mismatch.

Conclusion:

The procedure controls informal expansion and prevents meeting discussion from becoming an approved commitment.

### 11.4 Scenario CE-04 - Dependency Failure

Facts:

- Client-provided evidence and system access are delayed.
- Continuing would require unsupported assumptions and could affect quality and timing.

Procedure result:

- Current state: On hold for the affected work.
- Decision: Assess impact and communicate the dependency failure.
- Required review: Scope, schedule, quality, assumptions, change, and escalation.
- Evidence: Dependency status, impact, client communication, decision, and residual risk.
- Residual risk: Delivery delay and reduced evidence sufficiency.

Conclusion:

The procedure provides a proportionate hold and change path without defining fixed thresholds.

### 11.5 Scenario CE-05 - Deliverable With Unclear Release Authority

Facts:

- A deliverable is complete and formatted.
- The intended recipient and reliance purpose are unclear.
- Confidentiality and release authority are unresolved.

Procedure result:

- Current state: Active or Pending client review or acceptance, depending on the authorized purpose.
- Decision: Maintain release hold.
- Handoff: External Deliverable Governance Policy, Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy, and Publication and Export Procedure.
- Evidence: Intended purpose, recipient, reliance, scope, authority, confidentiality, review, and publication status.
- Residual risk: Schedule pressure and mistaken final-status representation.

Conclusion:

The procedure coordinates engagement readiness while preserving external-release authority.

### 11.6 Scenario CE-06 - Closeout With Unresolved Follow-Up

Facts:

- Core authorized work is complete.
- Client acceptance is not yet evidenced.
- One material issue remains open.
- The client requests separate additional work.

Procedure result:

- Current state: Closing.
- Decision: Do not close until acceptance and the open issue are resolved or appropriately dispositioned.
- Follow-up: Separate the additional work for qualification and authorization.
- Evidence: Completion evidence, acceptance status, open-item owner, follow-up disposition, residual risk, and closeout review.
- Residual risk: Delayed closure and possible disagreement over completion.

Conclusion:

The procedure prevents premature closure and uncontrolled continuation of new work.
