## 5. Validation Method

Each Release 2.1 scenario:

- Uses fictional and non-client-specific facts.
- Is paired with one Release 2.0 scenario family.
- Differs materially from its comparator in at least four independence dimensions.
- Preserves the primary control question.
- Records facts, assumptions, unresolved questions, status, state, roles, authority, purpose, recipient, intended use, reliance, restrictions, reviews, gates, decisions, evidence, closure, reopening, residual risk, and findings.
- Applies the nine approved deliverable statuses.
- Applies the twelve approved operating-process states.
- Applies the eight approved control gates.
- Preserves the distinction among authority to create, publish, and release.
- Preserves cross-policy boundaries.
- Does not force an outcome to match the comparator.
- Classifies the result based on evidence.

Consistency classifications:

- Materially consistent.
- Materially consistent with proportionate variation.
- Inconsistent but supportable after clarification.
- Materially inconsistent.
- Unable to determine.

Finding classifications:

- Confirmed policy defect.
- Confirmed procedure defect.
- Operating ambiguity.
- Boundary ambiguity.
- Documentation clarification.
- Execution friction.
- Training need.
- Execution-aid candidate.
- No issue.
