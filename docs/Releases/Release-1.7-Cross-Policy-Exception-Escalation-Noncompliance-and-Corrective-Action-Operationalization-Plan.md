## 2. Purpose

Release 1.7 will determine, define, implement, and validate one practical operating procedure that makes the seven approved core policies consistently executable when an exception, escalation, noncompliance condition, or corrective action occurs.

The release will operationalize existing policy requirements without changing their substantive obligations, scope boundaries, authority, review cycles, or precedence.

## 3. Planning Basis

Release 1.6 completed the administrative normalization of the seven approved core policies and confirmed that their substantive bodies remained unchanged.

A read-only cross-policy inventory was then completed against the seven canonical policy files.

Inventory execution evidence:

- Repository branch: `main`
- Baseline commit: `5220744`
- Canonical policies reviewed: `7`
- Inventory line count: `1157`
- Inventory SHA-256: `996f9cc0c61866025c6e8646638ae1dd0d3c944a54bf496951d246637e856e66`
- Repository status after inventory: unchanged and clean
- Filename-level duplicate-artifact search: no matching result

The inventory identified related requirements throughout all seven policies but no single operating procedure that defines one consistent execution method.

## 4. Cross-Policy Findings

The inventory supports the following conclusions:

1. All seven policies contain noncompliance requirements.
2. All seven policies reference remediation, corrective action, exception evidence, or another controlled response.
3. Six policies contain an explicit exception section or exception-handling section.
4. The Client Engagement Governance Policy contains escalation and noncompliance requirements but no dedicated exception section.
5. Six policies contain an explicit risk-escalation section.
6. The Governance and Operating Authority Policy establishes central decision rights and exception authority but does not provide a complete end-to-end operating workflow.
7. Exception-record fields vary by policy.
8. Review dates, expiration dates, duration, and closure conditions are not expressed consistently.
9. Corrective-action ownership, due dates, progress status, closure evidence, and closure verification are not consistently defined.
10. Recurrence and root-cause review are not consistently required across the policy set.
11. Evidence expectations exist, but there is no single operational record model connecting intake, assessment, approval, action tracking, review, extension, closure, and recurrence analysis.
12. Repeating a full workflow inside every policy would increase duplicate-guidance and inconsistency risk.

## 5. Need Determination

A common operating procedure is needed.

The procedure is justified because the approved policies establish what must be controlled, but they do not collectively define one complete and consistent method for execution.

The procedure will serve as an implementation layer beneath the policies. It will not replace, amend, narrow, or expand any policy.

## 7. Primary Release Objective

Create one proportionate, auditable, and non-duplicative operating procedure that defines how Send Manna Too LLC will:

- Identify and record an exception, escalation, or noncompliance condition.
- Distinguish an exception request from existing noncompliance.
- Assess impact, materiality, urgency, and affected obligations.
- Assign an accountable owner.
- Identify required decision and approval authority.
- Escalate material or uncertain matters.
- Record rationale, accepted risk, and compensating controls.
- Establish effective, review, expiration, extension, and closure dates when applicable.
- Define and track corrective actions.
- Preserve evidence and cross-references.
- Verify closure.
- Review recurrence and root cause when warranted.
- Identify possible governance, procedure, template, training, or tooling improvements.

## 8. Planned Minimum Procedure Scope

The procedure shall address the following lifecycle.

### 8.1 Intake and Classification

The procedure shall define how to identify and classify:

- Exception request.
- Approved exception.
- Expired exception.
- Noncompliance.
- Escalated issue.
- Corrective action.
- Recurring issue.
- Closed matter.
- Reopened matter.

### 8.2 Initial Assessment

The procedure shall define a proportionate assessment of:

- Requirement affected.
- Policy or controlled document affected.
- Client, engagement, contract, partner, record, deliverable, or business activity affected.
- Known facts.
- Assumptions and unresolved questions.
- Actual or potential impact.
- Urgency.
- Materiality.
- Legal, contractual, client, confidentiality, commercial, evidence, delivery, operational, or reputational implications.
- Need for qualified professional review.

### 8.3 Ownership and Authority

The procedure shall define:

- Matter owner.
- Corrective-action owner.
- Decision authority.
- Exception approving authority.
- Escalation recipient.
- Separation between preparation, recommendation, approval, and closure verification when proportionate.
- Conservative handling when authority is unclear.

### 8.4 Exception Decision

The procedure shall define minimum exception-record fields, including:

- Unique identifier.
- Date identified or requested.
- Requirement affected.
- Reason for the exception.
- Scope affected.
- Facts and assumptions.
- Risk created or accepted.
- Materiality assessment.
- Compensating controls or limitations.
- Matter owner.
- Approving authority.
- Approval decision.
- Effective date.
- Review or expiration date.
- Closure condition.
- Related evidence.
- Status.

### 8.5 Escalation

The procedure shall define escalation criteria and handling for:

- Unclear authority.
- Conflicting obligations.
- Potential legal or contractual exposure.
- Client trust or confidentiality impact.
- Commercial or financial exposure.
- Material delivery or scope impact.
- Evidence loss, integrity, or recoverability concern.
- Potential unauthorized disclosure.
- Unsupported commitment.
- Repeated noncompliance.
- Unresolved high-impact matter.
- Request to bypass approved governance.

### 8.6 Corrective Action

The procedure shall define minimum corrective-action fields, including:

- Action description.
- Action owner.
- Target completion date.
- Priority or urgency.
- Dependencies.
- Interim control.
- Progress status.
- Completion evidence.
- Residual risk.
- Closure reviewer.
- Closure decision and date.

### 8.7 Review, Extension, Expiration, and Closure

The procedure shall define:

- Periodic review expectations.
- Review before exception expiration.
- Extension approval and rationale.
- Handling of expired exceptions.
- Closure criteria.
- Independent or governance review when proportionate.
- Required closure evidence.
- Reopening conditions.
- Linkage to related decisions, releases, client communications, or corrected deliverables.

### 8.8 Recurrence and Root Cause

The procedure shall define when recurrence or root-cause review is warranted, including:

- Repeated exceptions involving the same requirement.
- Repeated noncompliance.
- Repeated corrective-action extensions.
- Similar issues across clients, engagements, partners, deliverables, or records.
- Evidence of unclear guidance, unrealistic requirements, insufficient training, weak templates, missing controls, or ineffective operating practices.

### 8.9 Records and Evidence

The procedure shall align with the Records Evidence and Retention Policy and define minimum traceability without creating a retention schedule.

Evidence may include:

- Intake record.
- Assessment.
- Approval or rejection.
- Escalation record.
- Corrective-action plan.
- Review record.
- Extension decision.
- Closure evidence.
- Recurrence or root-cause analysis.
- Related decision, release, contract, client communication, corrected deliverable, or other controlled record.

## 9. Materiality Approach

Release 1.7 may define an operational materiality assessment but shall not create legal conclusions, fixed financial thresholds, or domain-specific technical severity models.

The approach should consider:

- Scope and number of affected matters.
- Client or external-party impact.
- Contractual or legal implication.
- Confidentiality or information-handling impact.
- Commercial or financial exposure.
- Delivery and operational impact.
- Evidence integrity or recoverability.
- Duration.
- Detectability.
- Recurrence.
- Ability to reverse or contain the effect.
- Need for professional review.

The procedure shall require conservative escalation when materiality remains uncertain.

## 10. Cross-Policy Authority and Precedence

The procedure shall operate under the Constitution and the Governance and Operating Authority Policy.

The procedure shall preserve these rules:

- Higher-level governance controls.
- A more specific approved policy controls within its defined scope.
- A signed agreement or applicable legal obligation may create additional requirements.
- The procedure cannot authorize an exception to law, contractual obligation, client commitment, confidentiality requirement, preservation requirement, or the Constitution.
- The procedure cannot create authority that an approver does not otherwise possess.
- Unresolved conflicts require documentation and escalation.

## 11. Required No-Loss Mapping

Before the procedure is approved, implementation shall map its requirements to all seven policies and confirm:

- Every existing policy-specific exception field remains supported.
- Every existing escalation trigger remains supported.
- Every existing noncompliance response remains available.
- Existing evidence obligations remain supported.
- Existing review-cycle requirements remain unchanged.
- Existing policy precedence remains unchanged.
- No policy-specific prohibition is weakened.
- No new substantive policy obligation is introduced unintentionally.

## 18. Stop Conditions

Implementation shall stop for review if:

- A proposed procedure requirement would change a policy obligation.
- Approval authority cannot be reconciled with existing governance.
- A requirement conflicts with a signed agreement, legal obligation, or higher-level governance.
- A policy-specific requirement would be lost or weakened.
- Duplicate guidance cannot be resolved.
- The procedure expands into technical incident response, cybersecurity, privacy, employment, legal, contracting remedies, or another excluded domain.
- A template, tool, or tracking system becomes necessary beyond the approved boundary.
- The working tree becomes unexpectedly modified.
- Local and remote references are not synchronized at a required gate.

## 19. Success Criteria

Release 1.7 will be successful when:

- One approved procedure defines a complete cross-policy operating lifecycle.
- The procedure remains subordinate to and consistent with all seven policies.
- No substantive policy body is changed without separate approval.
- Exception and corrective-action records have clear minimum fields.
- Material matters have an identifiable escalation path.
- Ownership, approval, review, expiration, extension, closure, and evidence requirements are operationally clear.
- Recurring matters can trigger root-cause and governance-improvement review.
- Duplicate policy guidance is not created.
- The release is validated, documented, synchronized, tagged, backed up, checksum-validated, copied to iCloud, and recoverable.
