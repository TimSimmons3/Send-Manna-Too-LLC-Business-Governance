## 1. Purpose

This plan governs Release 2.4 of the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

Release 2.4 will establish the minimum controlled commercial-readiness package needed to pursue one narrowly scoped, owner-operated advisory engagement that may later qualify as the actual operating-use pilot authorized in principle by DEC-0017.

The release will translate existing approved commercial, contracting, client-engagement, external-deliverable, confidentiality, records, authority, exception, publication, and closeout requirements into a bounded set of reusable commercial execution aids.

Release 2.4 will not select a client, authorize an engagement, approve actual pricing, sign an agreement, begin client work, start the operating-use pilot, or claim that any legal instrument is legally sufficient without qualified review.

## 2. Release Objective

Create and validate a minimum commercial-readiness package for one low-complexity advisory service:

`Cybersecurity Governance Baseline and 90-Day Executive Action Roadmap`

The package shall enable Send Manna Too LLC to:

1. Describe the service consistently.
2. Bound scope, assumptions, dependencies, exclusions, and deliverables.
3. Develop and approve pricing through a controlled method.
4. Prepare a client proposal without unsupported promises.
5. Prepare a client-specific statement of work from an approved internal template.
6. Review client or firm contracting documents against approved business requirements.
7. Control scope changes.
8. Distinguish business approval from legal review and legal sufficiency.
9. Preserve confidential pricing and negotiation information outside the public framework repository.
10. Determine whether a standalone service-level agreement is necessary.
11. Preserve all DEC-0017 pilot boundaries.
12. Remain ready to perform a separate client-specific eligibility and start-gate decision when a real engagement exists.

## 6. Target Advisory Service Boundary

Release 2.4 shall design the commercial package around one proposed service:

`Cybersecurity Governance Baseline and 90-Day Executive Action Roadmap`

### 6.1 Intended Service Purpose

Provide a client with a bounded executive assessment of selected cybersecurity governance practices and a prioritized 90-day action roadmap for management decision support.

### 6.2 Intended Delivery Model

The service shall be designed for:

1. Owner-operated delivery.
2. One identified client or contracting party.
3. Low commercial and delivery complexity.
4. Limited duration.
5. One primary external deliverable.
6. No subcontractor, referral-partner, or external-collaborator delivery participation.
7. No technical system administration.
8. No production system access.
9. No production credentials, secrets, or privileged access.
10. Low-sensitivity, authorized, need-to-know information only.
11. Preservation-only records handling.
12. Defined pause, stop, escalation, and termination conditions.

### 6.3 Proposed External Deliverable

One executive-ready document containing:

1. Engagement purpose and scope.
2. Assessment basis and information limitations.
3. Current-state governance observations.
4. Prioritized risks or gaps.
5. Practical recommendations.
6. A 90-day management action roadmap.
7. Assumptions and dependencies.
8. Exclusions and limitations.
9. Intended use and reliance boundaries.
10. Residual risk and next-step considerations.

### 6.4 Prohibited Service Characteristics

The proposed service shall not include:

1. Legal advice or legal conclusions.
2. Regulatory certification.
3. Compliance attestation.
4. Audit opinion.
5. Penetration testing.
6. Vulnerability scanning.
7. Managed detection or monitoring.
8. Incident response retainer services.
9. Technical implementation.
10. Security-tool administration.
11. Production system access.
12. Credential handling.
13. Regulated, privileged, highly sensitive, or unnecessary personal information.
14. Guaranteed outcomes.
15. Unlimited support.
16. Third-party delivery participation.
17. A formal assurance opinion.
18. A representation that the client is secure, compliant, certified, or risk free.

## 7. Public Repository Confidentiality Boundary

The Business Governance Framework repository shall be treated as publicly visible unless a separately approved private repository or private controlled storage location is established and validated.

The following information shall not be committed to the public framework repository:

1. Actual internal billing rates.
2. Minimum acceptable rates.
3. Negotiation floors or ceilings.
4. Target margins.
5. Client-specific pricing.
6. Discount authority thresholds when disclosure would weaken negotiating position.
7. Bank, payment-account, or tax information.
8. Client-identifying commercial information.
9. Counsel communications.
10. Privileged legal analysis.
11. Insurance-policy details not approved for public disclosure.
12. Confidential contract negotiation strategy.
13. Signatures.
14. Personal information not required for governance evidence.

Public repository artifacts may define the governance method, required fields, approval controls, and non-confidential template structure.

Actual confidential pricing and negotiation records require a separately approved non-public storage location, access boundary, version-control method, backup method, and recovery method before creation.

2. Schedule.

## 22. Risks and Controls

| Risk | Control |
| --- | --- |
| Commercial-readiness work expands into an unsupported service catalog | Limit the release to one narrow advisory service |
| Reusable templates create apparent authority | Require client-specific business, legal, authority, and signature review |
| Actual rates are exposed in the public repository | Prohibit confidential rates and negotiation positions from Git |
| A business matrix is mistaken for legal advice | Mark matrices as business requirements only and require qualified counsel |
| A proposal is mistaken for an authorized engagement | State that proposal approval does not create engagement authorization |
| An internal SOW template is signed without review | Require client-specific review and approval before execution |
| Informal scope expansion occurs | Use the approved change-order method |
| Unsupported guarantees are made | Prohibit guarantees, certification, attestation, and unlimited commitments |
| A standalone SLA creates unnecessary obligations | Defer SLA unless recurring operational service characteristics require it |
| Client-specific work begins prematurely | Preserve the separate engagement and DEC-0017 pilot start gates |
| Lower-level artifacts duplicate policies | Use references and no-loss traceability rather than restating authority |
| Legal or privileged information is exposed | Prohibit counsel communications and privileged analysis from the public repository |
| Release 2.4 is treated as broad rollout | State that only minimum commercial readiness is approved |
| Production publication begins early | Preserve DEC-0011 |
