## Closeout Notes

The External Deliverable Governance Policy establishes the governing decision boundary between internally prepared work product and an externally issued deliverable.

The policy distinguishes authority to create content, generate a publication output, and release a deliverable externally. It also governs final status, authorized distribution, reliance restrictions, post-approval changes, correction, withdrawal, replacement, recipient notification, acceptance, evidence, escalation, and exceptions.

Existing governance documents continue to control engagement scope, commercial commitments, confidentiality, partner involvement, records and evidence, document quality, publication execution, and governance authority.

The policy is intentionally governance-level and does not replace signed agreements, client-specific obligations, legal advice, qualified professional review, publication procedures, technical controls, or professional judgment.
