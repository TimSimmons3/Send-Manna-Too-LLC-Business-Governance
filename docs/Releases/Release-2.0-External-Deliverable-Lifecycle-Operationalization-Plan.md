## 2. Purpose

Release 2.0 will create one controlled External Deliverable Lifecycle Procedure covering the normal operating lifecycle from release-request initiation through external issuance, client review or acceptance coordination, post-issuance control, and external-deliverable record closure.

The procedure will provide a proportionate, auditable, and repeatable operating method while preserving existing policy authority and the boundaries of the Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure, Publication and Export Procedure, Publication Artifact Validation Checklist, and Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure.

Release 2.0 will not change substantive policy obligations, create new authority, create client-specific records, establish legal or commercial conclusions, or create reusable forms, checklists, registers, trackers, automated workflows, ticketing requirements, or training materials.

## 4. Planning Basis

The External Deliverable Governance Policy establishes comprehensive requirements for deliverable status, intended purpose and use, ownership, release authority, pre-release review, final-status determination, source and version traceability, authorized distribution, post-approval changes, correction, withdrawal, replacement, recipient notification, acceptance or acknowledgment, records, escalation, exceptions, and noncompliance.

The Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure coordinates expected deliverables and establishes an explicit handoff to external-deliverable governance. It does not authorize external release or provide the complete external-deliverable lifecycle.

The Publication and Export Procedure governs publication-output preparation, formatting, validation, source traceability, storage, and distribution mechanics. It does not establish the external-release decision method, recipient authorization, reliance controls, or the complete post-issuance lifecycle.

The Publication Artifact Validation Checklist validates Word, PDF, and PowerPoint publication artifacts. It does not establish external-release authority or a complete external-deliverable operating sequence.

The Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure governs departures, material uncertainty, escalation, corrective action, closure, and recurrence. It preserves, rather than replaces, the normal domain-specific external-deliverable method.

The existing documents therefore contain the required authority and controls but require users to construct the normal operating sequence themselves. Release 2.0 will address that operating-method gap without duplicating policy text or creating unsupported execution aids.

- Corrected deliverable.

- Release not requested.

- Under pre-release review.

## 11. Policy and Procedure Boundaries

### 11.1 Client Engagement

The external-deliverable procedure may receive expected deliverable, purpose, scope, timing, source, review, acceptance, and closeout context from the Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure.

It shall not replace engagement qualification, authorization, scope baselining, delivery governance, material engagement change control, engagement acceptance, or engagement closeout.

External-deliverable record closure shall not by itself close an engagement.

### 11.2 Publication and Artifact Validation

The external-deliverable procedure may require publication readiness and applicable artifact validation before release.

It shall not duplicate the complete method for preparing, formatting, exporting, validating, tracing, storing, or producing Word, PDF, or PowerPoint publication outputs.

Publication readiness, formatting, branding, export, upload, or artifact validation shall not establish external-release authority.

### 11.3 Cross-Policy Matters

The external-deliverable procedure shall not duplicate the complete exception, escalation, noncompliance, corrective-action, extension, expiration, reopening, recurrence, or root-cause lifecycle.

Requested departures, completed departures, material uncertainty, corrective action, recurrence, and cross-policy authority conflicts shall route to:

`docs/Procedures/Cross-Policy-Exception-Escalation-Noncompliance-and-Corrective-Action-Procedure.md`

The external-deliverable procedure may identify deliverable-specific triggers, release holds, containment actions, owners, recipient impacts, and handoff evidence.

### 11.4 Commercial, Confidentiality, Third-Party, and Records Controls

The procedure shall preserve:

- Commercial terms, commitments, pricing, signature authority, remedies, warranties, guarantees, and contract interpretation under appropriate commercial and professional authority.
- Confidentiality, need-to-know, minimization, redaction, disclosure authorization, restricted use, and information handling requirements.
- Third-party qualification, authorization, role clarity, conflicts, client-facing involvement, conduct, and information-sharing requirements.
- Record status, evidence sufficiency, integrity, provenance, preservation, protection, recoverability, retention, review, and disposition principles.

Release 2.0 shall not create legal advice, contract interpretation, a technical information-classification standard, a retention schedule, a third-party procedure, or technical cybersecurity or privacy controls.

## 13. Required Validation Scenario Set

### 13.1 Routine Final Deliverable Release

Test a low-complexity deliverable with clear scope, source, recipient, purpose, approval authority, publication readiness, and delivery method.

Required coverage:

- Release request.
- Deliverable status.
- Purpose and intended use.
- Ownership and authority.
- Proportionate pre-release review.
- Final-status determination.
- Issuance and delivery evidence.
- Record closure.

### 13.2 Client Review Copy Versus Final Deliverable

Test material provided to a client for review and feedback where final status, permitted reliance, and redistribution could be misunderstood.

Required coverage:

- Client review-copy status.
- Purpose and reliance limitation.
- Marking and communication.
- Recipient authorization.
- No inferred final status.
- Review feedback handling.
- Transition to final status or closure without release.

### 13.3 Release Hold for Unresolved Authority or Recipient Risk

Test a completed deliverable with unresolved release authority, recipient authorization, confidentiality, commercial language, or third-party involvement.

Required coverage:

- Pre-release governance gate.
- Release hold.
- Required subject-matter handoffs.
- Escalation or exception routing.
- Resolution, limitation, or rejection.
- No release while material conditions remain unresolved.
- Decision evidence.

### 13.4 Material Change After Approval

Test an approved deliverable that changes materially before issuance or continued reliance.

Required coverage:

- Materiality assessment.
- Approval invalidation or limitation.
- Source and version control.
- Re-review and proportionate reauthorization.
- Updated recipient, reliance, and publication review.
- Traceability to the prior approved version.
- Final release decision.

### 13.5 Misdirected or Materially Defective Issued Deliverable

Test an issued deliverable sent to an incorrect recipient or later found to contain a material factual, scope, confidentiality, or reliance defect.

Required coverage:

- Immediate containment or reliance limitation.
- Cross-policy routing.
- Correction, withdrawal, supersession, or replacement decision.
- Recipient identification and notification.
- Evidence preservation.
- Residual risk.
- Closure or reopening criteria.

### 13.6 Acceptance and Closure With an Open Post-Issuance Issue

Test an issued deliverable where receipt occurred but acknowledgment or acceptance is unclear and a material post-issuance issue remains open.

Required coverage:

- Receipt, review, acknowledgment, and acceptance distinction.
- Engagement-record linkage.
- Open-item disposition.
- Client communication.
- Post-issuance action ownership.
- External-deliverable record closure criteria.
- No premature engagement or matter closure.

- No reusable aid or production publication artifact will be created.
- Any future aid or correction requires separately approved scope.
- No utility will be used without the Mandatory Utility Quality Gate.
- Closeout and recovery controls remain required.

## 29. Release Outcome Target

Release 2.0 should establish one practical, controlled, and auditable external-deliverable lifecycle operating method that reduces inferred approval, recipient error, uncontrolled reliance, weak issuance evidence, stale approval, incomplete post-issuance response, and premature closure.

The release should improve execution consistency without expanding the governance architecture, duplicating client engagement, publication, or cross-policy controls, creating unsupported tooling, or beginning production publication.
