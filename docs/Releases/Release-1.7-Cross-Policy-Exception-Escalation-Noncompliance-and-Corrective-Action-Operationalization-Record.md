### 6. Substantive-Change Assessment

- The candidate procedure defines method, records, statuses, roles, and review mechanics.
- The candidate does not amend any policy body.
- The candidate does not create new legal, contractual, technical, privacy, cybersecurity, employment, or retention requirements.
- The candidate does not delegate exception authority.
- The candidate preserves policy-specific responses and precedence.
- The routine, elevated, and material labels are operational aids and do not replace domain determinations.
- The recurrence section operationalizes existing recurrence-risk and root-cause expectations without changing policy review cycles.
