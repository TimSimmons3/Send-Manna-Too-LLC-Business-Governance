# Release 2.0 - External Deliverable Lifecycle Operationalization Closeout

## Release Summary

Release 2.0 established and adopted one controlled External Deliverable Lifecycle Procedure for external deliverables governed by the External Deliverable Governance Policy.

The release operationalized the external-deliverable lifecycle from release-request initiation through deliverable identification, pre-release review, final-status determination, release approval, issuance, client review or acceptance coordination, material-change control, post-issuance action, and external-deliverable record closure.

The procedure distinguishes deliverable status from operating-process state and preserves the distinction among authority to create, publish, and release.

Six fictional, non-client-specific scenarios validated the proposed lifecycle across routine final release, client review-copy status, unresolved authority and recipient risk, material change after approval, misdirected and materially defective issuance, and acceptance with an open post-issuance issue.

No substantive policy amendment, supporting-procedure amendment, reusable execution aid, production publication artifact, automated workflow, ticketing requirement, or training artifact was justified.

## Release Objective

Create one controlled operating procedure for executing the approved External Deliverable Governance Policy across the external-deliverable lifecycle while preserving existing governance authority and avoiding premature forms, trackers, checklists, workflows, or other execution aids.

## Read-Only Gap Review

A read-only review confirmed that the framework contained comprehensive external-deliverable authority and control requirements but no single normal-state operating sequence for:

- Release-request initiation.
- Deliverable identification and status confirmation.
- Purpose, audience, recipient, intended-use, reliance, and redistribution review.
- Deliverable ownership and release-authority identification.
- Pre-release governance coordination.
- Release holds and unresolved-condition handling.
- Final-status determination.
- External-release decisions.
- Controlled issuance and delivery evidence.
- Client receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance coordination.
- Material changes after approval.
- Post-issuance correction, withdrawal, supersession, replacement, reliance limitation, and recipient notification.
- External-deliverable record closure.

The Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure stopped at the external-release handoff.

The Publication and Export Procedure and Publication Artifact Validation Checklist controlled publication-output preparation and format validation but did not establish external-release authority.

The Cross-Policy Exception, Escalation, Noncompliance, and Corrective-Action Procedure controlled exceptions, escalation, noncompliance, corrective action, reopening, recurrence, and root-cause review but did not provide the normal external-deliverable lifecycle.

The gap review therefore supported one narrowly scoped external-deliverable operationalization release.

## Deliverable-Status Conclusion

The procedure preserves nine policy-defined deliverable statuses:

1. Internal work product.
2. Working draft.
3. Review draft.
4. Client review copy.
5. Final deliverable.
6. Superseded deliverable.
7. Withdrawn deliverable.
8. Corrected deliverable.
9. Replacement deliverable.

The status model is sufficient to distinguish:

- Internal from external-use material.
- Draft from review-ready material.
- Review-copy status from final status.
- Current final status from superseded or withdrawn status.
- Correction from replacement.
- Status change from process execution.

Deliverable status alone does not prove authority, approval, issuance, acceptance, or closure.

## Operating-Process State Conclusion

The procedure establishes twelve operating-process states:

1. Release not requested.
2. Release requested.
3. Under pre-release review.
4. On release hold.
5. Pending release decision.
6. Approved for release.
7. Not approved for release.
8. Issued.
9. Pending receipt, review, acknowledgment, or acceptance.
10. Post-issuance action open.
11. Ready for record closure.
12. Closed.

The state model is sufficient to distinguish:

- Existing work product from an active release request.
- Review from approval.
- Approval from issuance.
- Issuance from receipt or acceptance.
- Normal release from a hold.
- Completed delivery from open post-issuance action.
- Readiness for closure from verified closure.

No software, tracker, identifier syntax, workflow platform, or ticketing system is mandated.

## Authority and Boundary Conclusion

### Governance Authority

The procedure creates no new authority.

### Client Engagement

The Client Engagement Lifecycle Procedure remains authoritative for engagement qualification, authorization, scope, delivery governance, deliverable coordination, client acceptance, and engagement closeout.

The external-deliverable procedure receives the engagement handoff but does not replace engagement governance.

### Commercial and Contracting

The procedure creates no pricing, terms, signature authority, payment obligation, warranty, guarantee, remedy, certification, or binding commitment.

### Information Handling and Confidentiality

The procedure requires authorized information use, need-to-know access, minimization, controlled disclosure, recipient review, redaction, and confidentiality escalation without creating technical data-classification controls.

### Third Parties and Partners

The procedure preserves partner qualification, authorization, role clarity, information boundaries, client-facing involvement, attribution, conflicts, conduct, and risk controls.

### Records and Evidence

The procedure identifies external-deliverable evidence while preserving records retention, preservation, protection, recoverability, review, and disposition authority.

### Publication and Artifact Validation

The procedure preserves the Publication and Export Procedure and Publication Artifact Validation Checklist as authoritative for publication-output preparation and format validation.

Publication readiness does not grant external-release authority.

### Cross-Policy Matters
