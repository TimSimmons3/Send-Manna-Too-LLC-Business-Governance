## 13. Pilot Eligibility Boundary

A future pilot may begin only when all applicable approved conditions are satisfied.

The minimum conditions include:

1. Low complexity.
2. Limited duration.
3. Clearly identified client or contracting party.
4. Sufficiently understood requesting and approving parties.
5. Written authorization and scope evidence.
6. Identifiable expected deliverables.
7. Identifiable client and firm responsibilities.
8. Documented assumptions, dependencies, constraints, and exclusions.
9. Standard commercial terms or completed appropriate review.
10. No unresolved unusual liability, indemnity, warranty, guarantee, insurance, audit, security, regulatory, tax, or professional obligation.
11. No third-party delivery participation.
12. No regulated, privileged, highly sensitive, or unnecessary personal information requirement.
13. Authorized need-to-know information handling.
14. One clearly defined external deliverable.
15. Identifiable release authority, recipients, intended use, reliance, and redistribution conditions.
16. Identifiable client review, acknowledgment, acceptance, or completion expectations.
17. Identifiable and recoverable records.
18. No record disposition.
19. Ability to pause, escalate, or terminate.
20. No requirement for a reusable controlled execution aid.
21. No requirement for production publication of the complete framework.

## 14. Pilot Prohibited Characteristics

A future pilot shall not proceed when it requires or materially involves:

1. Unclear authority.
2. Highly negotiated or materially unusual terms without qualified review.
3. Regulated, privileged, highly sensitive, or security-sensitive information outside the approved boundary.
4. Third-party delivery involvement.
5. Professional conclusions outside authorized competence.
6. Guaranteed outcomes or unsupported promises.
7. Unbounded client reliance.
8. Record destruction, deletion, or disposition.
9. Ungoverned technical control operation.
10. Activity that cannot be paused safely.
11. Required reusable forms, trackers, software, automation, or training before control is possible.
12. Unmanaged legal, contractual, confidentiality, commercial, evidence, client-trust, or reputational exposure.

## 15. Pilot Stop and Escalation Conditions

A future pilot shall pause affected activity when:

1. Authorization is absent, withdrawn, inconsistent, or unclear.
2. Scope, deliverables, timing, responsibilities, pricing, or obligations materially change without approval.
3. Work or commitments are requested outside approved scope.
4. A deliverable cannot be supported by sufficient evidence.
5. Confidentiality, disclosure authority, need-to-know, or sensitivity is unclear.
6. Third-party involvement is proposed or discovered.
7. Material contract or professional-review needs arise.
8. Release authority, final status, recipient, reliance, redistribution, or acceptance conditions are unresolved.
9. A material error, misdirection, unauthorized release, obsolete version, or reliance concern is identified.
10. Required records are missing, altered, unrecoverable, duplicated without control, or maintained only in an unstable location.
11. Continued activity could materially increase exposure.
12. The matter exceeds the owner's approved authority or competence.
