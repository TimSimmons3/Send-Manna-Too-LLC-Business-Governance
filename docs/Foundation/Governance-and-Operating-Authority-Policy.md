## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the governance and operating authority model for Send Manna Too LLC.

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear decision rights, approval expectations, accountability, exception authority, and operating discipline for controlled governance activity.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to governance decisions, operating decisions, controlled documents, client-facing commitments, internal business records, and framework changes made under the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

This policy applies to all firm-controlled work unless a more specific approved policy, standard, procedure, client agreement, or legal obligation applies.

## 3. Policy Position

Send Manna Too LLC shall operate under a controlled governance model that protects business integrity, client trust, evidence quality, confidentiality, and decision accountability.

Governance authority shall be exercised deliberately, documented when material, and aligned to the approved document hierarchy.

No person, process, template, publication artifact, or informal practice may override the Constitution, approved policies, or the canonical Markdown source maintained in Git.

## 4. Governance Authority

Governance authority means the authority to approve, reject, defer, revise, or retire controlled governance content, operating expectations, and material business rules.

Governance authority includes authority over:

- Constitution interpretation.
- Policy approval.
- Standard approval.
- Procedure and playbook approval.
- Template and checklist approval.
- Material exception approval.
- Controlled release approval.
- Final acceptance of governance deliverables.

Governance authority shall remain accountable to the approved governance architecture and shall not be delegated informally when the decision affects controlled obligations, client-facing commitments, confidentiality, evidence, or business risk.

## 5. Operating Authority

Operating authority means the authority to perform day-to-day work within approved governance boundaries.

Operating authority may be used to create drafts, propose changes, perform validation, prepare publication artifacts, organize records, and execute approved procedures.

Operating authority does not include authority to bypass approval requirements, change the document hierarchy, approve policy exceptions, or treat publication artifacts as canonical sources.

## 6. Decision Rights

Decision rights shall be assigned based on the nature and impact of the decision.

| Decision Type | Required Authority | Evidence Expectation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Constitution change | Governance authority | Decision record and release record |
| New policy | Governance authority | Release plan, approved Markdown source, and closeout |
| Policy change | Governance authority | Diff, validation, approval, and closeout when material |
| Standard change | Governance authority or approved delegate | Diff, validation, and change record |
| Procedure change | Operating authority with governance review when material | Diff and validation evidence |
| Template change | Operating authority with governance review when material | Versioned artifact or source record |
| Client-facing commitment | Authorized business approval | Written approval or contract record |
| Exception to controlled guidance | Governance authority | Exception record with rationale and expiration or review date |

Decision rights shall be interpreted conservatively when impact, authority, or risk is unclear.

## 7. Approval Expectations

Approval is required before controlled governance content is treated as final, active, or authoritative.

Approval shall be based on sufficient review of purpose, scope, hierarchy alignment, duplicate guidance risk, evidence quality, confidentiality impact, and operational feasibility.

Approval shall not be assumed from draft creation, file existence, publication formatting, or informal discussion.

The approved Markdown source in Git is the authoritative source for controlled governance content.

## 8. Exception Handling

Exceptions to controlled governance requirements shall be limited, documented, risk-based, and time-bound when practical.

An exception shall identify:

- The requirement being excepted.
- The reason for the exception.
- The approving authority.
- The affected document, process, client, or business activity.
- The risk created by the exception.
- The compensating control, if any.
- The review date or closure condition, when practical.

Exceptions shall not be used to avoid the Constitution, bypass legal obligations, weaken confidentiality commitments, or create unmanaged client-facing risk.

Publication-specific exceptions shall continue to follow the approved publication procedure.

## 9. Controlled Operating Discipline

Controlled operating discipline means that governance work is planned, validated, documented, versioned, and closed out before it is treated as complete.

Controlled governance work shall follow the sequence: plan, implement, validate, document, synchronize, and close out.

Material changes shall be traceable through Git history, release records, decision records, or other approved evidence.

Work shall be divided into manageable releases to reduce scope creep, duplicate guidance, and approval ambiguity.

## 10. Relationship to the Constitution

The Constitution remains the highest internal governance authority within the Business Governance Framework.

This policy operates under the Constitution and shall be interpreted to support the Constitution.

If this policy conflicts with the Constitution, the Constitution controls unless the Constitution is formally amended through an approved governance release.

## 11. Relationship to Standards, Procedures, and Templates

Policies define required governance positions and operating expectations.

Standards define specific requirements that support policies.

Procedures and playbooks define approved methods for execution.

Templates, checklists, and scripts support repeatable execution but do not create authority beyond approved policies, standards, or procedures.

Publication artifacts do not replace the canonical Markdown source maintained in Git.

## 12. Client-Facing Authority

Client-facing commitments shall be made only with appropriate business authority and sufficient understanding of scope, assumptions, dependencies, confidentiality obligations, and delivery risk.

No uncontrolled draft, informal statement, template, or internal planning note shall be treated as a binding client-facing commitment unless approved through the appropriate business or contracting process.

Client-facing deliverables shall be reviewed for accuracy, completeness, confidentiality, and consistency with approved scope before release.

## 13. Records and Evidence

Material governance decisions shall be supported by records sufficient to show what was decided, who approved it, when it was approved, and what evidence supported the decision.

Acceptable evidence may include approved Markdown files, Git commits, release records, decision records, validation output, checksum records, backup records, and approved publication artifacts.

Evidence shall be retained in a manner that supports auditability, repeatability, and business continuity.

## 14. Conflict Resolution

When governance documents appear to conflict, the approved document hierarchy shall be used to resolve the conflict.

When two documents at the same hierarchy level appear to conflict, the more specific approved document controls for its defined scope unless governance authority determines otherwise.

Unresolved conflicts shall be documented and corrected through a controlled release or decision record.

## 15. Noncompliance

Failure to follow this policy may create business, contractual, confidentiality, quality, or evidence risk.

Identified noncompliance shall be reviewed based on impact, cause, corrective action, and recurrence risk.

Material noncompliance shall be documented and remediated through an approved corrective action, decision record, release update, or exception record.

## 16. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed when governance authority determines that business operations, client obligations, legal obligations, document hierarchy, or operating practices materially change.

Changes to this policy shall be made through controlled Markdown updates in Git and shall include validation appropriate to the change.
