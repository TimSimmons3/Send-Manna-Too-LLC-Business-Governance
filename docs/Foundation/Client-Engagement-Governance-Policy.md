## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the governance requirements for the lifecycle of client engagements conducted by Send Manna Too LLC.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that client engagements are qualified, authorized, scoped, communicated, delivered, changed, accepted, and closed in a controlled, consistent, and auditable manner.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to prospective and active client engagements conducted under the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

This policy governs the client engagement lifecycle from initial qualification through engagement closeout.

This policy does not define contract terms, pricing requirements, confidentiality handling rules, records retention requirements, third-party governance requirements, or publication procedures unless those subjects directly affect client engagement governance.

## 3. Policy Position

Send Manna Too LLC shall manage client engagements through controlled governance practices that protect business integrity, client trust, delivery quality, scope discipline, evidence quality, and accountability.

Client engagements shall not rely on informal understandings, undocumented commitments, uncontrolled deliverables, or unclear acceptance expectations.

Engagement decisions shall be documented when needed to support auditability, client clarity, risk management, and business continuity.

## 4. Client Qualification

Prospective client engagements shall be evaluated before acceptance.

Client qualification shall consider:

- Business fit.
- Requested services.
- Capability to deliver.
- Resource availability.
- Independence or conflict concerns.
- Delivery risk.
- Client expectations.
- Known legal, commercial, confidentiality, or operational constraints.

Engagements shall not be accepted when the firm lacks sufficient understanding of the requested work, expected deliverables, material dependencies, or material risks.

## 5. Engagement Authorization

A client engagement shall not begin until appropriate authorization exists.

Engagement authorization shall confirm that:

- The client or prospect is sufficiently identified.
- The requested work is understood at a governance level.
- The engagement scope is defined enough to begin controlled work.
- Material assumptions and dependencies are known or explicitly unresolved.
- Required approvals have been obtained.
- The engagement does not bypass applicable governance, legal, commercial, or confidentiality obligations.

Authorization may be documented through an approved agreement, written approval, approved statement of work, approved engagement record, or other appropriate business evidence.

## 6. Scope Definition and Scope Control

Each client engagement shall maintain a defined scope.

Scope governance shall address:

- Engagement objectives.
- In-scope services.
- Out-of-scope activities.
- Expected deliverables.
- Client responsibilities.
- Firm responsibilities.
- Key assumptions.
- Material dependencies.
- Target dates or timing expectations when applicable.
- Known constraints.

Material scope uncertainty shall be resolved or documented before work is treated as approved, final, or client-ready.

Scope shall not be expanded through informal conversation, draft deliverables, meeting notes, or unsupported assumptions.

## 7. Assumptions and Dependencies

Material assumptions and dependencies shall be identified, communicated, and reviewed during the engagement lifecycle.

Assumptions and dependencies may include:

- Client-provided information.
- Access to personnel or systems.
- Timely client feedback.
- Availability of evidence.
- Legal or compliance constraints.
- Third-party participation.
- Technology or data limitations.
- Business timing constraints.

When an assumption or dependency becomes inaccurate, incomplete, or unavailable, the engagement impact shall be reviewed before continuing affected work.

## 8. Client Communications

Client communications shall be timely, accurate, professional, and consistent with approved engagement objectives.

Material client communications shall be clear enough to distinguish:

- Facts.
- Assumptions.
- Recommendations.
- Open items.
- Decisions needed.
- Risks or constraints.
- Next steps.

Client communications shall not create uncontrolled commitments, unsupported promises, or expectations that conflict with approved scope, authority, or documented constraints.

## 9. Deliverable Governance

Client deliverables shall be governed before release.

Deliverable governance shall confirm that deliverables are:

- Aligned with approved scope.
- Accurate based on available evidence.
- Complete for their stated purpose.
- Written in clear business language.
- Reviewed for quality.
- Reviewed for client-facing risk.
- Marked or handled consistently with applicable confidentiality expectations.
- Approved before external release when approval is required.

Draft deliverables shall not be treated as final unless approved through the applicable engagement and governance process.

## 10. Engagement Changes

Material engagement changes shall be evaluated before implementation.

Change evaluation shall consider impact to:

- Scope.
- Deliverables.
- Schedule.
- Resources.
- Assumptions.
- Dependencies.
- Client responsibilities.
- Firm responsibilities.
- Business risk.
- Approval requirements.

Material changes shall be documented in a manner appropriate to the engagement, risk, and client commitment.

## 11. Risk Escalation

Engagement risks shall be escalated when they may affect client trust, delivery quality, scope control, confidentiality, legal or commercial obligations, independence, evidence quality, or firm reputation.

Escalation triggers may include:

- Unclear authority.
- Conflicting client instructions.
- Requested work outside approved scope.
- Incomplete or unreliable client information.
- Missed client dependencies.
- Material delivery delays.
- Potential confidentiality concerns.
- Potential legal or commercial exposure.
- Disagreement over deliverable content or acceptance.
- Requests to bypass approved governance controls.

Escalated risks shall be reviewed before affected commitments are made or affected deliverables are released.

## 12. Client Acceptance and Engagement Closeout

Client engagements shall be closed in a controlled manner.

Engagement closeout shall confirm, as applicable:

- Agreed deliverables were completed or dispositioned.
- Open issues were resolved, deferred, or documented.
- Client feedback or acceptance was obtained when appropriate.
- Final deliverables were identified.
- Material decisions and approvals were retained as evidence.
- Follow-up work was separated from the closed engagement unless formally approved.

Engagement closeout shall not be used to conceal unresolved commitments, unmanaged exceptions, or incomplete deliverables.

## 13. Required Records

Client engagement records shall be sufficient to support auditability, continuity, and accountability.

Required records may include:

- Qualification notes.
- Authorization evidence.
- Scope or statement of work evidence.
- Assumption or dependency records.
- Material client communications.
- Change approvals.
- Deliverable review evidence.
- Acceptance or closeout evidence.
- Risk escalation records.

This policy defines record expectations for engagement governance only. Detailed retention periods and storage rules shall be governed by an approved records and retention policy when established.

The Document Hierarchy Standard controls document classification, placement, naming, hierarchy, and conflict resolution.

The Document Quality Standard controls quality, evidence, readability, review, and publication readiness expectations.

Where another approved policy specifically governs contracting, confidentiality, records retention, third-party governance, or external deliverables, that policy controls for its defined scope.

## 15. Noncompliance

Failure to follow this policy may create scope, quality, client trust, contractual, confidentiality, commercial, evidence, or reputational risk.

Identified noncompliance shall be reviewed based on impact, cause, corrective action, and recurrence risk.

Material noncompliance shall be documented and remediated through an approved corrective action, decision record, release update, exception record, or other appropriate governance evidence.

## 16. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed when engagement practices, service offerings, legal obligations, client obligations, governance expectations, or operating practices materially change.

Changes to this policy shall be made through controlled Markdown updates in Git and shall include validation appropriate to the change.
