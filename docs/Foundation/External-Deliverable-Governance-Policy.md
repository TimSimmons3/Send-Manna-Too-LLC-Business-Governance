## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes governance requirements for deliverables issued outside Send Manna Too LLC.

Its purpose is to ensure that external deliverables are authorized, appropriate for their intended purpose and audience, aligned with approved scope and obligations, supported by sufficient evidence, reviewed for applicable risk, traceable to their source, distributed only to authorized recipients, and controlled after issuance.

This policy governs the decision boundary between internally prepared work product and an externally issued deliverable.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to deliverables prepared, approved, issued, presented, transmitted, or distributed by or on behalf of Send Manna Too LLC to a client, prospective client, partner, subcontractor, vendor, regulator, insurer, attorney, auditor, executive, board, or other external recipient.

External deliverables may include reports, assessments, findings, recommendations, risk analyses, governance documents, executive summaries, presentations, questionnaires, evidence packages, plans, roadmaps, formal correspondence, proposals, statements of work, and other work products intended for external reliance, action, decision-making, or record.

This policy applies regardless of file format, delivery channel, document title, or whether the deliverable is provided electronically, physically, through an approved platform, or through a formal presentation.

This policy does not replace signed agreements, client-specific obligations, legal advice, professional judgment, or more specific approved governance requirements.

## 3. Policy Statement

No work product shall be treated as an authorized external deliverable merely because it exists, has been formatted, has been exported, has been uploaded, has been transmitted, has been reviewed informally, uses a final-looking filename, includes branding, or was created from an approved template.

An external deliverable shall be treated as approved for release only when the applicable scope, authority, quality, confidentiality, commercial, partner, records, publication, and risk requirements have been satisfied or an approved exception applies.

## 4. External Deliverable Governance Principles

External deliverables shall be governed according to the following principles:

- The deliverable must have a defined and legitimate business purpose.
- Release authority must be clear and appropriate.
- Content must remain within approved scope and obligation boundaries.
- Material statements must be supportable based on available evidence.
- The deliverable must be sufficiently complete for its stated purpose.
- Material assumptions, limitations, dependencies, and residual risks must be disclosed or otherwise controlled.
- Information must be appropriate for the intended recipient and use.
- Review and approval rigor must be proportionate to sensitivity, reliance, complexity, obligation, and risk.
- The deliverable must remain traceable to its source, version, review, approval, and issuance evidence where material.
- Distribution must be limited to authorized recipients and approved purposes.
- Material errors or unauthorized disclosures must be addressed through controlled correction, withdrawal, or replacement.
- Ownership and release accountability must remain identifiable.

## 5. Deliverable Status

Deliverable status shall be determined by approved purpose and use, not merely by filename, format, location, or possession.

Statuses may include:

- Internal work product: Material intended only for internal preparation, analysis, or coordination.
- Working draft: Incomplete or unapproved material subject to change.
- Review draft: Material provided for defined review, comment, or validation but not approved as final.
- Client review copy: Material provided to a client for review, clarification, or feedback without final-status representation.
- Final deliverable: Material approved for the stated external purpose and intended recipient.
- Superseded deliverable: A previously issued deliverable replaced by a later approved version.
- Withdrawn deliverable: A deliverable whose use or reliance has been revoked.
- Corrected deliverable: A deliverable reissued to correct an error while preserving its general purpose.
- Replacement deliverable: A deliverable issued in place of a prior version when material content, scope, status, or intended reliance has changed.

The current controlling status shall be clear when confusion could create risk.

Draft, review, superseded, or withdrawn material shall not be represented as current final guidance.

## 6. Purpose, Audience, Recipient, and Reliance

Before external release, the deliverable's intended purpose, audience, recipient, and expected use shall be sufficiently understood.

Review shall consider:

- The decision, action, communication, obligation, or outcome the deliverable is intended to support.
- Whether the intended recipient is authorized to receive it and has a legitimate business need.
- Whether the deliverable is appropriate for the recipient's role, knowledge, and expected use.
- Whether reliance should be limited to a defined purpose, time, scope, system, client, engagement, or decision.
- Whether redistribution, publication, quotation, modification, or reuse should be restricted.
- Whether the deliverable could reasonably be misunderstood as legal advice, certification, guarantee, warranty, audit opinion, or binding commitment.

When intended use or permitted reliance is unclear, the deliverable shall not be released until clarified or appropriately limited.

## 7. Ownership and Release Authority

Each material external deliverable shall have an identifiable owner or accountable business function.

Ownership includes responsibility for:

- Confirming purpose and status.
- Coordinating required reviews.
- Confirming alignment with approved scope and obligations.
- Ensuring that material issues are resolved, disclosed, escalated, or excepted.
- Confirming the appropriate release authority.
- Preserving required records and evidence.
- Supporting correction, withdrawal, replacement, or recipient notification when needed.

Ownership does not by itself grant authority to release a deliverable externally.

The release decision shall be made by a person with appropriate business, governance, engagement, or commercial authority.

## 8. Pre-Release Governance Gate

Before external release, the accountable owner shall confirm that applicable governance requirements have been satisfied.

The review shall address, as applicable:

- Scope, engagement, authority, and client-obligation alignment.
- Source reliability and evidence sufficiency.
- Material assumptions, limitations, dependencies, and residual risks.
- Confidentiality, need-to-know, minimization, redaction, and disclosure authorization.
- Legal, commercial, insurance, tax, regulatory, or professional-review needs.
- Third-party or partner involvement and authority.
- Document quality and acceptance criteria.
- Publication readiness and artifact validation.
- Version, source, approval, and release traceability.
- Recipient, distribution, redistribution, and reliance restrictions.

Detailed requirements remain controlled by the applicable governance documents identified in Section 18.

A deliverable shall not be released when a material requirement remains unresolved unless the issue is appropriately disclosed, limited, escalated, or covered by an approved exception.

## 9. Approval to Create, Publish, and Release

Authority to create content, authority to generate a publication output, and authority to release a deliverable externally are distinct.

A person may be authorized to prepare or revise a draft, perform analysis, create a Word, PDF, PowerPoint, or other publication output, conduct a review, or recommend approval without having authority to approve external release.

External release requires the applicable business, governance, engagement, or commercial authorization.

Approval shall not be assumed from draft creation, review participation, formatting, export, file storage, client possession, informal discussion, or lack of objection.

## 10. Final-Status Determination

A deliverable shall be treated as final only when it is approved for its stated purpose, recipient, and release context.

Final status requires, as applicable:

- Approved scope and obligation alignment.
- Completion of required review.
- Resolution, disclosure, escalation, or exception of material issues.
- Appropriate release authority.
- Clear version and status.
- Appropriate confidentiality and distribution controls.
- Applicable publication validation.
- Sufficient traceability and evidence.

Final status shall not be inferred solely from a filename containing "final," conversion to PDF, branding, a signature image, upload to a delivery platform, transmission to a client, informal verbal approval, client silence, or prior use of a similar deliverable.

## 11. Version, Source, and Publication Traceability

Material external deliverables shall remain traceable to their approved source, version, review, approval, and issuance evidence where practical and proportionate.

Traceability may include:

- Source document or business record.
- Source path, system, Git commit, release tag, document version, or revision identifier.
- Review or approval record.
- Publication output identifier.
- Issue date.
- Authorized recipient or distribution list.
- Delivery method.
- Correction, withdrawal, or replacement relationship.
- Acceptance, acknowledgment, or closeout evidence.

When a controlled publication format is used, the deliverable shall also satisfy the applicable publication and artifact-validation requirements.

Publication formatting does not make an unapproved source final or authoritative.

## 12. Authorized Distribution and Restrictions

Only approved external deliverables shall be distributed as final.

Distribution shall be:

- Limited to authorized recipients.
- Consistent with the approved purpose.
- Appropriate to the information sensitivity and delivery risk.
- Consistent with client, contractual, confidentiality, and commercial obligations.
- Performed through a reasonable and approved method.
- Supported by delivery evidence when material.

Appropriate restrictions shall be used when the deliverable is limited to named recipients, internal client use, a defined decision purpose, specific systems or time periods, restricted redistribution, restricted quotation or modification, third-party reliance limits, or required independent professional review.

Restrictions shall be clear, proportionate, and consistent with applicable obligations.

A restriction shall not be used to conceal known error, avoid responsibility, or contradict a signed commitment.

Uncontrolled working copies, obsolete versions, superseded versions, or unapproved drafts shall not be distributed as final deliverables.

## 13. Changes After Approval

Material changes to an approved deliverable shall be reviewed before release or continued reliance.

Material changes may include changes to:

- Scope.
- Findings, conclusions, or recommendations.
- Evidence.
- Assumptions, limitations, dependencies, or residual risk.
- Commercial statements.
- Confidential information.
- Recipient or intended purpose.
- Distribution or reliance restrictions.
- Version status or acceptance criteria.

A materially changed deliverable shall not retain prior approval merely because its filename, format, or general structure remains unchanged.

Post-approval changes shall be traceable and shall receive reauthorization proportionate to their effect.

## 14. Correction, Withdrawal, and Replacement

An issued deliverable shall be corrected, withdrawn, superseded, or replaced when a material condition affects its accuracy, authority, confidentiality, scope alignment, intended reliance, or continued use.

Potential triggers include:

- Material factual error.
- Unsupported conclusion.
- Omitted material limitation.
- Incorrect recipient or unauthorized disclosure.
- Wrong version.
- Scope conflict.
- Material change in evidence or circumstances.
- Contradiction with an applicable obligation.
- Unapproved commitment.
- Misleading status.
- Identification of a material defect by a client, reviewer, or other responsible party.

The selected action shall be proportionate to the risk and shall preserve evidence of what changed, why, who approved the action, and what recipients were affected.

A superseded, withdrawn, or replaced deliverable shall not be silently altered in a manner that destroys material issuance history.

## 15. Recipient Notification

Affected recipients shall be notified when correction, withdrawal, replacement, or reliance limitation is material to their use of an issued deliverable.

Notification shall be timely and proportionate to:

- Severity of the issue.
- Likelihood of continued reliance.
- Potential client, legal, commercial, confidentiality, operational, security, or reputational impact.
- Number and type of recipients.
- Applicable contractual or professional obligations.
- Availability of a corrected or replacement deliverable.

Notification should identify, as appropriate:

- The affected deliverable.
- The issue or status change.
- Whether reliance should stop or be limited.
- Required recipient action.
- Availability of a corrected or replacement version.
- Contact or escalation path.

Notification shall not include unnecessary confidential information.

## 16. Client Acceptance or Acknowledgment

Client acceptance, acknowledgment, receipt confirmation, review completion, or closeout evidence shall be obtained when appropriate to the engagement, obligation, deliverable, and risk.

Acceptance or acknowledgment may be appropriate when:

- A contract or statement of work requires it.
- The deliverable establishes completion of a defined obligation.
- Client approval is needed before further work.
- Material findings or residual risks require confirmation.
- The deliverable affects scope, responsibility, or decision rights.
- Delivery or receipt may later be disputed.

Client possession, silence, meeting attendance, or informal discussion shall not be represented as acceptance unless the applicable context supports that conclusion.

Acceptance evidence does not override unresolved legal, confidentiality, commercial, quality, or governance issues.

## 17. External Deliverable Records and Evidence

Material external-deliverable activity shall be supported by records sufficient to demonstrate what was prepared, reviewed, approved, issued, changed, corrected, withdrawn, replaced, accepted, or closed.

Evidence may include:

- Approved scope or deliverable requirement.
- Source and evidence references.
- Review and approval records.
- Publication validation records.
- Version or revision records.
- Distribution or delivery evidence.
- Recipient list or delivery destination.
- Confidentiality, commercial, partner, or professional-review evidence.
- Exception or escalation records.
- Client acknowledgment or acceptance evidence.
- Correction, withdrawal, replacement, or notification evidence.
- Closeout evidence.

This policy identifies external-deliverable evidence expectations. The Records Evidence and Retention Policy controls retention, preservation, protection, recoverability, review, and authorized disposition principles for those records.

## 19. External Deliverable Risk Escalation

External-deliverable risk shall be escalated when uncertainty or exposure could materially affect client trust, scope, authority, quality, confidentiality, legal or commercial obligations, reliance, delivery, independence, evidence integrity, or firm reputation.

Escalation triggers include:

- Unclear release authority or conflicting scope.
- Unsupported, disputed, incomplete, unreliable, or conflicting material information.
- Potential unauthorized disclosure or incorrect recipient.
- Potential legal, commercial, insurance, tax, compliance, or contractual exposure.
- Unsupported guarantee, warranty, promise, certification, or binding representation.
- Partner or subcontractor role uncertainty.
- Request to suppress material limitations or residual risk.
- Request to issue an unapproved or incomplete deliverable.
- Request to bypass quality, confidentiality, commercial, partner, records, or publication controls.
- Discovery that a released deliverable may be materially inaccurate, obsolete, misdirected, or improperly relied upon.

Escalated risk shall be reviewed before release or continued reliance when practical.

## 20. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy shall be limited, documented when material, risk-based, and approved through appropriate authority.

An exception record shall identify:

- The requirement being excepted.
- The reason for the exception.
- The affected deliverable, engagement, client, recipient, or activity.
- The approving authority.
- The risk created.
- Any compensating control.
- Any distribution or reliance limitation.
- The review date, expiration date, or closure condition when practical.

Exceptions shall not be used to avoid the Constitution, bypass legal or contractual obligations, weaken confidentiality commitments, conceal material error or risk, create unsupported client expectations, avoid required professional review, misrepresent a draft as final, destroy required evidence, or circumvent authorized decision rights.

## 21. Noncompliance

Failure to follow this policy may create client trust, scope, quality, confidentiality, legal, commercial, contractual, evidence, reliance, partner, operational, or reputational risk.

Material noncompliance shall be documented and addressed through appropriate correction, withdrawal, replacement, notification, escalation, exception, client communication, commercial review, confidentiality review, corrective action, release update, or other controlled evidence.

Recurring noncompliance shall be reviewed for root cause and potential governance, process, training, template, or tooling improvement.

## 22. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed when services, client obligations, delivery methods, publication practices, partner use, confidentiality expectations, legal or commercial obligations, technology, professional standards, or governance requirements materially change.

Approved changes shall follow the controlled governance release process.

Recurring exceptions, corrections, withdrawals, replacements, distribution errors, or reliance disputes may indicate that this policy or related standards, procedures, templates, checklists, training, or tooling should be updated.
