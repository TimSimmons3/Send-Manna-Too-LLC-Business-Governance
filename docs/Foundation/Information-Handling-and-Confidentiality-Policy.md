## 1. Purpose

This policy defines governance requirements for how Send Manna Too LLC handles firm information, client information, sensitive information, confidentiality expectations, information sharing boundaries, client-provided evidence, working drafts, internal notes, external disclosure, and confidentiality-related escalation.

The purpose of this policy is to protect client trust, firm integrity, professional independence, evidence quality, commercial discipline, and responsible information use.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to firm-controlled information and client-provided information handled under the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

This policy applies to internal work, client engagement activity, commercial and contracting activity, governance records, drafts, working notes, client evidence, advisory materials, and information shared with authorized external parties.

This policy does not create data classification technical standards, cybersecurity technical controls, retention schedules, third-party onboarding procedures, external deliverable review procedures, NDA clauses, legal advice, or contract templates.

## 3. Policy Position

Send Manna Too LLC shall handle information in a controlled, professional, need-to-know, and confidentiality-aware manner.

Information shall be used only for authorized business, governance, advisory, client engagement, commercial, legal, compliance, or operational purposes.

No information shall be shared, disclosed, copied, reused, published, or relied upon in a manner that creates unmanaged client trust risk, confidentiality risk, legal risk, commercial risk, evidence risk, or reputational risk.

## 4. Information Governance Principles

Information handling shall be governed by the following principles:

- Respect client trust and firm integrity.
- Use information only for authorized purposes.
- Limit access and sharing to a legitimate business need.
- Preserve confidentiality expectations communicated by clients, partners, contracts, or firm governance.
- Avoid unnecessary collection, copying, distribution, or retention of sensitive information.
- Clearly distinguish facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, and residual risk when information supports advisory work.
- Escalate uncertainty before disclosing, reusing, or relying on sensitive or confidential information.
- Preserve evidence sufficient to support auditability without creating unnecessary duplication or uncontrolled records.

## 5. Information Categories

Send Manna Too LLC shall apply handling judgment based on the nature, source, sensitivity, and intended use of information.

| Category | Description | Governance Expectation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Firm information | Information created, owned, or maintained by Send Manna Too LLC | Protect firm integrity, intellectual property, operations, decision records, and business interests. |
| Client information | Information received from, created for, or derived from work with a client | Use only for authorized client engagement, commercial, governance, or professional purposes. |
| Sensitive information | Information that could create harm, exposure, dispute, or loss if misused or disclosed improperly | Apply heightened care, need-to-know access, appropriate marking, and escalation when unclear. |
| Public information | Information lawfully available to the public and appropriate for business use | Verify reliability and avoid implying that public availability removes professional handling obligations. |

Information may fall into more than one category. When category or sensitivity is unclear, the more protective handling expectation shall apply until clarified.

## 6. Confidentiality Expectations

Confidentiality expectations may arise from client direction, signed agreements, statements of work, business context, professional judgment, legal obligations, governance decisions, or the nature of the information itself.

Send Manna Too LLC shall treat information as confidential when it is identified as confidential, reasonably appears non-public, is client-provided, contains sensitive business information, relates to governance decisions, or could create harm if disclosed without authorization.

Confidentiality expectations shall be interpreted conservatively when client trust, legal obligations, commercial obligations, personal information, security information, proprietary information, or reputational risk may be affected.

## 7. Confidentiality Markings

Information should be marked or labeled when a marking helps prevent misuse, misrouting, unauthorized disclosure, or confusion about handling expectations.

Common confidentiality markings may include:

- Confidential.
- Internal use only.
- Client confidential.
- Draft.
- Not for external distribution.
- Property and Confidential.

Markings support handling expectations but do not replace professional judgment. Unmarked information may still require confidential handling based on its content, source, context, or applicable obligation.

Publication-ready deliverables shall follow approved publication requirements and templates where applicable. Markdown in Git remains the canonical source for controlled framework documents.

## 8. Need-to-Know Access

Access to non-public, confidential, client-provided, or sensitive information shall be limited to persons or parties with a legitimate business need.

A legitimate business need may exist when access is required to perform approved advisory work, governance review, quality review, legal review, commercial review, delivery support, evidence validation, or authorized business administration.

Need-to-know access shall not be assumed from convenience, curiosity, informal involvement, prior access, partner status, vendor status, or general relationship to the firm.

## 9. Information Sharing Boundaries

Information sharing shall be purposeful, limited, and consistent with approved authority, client expectations, applicable agreements, and professional judgment.

Before sharing non-public, confidential, client-provided, or sensitive information, Send Manna Too LLC shall consider:

- Whether sharing is authorized.
- Whether the recipient has a legitimate need to know.
- Whether the information is accurate and appropriate for the purpose.
- Whether the information should be minimized, summarized, anonymized, redacted, or withheld.
- Whether client approval, business approval, legal review, commercial review, or escalation is needed.
- Whether the sharing could create confidentiality, legal, commercial, evidence, client trust, or reputational risk.

Information shall not be shared externally merely because it is useful, convenient, available, requested informally, or included in a prior draft.

## 10. Client-Provided Evidence Handling

Client-provided evidence shall be handled with care appropriate to its sensitivity, purpose, and source.

Client-provided evidence may include documents, screenshots, reports, system exports, questionnaires, policies, contracts, diagrams, risk records, control evidence, audit artifacts, meeting materials, or other client-supplied materials.

Client-provided evidence shall be used only for the authorized engagement, governance, review, or business purpose for which it was provided, unless the client authorizes another use or another approved obligation requires it.

Client-provided evidence shall not be reused across clients, included in unrelated deliverables, disclosed to unauthorized parties, or converted into firm reusable material unless appropriately sanitized, generalized, approved, and free of client-identifying or sensitive content.

This policy defines handling expectations only. Detailed retention periods, storage rules, and disposal requirements shall be governed by an approved records evidence and retention policy when established.

## 11. Working Drafts and Internal Notes

Working drafts and internal notes shall be treated as controlled work product when they include non-public, confidential, client-provided, sensitive, strategic, commercial, legal, governance, or risk-related information.

Drafts and notes shall not be treated as final, approved, client-ready, or externally distributable unless approved through the applicable governance, engagement, commercial, or publication process.

Internal notes shall not create uncontrolled commitments, unmanaged promises, unsupported conclusions, unauthorized disclosures, or client-facing positions.

Draft materials should be clearly identified when there is a reasonable risk that a recipient could mistake them for final, approved, or externally distributable content.

## 12. External Disclosure Limits

External disclosure of non-public, confidential, client-provided, or sensitive information shall occur only when authorized and appropriate for the business purpose.

External disclosure may require escalation or approval when it involves:

- Client information.
- Sensitive business information.
- Security, control, risk, or audit information.
- Contractual or commercial information.
- Personal, regulated, or legally sensitive information.
- Firm intellectual property or reusable framework content.
- Information received from a partner, vendor, subcontractor, or other external party.
- Information that may affect client trust, legal obligations, commercial obligations, or firm reputation.

External disclosure shall not occur when authority, purpose, accuracy, confidentiality status, recipient need, or risk is unclear.

## 13. Confidentiality-Related Escalation

Confidentiality-related issues shall be escalated when uncertainty or risk could affect client trust, legal obligations, commercial obligations, professional independence, evidence quality, delivery quality, or firm reputation.

Escalation triggers include:

- Unclear permission to use or share information.
- Suspected misdirected information.
- Suspected unauthorized disclosure.
- Conflicting confidentiality expectations.
- Requests for client information by unauthorized or unclear recipients.
- Use of client information in reusable firm materials.
- Proposed disclosure involving sensitive, regulated, legal, security, audit, or commercial information.
- Potential conflict between client direction, signed agreements, governance expectations, and professional judgment.
- Discovery that a draft, note, or work product may have been shared beyond its intended audience.

Escalated issues shall be reviewed before further disclosure, reliance, reuse, or distribution when practical.

## 14. Confidentiality Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy shall be limited, documented when material, risk-based, and approved through appropriate governance authority.

An exception may be appropriate when a legal obligation, client instruction, contractual obligation, emergency business need, professional review requirement, or approved governance decision requires a departure from normal handling expectations.

Exceptions shall not be used to bypass client trust, conceal risk, avoid legal or commercial review, weaken confidentiality commitments, or disclose information without a legitimate business purpose.

## 15. Relationship to Client Engagement Governance

The Client Engagement Governance Policy controls client qualification, engagement authorization, scope control, client communications, deliverable governance, engagement changes, risk escalation, client acceptance, engagement closeout, and required engagement records.

This policy controls information handling and confidentiality expectations that apply within client engagement activity.

When client engagement activity involves client-provided information, confidential information, sensitive information, draft materials, disclosure questions, or confidentiality escalation, this policy applies.

## 16. Relationship to Commercial and Contracting Governance

The Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy controls proposals, pricing, contracting, obligation review, signature authority, client-facing commercial commitments, commercial exceptions, commercial risk escalation, and commercial evidence.

This policy controls information handling and confidentiality expectations that apply within commercial and contracting activity.

When commercial or contracting activity involves confidentiality implications, information sharing, client-provided evidence, disclosure limits, or confidentiality escalation, this policy applies.

This policy does not replace signed agreements, legal advice, client-specific scopes of work, or professional judgment.

## 17. Relationship to Future Governance Documents

Future records, third-party, partner, cybersecurity, data classification, external deliverable, publication, or technical standards may define more specific requirements within their approved scope.

Where a future approved policy or standard specifically governs records retention, evidence preservation, third-party handling, technical control implementation, data classification mechanics, external deliverable review, or publication procedure, that document shall control for its defined scope.

Until more specific documents are approved, this policy provides the governing confidentiality and information handling baseline.

## 18. Noncompliance

Failure to follow this policy may create confidentiality, client trust, legal, commercial, evidence, delivery, operational, or reputational risk.

Material noncompliance shall be documented and remediated through an approved corrective action, decision record, release update, exception record, client communication, legal review, commercial review, or other appropriate governance evidence.
