## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the governance requirements for commercial and contracting activity conducted by Send Manna Too LLC.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that proposals, pricing decisions, contract review, obligation acceptance, signature authority, client-facing commercial commitments, commercial exceptions, and commercial evidence are managed in a controlled, consistent, and auditable manner.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to commercial and contracting activity performed under the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

This policy applies to proposals, statements of work, commercial terms, pricing decisions, contract review, contract approval, obligation review, commercial exceptions, signature control, and contract-related evidence.

This policy does not create contract templates, draft legal clauses, provide legal advice, define confidentiality handling rules, define records retention rules, define third-party governance requirements, or define publication procedures unless those subjects directly affect commercial and contracting governance.

## 3. Policy Position

Send Manna Too LLC shall manage commercial and contracting activity through controlled governance practices that protect business integrity, client trust, commercial discipline, scope control, obligation clarity, evidence quality, and accountability.

Commercial and contracting decisions shall be made deliberately, documented when material, and aligned with the approved governance hierarchy.

No proposal, estimate, informal statement, planning note, template, or draft may create an uncontrolled commercial commitment or unsupported promise.

## 4. Commercial Governance Principles

Commercial activity shall be governed by the following principles:

- Commitments must be clear, authorized, and supportable.
- Pricing must be tied to understood scope, assumptions, risks, and delivery obligations.
- Contract obligations must be reviewed before acceptance.
- Client-facing commercial statements must not exceed approved authority.
- Exceptions must be documented, risk-based, and approved before reliance.
- Evidence must be retained in a manner sufficient to support auditability, client clarity, and business continuity.

Commercial governance shall be interpreted conservatively when scope, authority, pricing, obligation, or risk is unclear.

## 5. Proposal Governance

Client-facing proposals shall be prepared with sufficient understanding of the requested work, expected deliverables, assumptions, dependencies, delivery constraints, and commercial risk.

A proposal shall not be treated as approved or client-ready until it has been reviewed for:

- Scope clarity.
- Deliverable clarity.
- Pricing basis.
- Material assumptions.
- Client dependencies.
- Firm dependencies.
- Delivery feasibility.
- Commercial exposure.
- Unsupported promises.
- Alignment with approved client engagement governance.

Proposal language shall distinguish planned work, assumptions, dependencies, exclusions, client responsibilities, and items requiring future agreement.

Proposal language shall not imply guaranteed outcomes, unlimited support, legal advice, regulatory approval, or obligations that Send Manna Too LLC is not prepared and authorized to accept.

## 6. Pricing Governance

Pricing decisions shall be based on a reasonable understanding of scope, effort, complexity, timing, risk, dependencies, and required expertise.

Pricing shall not be presented as final unless the scope, assumptions, commercial basis, and approval status are sufficiently clear.

Pricing changes shall be reviewed when a material change occurs in scope, timeline, assumptions, client dependency, delivery model, risk, or required resources.

Discounts, concessions, deferred payment arrangements, unusual payment terms, or other commercial departures shall be reviewed as commercial exceptions when they create material business or delivery risk.

This policy does not create pricing sheets or prescribe rate cards.

## 7. Contracting Governance

Contracting activity shall be managed so that commercial obligations are understood before acceptance.

Before a contract, statement of work, order, amendment, or similar commercial instrument is accepted, Send Manna Too LLC shall review whether the document accurately reflects the intended scope, pricing, deliverables, assumptions, dependencies, acceptance expectations, and commercial obligations.

Contracting review shall consider:

- Identity of the client or contracting party.
- Scope of work.
- Deliverables.
- Pricing and payment terms.
- Performance timelines.
- Acceptance expectations.
- Client responsibilities.
- Firm responsibilities.
- Confidentiality or information handling implications.
- Third-party or subcontractor implications.
- Intellectual property or reuse implications.
- Limitation, indemnity, warranty, insurance, audit, security, or compliance obligations when present.
- Termination, renewal, amendment, and dispute-related obligations when present.
- Evidence required to prove approval, delivery, acceptance, or closeout.

Contracting review shall identify when qualified legal review, insurance review, tax review, or other professional review is appropriate before acceptance.

This policy does not provide legal advice and does not replace review by qualified counsel when legal interpretation is required.

## 8. Obligation Review

Send Manna Too LLC shall not accept material commercial obligations without reasonable review of the obligation, the authority to accept it, and the ability to perform it.

Obligation review shall evaluate whether the obligation is:

- Clearly stated.
- Within approved scope.
- Commercially reasonable.
- Operationally feasible.
- Supported by available skills, tools, time, and evidence.
- Consistent with client engagement governance.
- Consistent with confidentiality, records, third-party, and publication requirements where applicable.
- Escalated when legal, commercial, delivery, reputational, or evidence risk is material.

Unclear obligations shall be clarified, revised, excluded, escalated, or documented before being treated as accepted.

## 9. Signature Authority

Only an authorized representative of Send Manna Too LLC may sign, approve, accept, or otherwise bind the firm to commercial or contracting obligations.

Signature authority shall be exercised only after sufficient review of scope, pricing, obligations, authority, and risk.

No person, process, draft, proposal, email, meeting statement, publication artifact, or informal communication may bypass required approval or create signature authority where it has not been granted.

When signature authority is unclear, the decision shall be escalated before any commitment is made.

## 10. Client-Facing Commercial Commitments

Client-facing commercial commitments shall be made only with appropriate business authority and sufficient understanding of the related scope, pricing, assumptions, dependencies, legal or commercial obligations, confidentiality implications, and delivery risk.

Commercial commitments may include statements about:

- Price.
- Discount.
- Scope.
- Deliverables.
- Timeline.
- Staffing or availability.
- Service level.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Client responsibility.
- Firm responsibility.
- Contractual obligation.
- Change handling.
- Payment expectation.
- Future work.

Client-facing commercial statements shall be accurate, supportable, and consistent with approved scope and contracting status.

Material commercial uncertainty shall be resolved or documented before a statement is treated as final, binding, or client-ready.

## 11. Commercial Exceptions

Commercial exceptions shall be limited, documented, risk-based, and approved before reliance when practical.

A commercial exception may include a departure from normal proposal, pricing, contracting, obligation review, signature, payment, scope, or approval expectations.

A commercial exception record shall identify:

- The exception requested or taken.
- The reason for the exception.
- The approving authority.
- The affected client, proposal, contract, scope, pricing, or obligation.
- The risk created by the exception.
- Any compensating control or limitation.
- The expiration date, review date, or closeout condition when practical.

Commercial exceptions shall not be used to bypass legal obligations, conceal risk, weaken confidentiality commitments, create unsupported client expectations, or avoid the Constitution.

## 12. Commercial Risk Escalation

Commercial risk shall be escalated when it may affect client trust, revenue, collection, scope control, delivery feasibility, legal or commercial obligations, confidentiality, independence, evidence quality, or firm reputation.

Escalation triggers include:

- Unclear contracting authority.
- Unclear client authority.
- Unclear or conflicting scope.
- Unsupported pricing assumptions.
- Unusual payment terms.
- Material discount or concession request.
- Client request for unsupported promises.
- Contract terms that create unclear or excessive obligation.
- Delivery obligation that may exceed available capability or capacity.
- Conflict between proposal language and contract language.
- Potential legal, insurance, tax, confidentiality, third-party, or compliance exposure.
- Request to begin work before appropriate authorization exists.

Escalated commercial risks shall be reviewed before affected commitments are made, affected contracts are signed, or affected work begins.

## 13. Commercial Evidence Requirements

Material commercial and contracting decisions shall be supported by records sufficient to show what was proposed, reviewed, approved, accepted, changed, escalated, or closed.

Commercial evidence may include:

- Approved proposal records.
- Approved statement of work records.
- Contract or order records.
- Pricing approval evidence.
- Scope approval evidence.
- Obligation review evidence.
- Signature approval evidence.
- Client approval or acceptance evidence.
- Commercial exception records.
- Risk escalation records.
- Change approval records.
- Contract closeout or completion evidence.

Commercial evidence shall be retained in a manner that supports auditability, client clarity, business continuity, and future review.

## 14. Relationship to Client Engagement Governance

The Client Engagement Governance Policy controls the client engagement lifecycle from qualification through closeout.

This policy controls commercial and contracting governance for proposals, pricing, contracting, obligation review, signature authority, commercial exceptions, and commercial evidence.

When client engagement activity creates a commercial or contracting commitment, this policy applies to that commitment.

When a commercial or contracting issue affects engagement scope, delivery, communication, risk, acceptance, or closeout, the Client Engagement Governance Policy also applies.

## 15. Relationship to Other Governance Documents

The Constitution remains the highest internal governance authority within the Business Governance Framework.

The Governance and Operating Authority Policy controls decision rights, approval expectations, exception authority, controlled operating discipline, client-facing authority, and records and evidence expectations.

The Document Hierarchy Standard controls document type, placement, naming, hierarchy, conflict resolution, and duplicate guidance expectations.

The Document Quality Standard controls quality, evidence, readability, review, legal and commercial caution, and publication readiness expectations.

Where another approved policy specifically governs confidentiality, records retention, third-party governance, publication, or external deliverables, that policy controls for its defined scope.

Signed agreements, legal obligations, and client-specific scopes of work may create obligations outside this policy. This policy governs internal commercial and contracting control and does not replace signed agreements, legal advice, client-specific scopes of work, or professional judgment.

## 16. Noncompliance

Failure to follow this policy may create scope, revenue, collection, contractual, confidentiality, commercial, evidence, delivery, client trust, or reputational risk.

Identified noncompliance shall be reviewed based on impact, cause, corrective action, and recurrence risk.

Material noncompliance shall be documented and remediated through an approved corrective action, decision record, release update, exception record, or other appropriate governance evidence.

## 17. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed when commercial practices, contracting practices, service offerings, pricing approaches, legal obligations, client obligations, governance expectations, or operating practices materially change.

Updates shall follow the approved governance lifecycle and remain aligned with the Constitution, document hierarchy, and canonical Markdown source control requirements.
