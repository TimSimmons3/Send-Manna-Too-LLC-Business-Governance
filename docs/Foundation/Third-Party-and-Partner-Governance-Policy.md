## 1. Purpose

This policy defines governance requirements for third-party and partner relationships involving Send Manna Too LLC.

The purpose of this policy is to protect client trust, professional independence, commercial integrity, confidentiality, delivery quality, evidence quality, and firm reputation when Send Manna Too LLC works with referral partners, subcontractors, vendors, external collaborators, or other outside parties.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to third-party and partner relationships that may affect Send Manna Too LLC business operations, client engagement activity, commercial commitments, information sharing, delivery support, evidence, professional judgment, or firm reputation.

This policy applies to referral partners, subcontractors, vendors, external collaborators, advisors, service providers, and other outside parties when their involvement may affect work performed by or for Send Manna Too LLC.

This policy does not create vendor security technical control standards, cybersecurity assessment procedures, procurement procedures, third-party onboarding checklists, referral agreement templates, subcontractor agreement templates, NDA clauses, legal advice, contract templates, records retention schedules, external deliverable review procedures, or publication workflow requirements.

## 3. Policy Statement

Send Manna Too LLC shall manage third-party and partner relationships through controlled governance practices that protect client trust, professional independence, confidentiality, commercial discipline, delivery accountability, evidence quality, and firm integrity.

No third-party or partner relationship shall be used to bypass approved governance, create unsupported commitments, weaken confidentiality expectations, obscure accountability, conceal conflicts of interest, or create unmanaged client-facing risk.

## 4. Governance Principles

Third-party and partner governance shall be guided by the following principles:

- Relationships must support legitimate business, client, governance, advisory, delivery, or operational purposes.
- Partner involvement must be authorized before reliance.
- Roles, responsibilities, scope boundaries, and limitations must be understood before partner involvement affects client-facing activity.
- Professional independence must not be compromised by referral potential, partner preference, convenience, compensation opportunity, or informal relationship pressure.
- Client trust and confidentiality must be protected before information is shared or partner involvement is disclosed.
- Commercial and contracting implications must be reviewed through the appropriate commercial governance process.
- Material partner risk must be escalated before it creates unmanaged client, commercial, legal, confidentiality, evidence, or reputational exposure.

## 5. Third-Party and Partner Categories

For governance purposes, third-party and partner relationships may include:

| Category | Description | Governance Expectation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Referral partner | A party that refers potential clients, opportunities, relationships, or work to Send Manna Too LLC | Referral activity must not compromise professional independence, client trust, or commercial discipline. |
| Subcontractor | A party engaged to support delivery of work for or with Send Manna Too LLC | Subcontractor involvement must be authorized, role-specific, and aligned with client, commercial, confidentiality, and delivery expectations. |
| Vendor | A party providing products, services, platforms, tools, or operational support to Send Manna Too LLC | Vendor use must be appropriate for the business purpose and escalated when it may create material client, confidentiality, legal, commercial, or operational risk. |
| External collaborator | A party informally or formally contributing expertise, review, advice, analysis, or delivery support | Collaboration must be controlled when it may affect client work, firm work product, confidentiality, or external commitments. |
| Strategic partner | A party involved in a broader business, advisory, market, or delivery relationship | Strategic partner activity must remain aligned to firm governance, independence, client trust, and commercial authority. |

These categories support governance review and do not replace signed agreements, legal review, procurement review, client-specific requirements, or professional judgment.

## 6. Partner Qualification and Fit

Before Send Manna Too LLC relies on a third party or partner for material business, client-facing, delivery, advisory, operational, or commercial activity, the relationship shall be assessed for appropriate fit.

Qualification and fit review should consider:

- Business purpose.
- Role and expected contribution.
- Reputation and professional conduct.
- Relevant capability or expertise.
- Independence and conflict considerations.
- Confidentiality implications.
- Client-facing impact.
- Commercial or contracting implications.
- Evidence or documentation expectations.
- Known legal, regulatory, compliance, operational, or reputational concerns.

Qualification review shall remain proportionate to the relationship, risk, and expected use. This policy does not require a formal procurement procedure or technical vendor assessment unless a separate approved document requires one.

## 7. Partner Authorization and Role Clarity

Third-party or partner involvement shall be authorized before the partner is relied upon for material client-facing, delivery, advisory, commercial, or governance activity.

Authorization should establish, as applicable:

- The approved business purpose.
- The partner category or relationship type.
- The expected role.
- Scope boundaries.
- Information access boundaries.
- Client-facing involvement, if any.
- Commercial or contracting dependencies.
- Delivery responsibilities.
- Evidence expectations.
- Escalation path for uncertainty or risk.

No partner may be presented as authorized to speak for, bind, commit, or represent Send Manna Too LLC unless that authority has been specifically approved.

## 8. Client-Facing Partner Involvement

Client-facing partner involvement shall be managed carefully to protect client trust, scope control, confidentiality, and accountability.

Before a partner participates in client-facing activity, Send Manna Too LLC shall consider whether:

- The client should be informed of the partner involvement.
- The partner role is clear and supportable.
- The partner involvement is consistent with approved scope and client expectations.
- Confidentiality obligations and information sharing limits are understood.
- Commercial or contracting approval is needed.
- The partner involvement could create confusion about accountability, authority, independence, or deliverable ownership.

Partner involvement shall not create uncontrolled commitments, unsupported promises, unmanaged disclosure, or ambiguity about who is responsible for client outcomes.

## 9. Information Sharing Boundaries

Information shared with a third party or partner shall be limited to the information needed for an authorized business purpose.

Before sharing non-public, confidential, client-provided, sensitive, commercial, legal, security, governance, or work-product information with a third party or partner, Send Manna Too LLC shall confirm that the sharing is appropriate, authorized, and consistent with applicable confidentiality expectations.

Partner status alone does not create a right to access firm information, client information, sensitive information, working drafts, internal notes, or evidence.

The Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy controls confidentiality expectations, need-to-know access, external disclosure, information handling, and confidentiality escalation.

## 10. Commercial and Contracting Boundaries

Third-party and partner relationships shall not create commercial commitments, pricing commitments, referral obligations, subcontractor obligations, vendor obligations, client-facing promises, or payment expectations unless reviewed and approved through the appropriate commercial and contracting governance process.

Partner discussions, referral conversations, informal planning notes, capability statements, or draft materials shall not be treated as binding commitments unless approved through the appropriate business or contracting process.

The Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy controls proposals, pricing, contracting, obligation review, signature authority, commercial exceptions, commercial risk escalation, and commercial evidence.

## 11. Conflicts of Interest and Independence

Send Manna Too LLC shall preserve professional independence when working with third parties and partners.

Potential conflicts of interest shall be identified, reviewed, and escalated when a partner relationship, referral arrangement, compensation interest, vendor relationship, subcontractor relationship, personal relationship, or commercial opportunity could affect professional judgment, client trust, recommendations, or firm integrity.

Recommendations shall not be based solely on partner preference, referral potential, revenue opportunity, convenience, or short-term commercial benefit.

Material conflict concerns shall be documented and resolved before affected commitments are made, affected recommendations are delivered, or affected work proceeds.

## 12. Partner Conduct and Performance

Third parties and partners involved in Send Manna Too LLC activity are expected to act professionally, protect confidential information, respect scope boundaries, avoid unsupported commitments, and conduct themselves in a manner that does not harm client trust or firm reputation.

Performance or conduct concerns shall be escalated when they may affect:

- Client trust.
- Delivery quality.
- Confidentiality.
- Professional independence.
- Commercial obligations.
- Evidence quality.
- Legal or compliance expectations.
- Firm reputation.

Send Manna Too LLC may limit, suspend, or discontinue partner involvement when risk is not acceptable, accountability is unclear, conduct is inconsistent with firm expectations, or continued reliance would create unmanaged exposure.

## 13. Partner-Related Evidence

Material third-party and partner decisions shall be supported by evidence sufficient to show what was reviewed, approved, relied upon, escalated, changed, or closed.

Partner-related evidence may include, as applicable:

- Relationship purpose or business rationale.
- Qualification or fit review notes.
- Role and scope expectations.
- Approval or authorization records.
- Client-facing involvement decisions.
- Commercial or contracting review records.
- Confidentiality or information sharing decisions.
- Conflict review notes.
- Performance or conduct concern records.
- Risk escalation records.
- Exception records.

This policy defines evidence expectations but does not create records retention schedules. Future approved records governance may define retention, storage, and disposition requirements.

## 14. Risk Escalation

Third-party and partner risk shall be escalated when uncertainty or concern may affect client trust, delivery quality, confidentiality, professional independence, commercial obligations, legal obligations, evidence quality, operational continuity, or firm reputation.

Escalation triggers may include:

- Unclear partner role or authority.
- Unclear client-facing involvement.
- Potential conflict of interest.
- Proposed disclosure of sensitive or confidential information.
- Unclear subcontractor, vendor, or referral obligations.
- Partner conduct or performance concerns.
- Unsupported partner claims or recommendations.
- Pressure to bypass approved governance, commercial review, confidentiality expectations, or client expectations.
- Potential legal, commercial, insurance, tax, confidentiality, compliance, or reputational exposure.

Escalated risks shall be reviewed before affected partner involvement proceeds when practical.

## 15. Exceptions

Third-party and partner exceptions shall be limited, documented, risk-based, and approved before reliance when practical.

An exception may include a departure from normal qualification, authorization, role clarity, information sharing, client-facing involvement, evidence, escalation, or relationship governance expectations.

An exception record shall identify:

- The exception requested or taken.
- The reason for the exception.
- The person or role approving the exception.
- The affected partner, client, activity, or commitment.
- The risk created by the exception.
- The compensating control or follow-up action, if applicable.
- The expected duration or closure condition.

Exceptions shall not be used to bypass legal obligations, weaken confidentiality commitments, conceal conflicts, avoid commercial review, create unsupported client expectations, or avoid the Constitution.

## 16. Relationship to Client Engagement Governance

The Client Engagement Governance Policy controls client qualification, engagement authorization, scope control, client communications, deliverable governance, engagement changes, risk escalation, client acceptance, engagement closeout, and required engagement records.

This policy controls third-party and partner governance expectations that apply within client engagement activity.

When client engagement activity involves partner participation, subcontractor involvement, referral partner influence, vendor dependency, external collaboration, partner-related evidence, or partner risk escalation, this policy applies.

## 17. Relationship to Commercial and Contracting Governance

The Commercial and Contracting Governance Policy controls proposals, pricing, contracting, obligation review, signature authority, client-facing commercial commitments, commercial exceptions, commercial risk escalation, and commercial evidence.

This policy controls third-party and partner governance expectations that apply within commercial and contracting activity.

When commercial or contracting activity involves referral arrangements, subcontractor implications, vendor dependencies, partner compensation, partner obligations, client-facing partner statements, or partner-related commercial risk, this policy applies.

## 18. Relationship to Information Handling and Confidentiality Governance

The Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy controls information handling, confidentiality expectations, need-to-know access, external disclosure, client-provided evidence, working drafts, internal notes, and confidentiality-related escalation.

This policy controls third-party and partner governance expectations that affect whether a partner should be involved, what role the partner may play, and when partner-related risk should be escalated.

When partner involvement requires information sharing, external disclosure, client-provided evidence access, confidentiality review, or need-to-know assessment, the Information Handling and Confidentiality Policy applies.

The Governance and Operating Authority Policy controls decision rights, approval expectations, exception authority, controlled operating discipline, client-facing authority, and governance evidence expectations.

The Document Quality Standard controls quality, evidence, readability, review, legal and commercial caution, and publication readiness expectations.

Future records, external deliverable, cybersecurity, vendor security, data classification, procurement, publication, or technical standards may define more specific requirements within their approved scope.

## 20. Noncompliance

Failure to follow this policy may create client trust, confidentiality, commercial, legal, delivery, evidence, independence, operational, or reputational risk.

Material noncompliance shall be documented and remediated through an approved corrective action, decision record, release update, exception record, commercial review, confidentiality review, client communication, or other appropriate governance evidence.

## 21. Review and Maintenance

This policy shall be reviewed when partner practices, referral practices, subcontractor use, vendor reliance, external collaboration, service offerings, commercial practices, client obligations, legal obligations, governance expectations, or operating practices materially change.

Approved changes shall follow the controlled governance release process.
