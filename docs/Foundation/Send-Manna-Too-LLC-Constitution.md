# Send Manna Too LLC Constitution

## 1. Document Control

Version: 0.1
Status: Draft
Owner: Send Manna Too LLC
Canonical Source: Markdown in Git repository
Applies To: All Send Manna Too LLC governance documents, advisory work, client deliverables, partner relationships, and commercial decisions
Effective Date: TBD
Review Cycle: At least annually or upon material business, legal, commercial, or governance change

## 2. Purpose

This Constitution establishes the governing principles, authority, hierarchy, and quality expectations for the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

It is the highest internal governance authority for the framework. All lower-level policies, standards, frameworks, procedures, playbooks, templates, checklists, scripts, records, and publication formats must align with it.

This Constitution does not replace signed agreements, legal advice, client-specific scopes of work, or professional judgment. It governs how Send Manna Too LLC creates, maintains, and applies its own governance framework.

## 3. Firm Mission and Governance Intent

Send Manna Too LLC exists to provide trusted executive advisory and transformation services grounded in integrity, evidence, governance discipline, and long-term trust.

The framework is intended to protect firm integrity, relationships, reputation, commercial independence, and reusable intellectual property while enabling consistent, executive-quality client and partner outcomes.

The objective is not to create a large document library. The objective is to create enduring, governed intellectual property that can stand up to review by executive leadership, boards, counsel, procurement, regulators, clients, and strategic partners.

## 4. Governing Principles

The firm is governed by the following principles.

### 4.1 Integrity and Trust

- Integrity before growth.
- Relationships before transactions.
- Long-term trust before short-term revenue.
- Professional independence.
- Commercial integrity.

### 4.2 Evidence and Quality

- Evidence before opinion.
- Quality before speed.
- Plain English.
- Executive-quality writing.
- Distinguish facts, assumptions, inferences, and recommendations.
- Anticipate and address reasonable objections.

### 4.3 Governance and Maintainability

- Governance before execution.
- Simplicity before complexity.
- Consistency before customization.
- Build once; reuse often.
- One source of truth.
- No duplicate guidance.
- One primary purpose per document.
- Continuous improvement without unnecessary expansion.
- Every recommendation must materially improve the framework.

## 5. Governance Authority

This Constitution governs the Send Manna Too LLC Business Governance Framework.

If two framework documents conflict, the higher-level document controls unless a documented exception or approved change states otherwise.

Material changes to the governance architecture, Release 0.1 scope, document hierarchy, or controlling principles require a documented decision record.

No document may bypass the approved development lifecycle unless an exception is documented, justified, and approved.

## 6. Document Hierarchy

The framework uses the following document hierarchy:

1. Constitution
2. Policies
3. Standards
4. Frameworks
5. Procedures / Playbooks
6. Templates / Checklists / Scripts
7. Records

Each document must have exactly one primary purpose. Duplicate guidance is not permitted.

Detailed definitions, naming rules, placement rules, and document mechanics belong in the Document Hierarchy Standard.

## 7. One Source of Truth

Markdown in the Git repository is the canonical source for framework documents.

Git is the authoritative version history. GitHub provides remote backup, synchronization, and review support.

Word, PDF, PowerPoint, and other publication formats are controlled outputs. They are not the authoritative source unless a documented exception states otherwise.

Published outputs must be traceable to the canonical Markdown source.

## 8. Decision Rights and Records

Material governance decisions must be documented.

Architecture changes, Release 0.1 scope changes, new deliverables, hierarchy changes, and principle changes require a documented decision record.

Ideas that may be useful later but do not materially improve the current release must be recorded in the Future Considerations Register rather than inserted into current scope.

## 9. Quality Standard Commitment

Framework documents and final deliverables must be clear, accurate, purpose-specific, non-duplicative, and executive-ready.

Documents must use plain English and distinguish facts, assumptions, inferences, recommendations, constraints, and residual risks where relevant.

Documents must be capable of review by executive leadership, boards, counsel, procurement, regulators, clients, and strategic partners.

Detailed writing, formatting, review, publication, and quality-control rules belong in the Document Quality Standard.

## 10. Commercial Integrity and Independence

Send Manna Too LLC must preserve independent professional judgment.

Recommendations must not be based solely on revenue opportunity, partner preference, referral potential, convenience, or short-term commercial benefit.

Conflicts of interest, material limitations, and relevant assumptions must be disclosed or managed as appropriate to the engagement, relationship, or decision.

Commercial integrity requires clear scope boundaries, honest communication, and refusal to make unsupported promises.

## 11. Client and Partner Commitments

Send Manna Too LLC is committed to professional conduct, clear communication, confidentiality, evidence-based advice, and responsible escalation of material risks.

The firm must avoid unsupported guarantees, compliance warranties, breach-prevention promises, or commitments that exceed signed agreements or approved scope.

Client and partner relationships must be governed by trust, transparency, accountability, and respect for professional independence.

## 12. Change Control

Changes to the framework must be intentional, documented, and traceable.

Material changes require approval through the appropriate decision record, change log, or future governance mechanism.

New ideas default to the Future Considerations Register unless they materially improve the current release and are approved for inclusion.

Framework changes must preserve simplicity, maintainability, consistency, and non-duplication.

## 13. Exceptions

Exceptions must be documented, justified, and approved.

Exceptions must not compromise legality, integrity, confidentiality, client trust, professional independence, or the authority of this Constitution.

Recurring exceptions may indicate a framework defect and should be reviewed for possible correction.
